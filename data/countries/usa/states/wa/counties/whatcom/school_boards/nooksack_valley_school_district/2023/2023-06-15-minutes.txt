CALL TO ORDER
The regular meeting of the Nooksack Valley School Board of Directors was called to order by
Chairman, Steve Jones, at 7:00 p.m. on Thursday, June 15, 2023.
MASONIC EDUCATOR OF THE YEAR PRESENTATION
 Superintendent Galley, alongside Dick Decima from North Whatcom County Masons,
presented the Masonic Educator of the Year Award to Joyce Perkins. Joyce taught for 25 years
at Nooksack Valley Middle School and served as a teacher, leader, and mentor in that time. She
is retiring this year and will be greatly missed.
COMMUNICATIONS
 Superintendent Galley shared a celebration of graduation week, and Steve Jones praised the
work of the high school with a successful Honor’s Night as well.
 Ty Elmendorf shared his gratitude of being able to hand out diplomas in Mark Olson’s place.
 Halli Kimball shared her gratitude that our parent led Baccalaureate is held on campus.
 Katie DeVries asked a follow-up question related to a young adult novel in our middle school
library as well as shared a concern for a book read at the Elementary. Megan Vigre, Director of
Teaching and Learning, committed to following up with Katie at a later date.
 Lori Bruland noted the upcoming changes to the board, with Mark Olson and Cheryl Thornton
both retiring after 28 years, and shared concerns about the transition.

POLICY 2029-ANIMALS AS PART OF THE INSTRUCTIONAL PROGRAM
 Second Reading
 There was much discussion prior to a motion on Policy 2029. Halli Kimball continued to
raise concerns about potential liability and Cheryl Thornton raised questions about the extra
workload of principals to ensure all logistics are in place for each animal.
 The board would like the administration to monitor the process and the impacts to the
learning environment and make a long-term decision in June, 2024.
 Cheryl Thornton moved, and Ty Elmendorf seconded the motion to approve Policy 2029
with a 1-year trial period.
M.C. 5-0
