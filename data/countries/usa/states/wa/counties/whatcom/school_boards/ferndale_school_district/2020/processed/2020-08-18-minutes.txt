3. PUBLIC COMMENT

3.01 Receive Public Comment Regarding the Proposed 2020-2021 District Budget
Mr. McLaurin explained to audience members that the Revised Code of
Washington requires the School Board to hold a public hearing each year for the
purpose of receiving comments from the public on the following year’s budget.
He reminded audience members the 2020-2021 Budget has been posted on the
District website for over a week, and this hearing was advertised in advance,
along with three ways for people to make comments in our virtual setting.
Mr. McLaurin explained to attendees how to make public comment during the
meeting. Mr. McLaurin also noted that no comments were submitted in writing
prior to tonight’s Zoom hearing. Mr. McLaurin asked Mr. Deebach to talk about
the development of the budget since no comments have been received.
Mr. Deebach, Assistant Superintendent for Business & Support Services spoke
about the key points in the development process of the 2020-2021 budget. He
reminded the Board if the levy passes in November, we will have to do some
revisions to the budget.
Mr. Deebach reminded the board about the different types of budget documents
that are posted on BoardDocs and that they have been available on our website
for the public to review.
Mr. Deebach answered questions from the Board.
No public comments were received.
