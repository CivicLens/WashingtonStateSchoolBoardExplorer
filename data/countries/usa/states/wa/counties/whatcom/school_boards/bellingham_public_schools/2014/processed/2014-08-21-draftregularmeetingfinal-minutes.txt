OPENING ITEMS
Meeting Date and Location
August 21, 2014 – 1306 Dupont St., Bellingham, Wash.
Call to Order/Roll Call
Dr. Smith called the meeting to order at 5:15 p.m. Present: Directors Kelly Bashaw, Ken
Gass, Camille Hackler, Steve Smith, Superintendent Greg Baker, and recorder Ruth
Rasmussen. Newly appointed director Doug Benjamin was also present, to be seated
following administration of the oath of office.
Audience
No audience presented.
Welcome and Introduction of Newly Appointed Board Member
Dr. Smith welcomed Douglas Benjamin, who was appointed by the board on July 28,
2014 to fill the vacant School Board Position No. 5.
Administration and Oath of Office
Dr. Baker administered the oath of office to Mr. Benjamin.
Superintendent’s Announcements and Report
PreK-12 Visual and Performing Arts Advisory Group: Deputy Superintendent Mike
Copland presented a PowerPoint highlighting the group’s work. The group’s task was to
review the district’s PreK-12 visual and performing arts program and to recommend to
the superintendent ways to continue to sustain and improve it. Dr. Copland introduced
audience members Mark Schlichting, a community member who served on the
committee, and Todd Guiton, a parent who actively supported the committee’s work.
Also, he reviewed the committee’s recommendations, noting the focus is on a strong
commitment to equity and a shared view of the arts, not as an “add on” within the district
but as an integral part of the core program for every student.
Teacher/Principal Evaluation Project (TPEP): Dr. Baker referenced a video on the Office
of Superintendent of Public Instruction’s (OSPI) website highlighting the district’s TPEP
work.
Teacher of the Year Interview: Recently, Bill Gates interviewed Shuksan Middle School
teacher and 2014 Washington State Teacher of the Year Katie Brown. The interview is
featured on the Bill Gates blog, gatesnotes.

Back-to-School Activities: Dr. Baker summarized activities now in process.
Principals and some staff are back from summer break. Professional development
programs are underway in areas including Sheltered Instruction Observation Protocol
(SIOP), Elementary Literacy, and Highly Capable Learning programs. Administrators are
also focusing on enrollment and staffing. Additionally, 900 new computers will be in
place when students return to school.
Board Technology: Kurt Gazow, Executive Director of Educational Technology, and
Tanya Rowe, Executive Director of Communications and Community Relations,
highlighted changes in board use of technology. Each board member will be assigned a
new email address to be used for district-related communications. The Listening Post will
continue to be used as the primary communications method for families and community
members to send an electronic message to the entire board.
