B. REPORTS TO THE BOARD This section can be watched at 19:35 time stamp.
1. Address Position and RIF Reductions – Frances Guerrero, YEA President
Ms. Guerrero, YEA President, addressed the board on budget reductions, highlighting a
colleague's master's project, outlined cuts to the high school bands, and emphasized that offers
made outside of endorsements were not valid. She reviewed enrollment figures, hiring practices
amid the budget crisis and summer school budget cuts. Director Rice requested the written notes
that YEA President Guerrero read from. She agreed to send them.
2. 1000.1.0 ENDS Report Goal #1 - Hector Mendez, Executive Director of Leadership &
Accountability and Lucas Jaeger, Principal, Discovery Early Learning Center
Principal Jaeger of Discovery Early Learning Center presented the attached ENDS Goal #1
report. Director Rice inquired about alignment between preschool instruction and YSD
standards. Principal Jaeger explained school operations vary but assured collaboration in student
education. VP Villanueva acknowledged current trends and asked what Principal Jaeger's plans
are for the next two years to enhance standards. Principal Jaeger shared curriculum adoption,
staff training, PLT process investment, and planned PBIS and SEL training, emphasizing steady
progress. Director Navarro, Jr. raised concerns about discipline statistics. Principal Jaeger

highlighted minimal issues at DELC and efforts to enhance Social and Emotional Learning.
Director Beckett questioned the capacity of the newly constructed building. Principal Jaeger
estimated full capacity within about a year.
3. Annual Migrant Report to the Board - Hector Mendez, Executive Director of Leadership &
Accountability
Principal Lucas Jaeger presented the attached Migrant Presentation in the absence of Mr.
Mendez. He shared demographics as of six weeks ago that show 9.2% of all YSD students are
migrant students district-wide. They focused on Goal #2 and held a Middle School Wellness
Event with ESD 105. Goal #3's focus was addressed with a vision screening held at the school.
C. UPDATES This section can be watched at 48:10 time stamp.
1. Members of the Board
Director Rice informed the board that the Superintendent's Evaluation is nearing completion. She
requested feedback from colleagues by early next week before it returns to the board for
approval.
Director Navarro Jr. addressed his recent absence due to a life-threatening illness. He apologized
for missing meetings and cancellations, expressing gratitude to Robertson staff for their support
during this challenging time.
VP Villanueva commended staff for their efforts at all five graduation events earlier this
month.President Walker mentioned his recent family illness, which required his absence and
prevented his attendance at the graduations.
2. Superintendent's Update
Supt. Greene provided updates on district activities: WAMS experienced flooding on Friday
night. Five YSD schools were recognized in the Washington Schools Recognition Program by
OSPI. He announced a change in the presentation date for the Special Education department. The
July 9th meeting was rescheduled to July 30th, with a budget board study session scheduled for
July 9th. La Academia started today, featuring four days of training for our dual language
teachers. Additionally, he noted that the RCW requiring any governing entity with a contract
with YSD will be added to the consent agenda. He expressed his enthusiasm for last week's
graduations. He concluded by discussing his collaboration with multiple superintendents from
across Washington State, gathering at UW under the leadership of David Knight, Dean of
Education and Dr. Craig Anthony, Director of Leadership for Learning. The group, consisting of
approximately 15 to 20 superintendents, aims to unify district requests to legislation and, in the
future, establish a clearer vision for an equitable funding model for public schools in Washington
State.
3. Calendar Updates and Future Agenda Items
Kirsten Fitterer shared important school events and district events which are listed on
BoardDocs.
4. Monthly Financial Update - Jake Kuper
Mr. Kuper, Assistant Supt of Finance, shared the attached presentation on YSD's monthly
financial report.
