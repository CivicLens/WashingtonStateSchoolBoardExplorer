SUPERINTENDENT REPORT
Superintendent Galley presented on the following topics:
 Budget/Staffing: Supt. Galley explained the budget landscape and how declining
enrollments, staffing levels, and the sunsetting of ESSER money are factors that have
impacted budgets. He then explained that the district has identified a reduction of 5.0 FTE,
mainly through attrition, that will reduce the budget by approximately 2%, and will await a
final budget from the legislature before taking next steps.
 Levy information: Supt. Galley then explained how levy limits are decided upon, how NV
compares to neighboring districts, and what levy money is used for. He announced that
Cory Plager from DA Davidson will attend next month to explain the process in more
depth.
 Board Vacancy and Elections: Supt. Galley explained the process the board will follow to
appoint a new member in light of Jason Heutink’s resignation. He also explained the
upcoming election process.
 The school board reviewed the ICOS Asset Preservation Report as submitted by Dave
Gebhart and accepted and approved the results.
 Superintendent Galley gave a brief explanation on the following:
o The board will review a possible therapy animal policy and decide whether to pursue
a policy or not in May.
o CEE Survey: District will conduct the Educational Effectiveness Survey in May and
will provide access for parents to review the week of April 24-28.
o Elementary principals will present school improvement work in May.
o Tom Harmon will present school safety work in June.
o The district and both the NVEA and SEIU will begin contract negotiations in May.
