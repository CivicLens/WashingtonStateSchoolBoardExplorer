MERIDIAN SCHOOL DISTRICT #505
REGULAR SCHOOL BOARD MEETING
MEETING HELD IN PERSON & VIA YOUTUBE LIVE STREAM
November 6, 2024

REGULAR MEETING
The regular meeting was called to order by Board President Steve Corwin at 6:01 p.m. Steve welcomed
everyone that was in attendance.
Steve Corwin, Jarrod Karuza, Marty Gray, Jana Haugen, and Michelle Simmons were present at the
meeting.
Student Representatives Kennedy Brzozowski and Logan Ignacio were present at the meeting.
The pledge of allegiance was recited.
A motion to adopt the agenda was made by Jarrod Karuza and seconded by Jana Haugen. The motion
passed 5-0.
CONSENT AGENDA
A. Approval of Meeting Minutes
B. Accounts Payable
A motion to approve the consent agenda was made by Marty Gray and seconded by Jana Haugen. The
motion passed 5-0.
RECOGNITION
MHS AESD Accreditation Recognition
Northwest Educational Service District 189 Superintendent, Dr. Vivanco along with NWESD District 1
Board Director Kelly Bashaw, publicly recognized the leadership at Meridian High School for earning
their 6-year AESD Accreditation status.
PUBLIC COMMENT
Community member, Jerry Smoot, spoke to request a 6”x5” “In memory of Angela Roderick” plaque be
placed on the softball batting cage.
PRESENTATIONS
Special Education Program Report
The Special Education Program Report presentation was postponed to November 20, 2024.
Intervention Grants Report - Title I, LAP, Title III, and TBIP
Director of Teaching and Learning, Adrienne Somera, and Assistant Superintendent, Kurt Harvill,
presented a Intervention Support Grant Report. The report shared information about the Student
Support programs that Meridian School District utilizes and highlighted how we utilize funding amounts
from Title I, LAP, Title III, and TBIP, Title 1 Part C to support students.

NEW BUSINESS
First Review: Nooksack Paraprofessional Support Interlocal Agreement
Dr. Everett presented a Nooksack paraprofessional interlocal agreement for first review. After discussion
the board chose to move forward with action. Students favor approval. A motion to approve the
Nooksack paraprofessional interlocal agreement as presented was made by Jarrod Karuza and seconded
by Michelle Simmons. The motion passed 5-0.
First Review: Policy & Procedure 3205 – Sex Discrimination and Sex-Based Harassment
Assistant Superintendent, Kurt Harvill presented a first reading of a Policy & Procedure 3205 – Sex
Discrimination and Sex-Based Harassment. This will be brought back for second review and approval on
November 20, 2024.
First Review: Policy & Procedure 3206 – Pregnant and Parenting Students
Assistant Superintendent, Kurt Harvill presented a first reading of a Policy & Procedure 3206 – Pregnant
and Parenting Students. This will be brought back for second review and approval on November 20,
2024.
First Review: Policy & Procedure 5011 – Sex Discrimination and Sex-Based Harassment of District Staff
Prohibited
Assistant Superintendent, Kurt Harvill presented a first reading of a Policy & Procedure 5011 – Sex
Discrimination and Sex-Based Harassment of District Staff Prohibited. This will be brought back for

Assistant Superintendent, Kurt Harvill presented a first reading of Policy & Procedure 5012 – Parent
Family Marital Status and Pregnancy. This will be brought back for second review and approval on
November 20, 2024.
FINANCE REPORT
Finance Report
Director of Finance, Daniel Yorton gave a financial update to the Board. Daniel reported to the Board on
October enrollment compared to September and October enrollment compared to budget. Daniel
shared information about process development, staff support, and work that is being done to evaluate
the emergent needs we have across the district.
SUPERINTENDENT’S REPORT
Dr. Everett shared details with the board about the classrooms he has been visiting, and the multilingual family night at IRE. Dr. Everett spoke about the Ai Summit, the WA State Funding UW Convening,
WSRMP Board meeting, and the P3 National Conference he recently attended. Dr. Everett shared that
our newly added transitional kindergarten classroom is open and serving kids, in addition to
disappointment about the bond failure. Upcoming dates of importance were also shared.
INFORMATION FROM STUDENT BOARD REPRESENTATIVES
Student representatives shared that they will be going over to MMS to promote student advisory and
that a 5th grade advisory team has already been formed at IRE. More work is being developed about how
to pull the groups all together to meet. Had first student advisory meeting at MHS today.
INFORMATION FROM BOARD DIRECTORS
Volleyball has one more shot at state. Senior parents have donated tickets for any student that wants to
go to the next game.
