1. Call to Order, Welcome, Pledge of Allegiance and Roll Call
Meeting called to order at 6:31 P.M. Board members in attendance were Steve Jilk, CJ Costanti and David Vis.
Board members Brian Johnson and Kelli Kettels were absent and excused. Student Directors Ruby VanderHaak and
Cynthia Torres were present.
2. Good News
•

•
•

Governor Jay Inslee has proclaimed January as School Board Recognition Month. Our volunteer school
board members play a crucial role in developing policies and making tough decision on complex
educational and social issues. Our board serves as a vial link between community and our schools and we
thank them for their willingness to serve and we honor your dedication and hard work.
Former Lynden High School teacher – turned Seattle Firefighter – Randy Anderson, spent this week
training 230 high school students in the life saving skills of CPR and AED use.
The Cheer Team has qualified for the State Cheer Competition on February 2 nd in Battle Ground, WA.

None at this meeting.

6. Reports
Superintendent
• Social Emotional Learning Update
Tim Metz, Director of Special Education updated the Board on the tools and protocols that have been
implemented to help support students with social/emotional/behavioral challenges and the tiered support
provided to students, families, and staff. Tim explained the partnership with community entities to provide
extra support and interaction with students. Tim updated the Board on the Youth Mental Health First Aid
training for staff to assist in responding to student mental health needs.
•

Construction Update
Jim Frey provided a summary of the Fisher Elementary and Lynden Middle School construction and
completion status and additional projects that are expected to take place and others that are under
consideration.

Teaching and Learning
• Teaching and Learning Update
None at this meeting.
Finance and Operations
• Budget Update - November
The Board reviewed information on the budget for November containing a summary of financial status,
enrollment, general fund comparative, and revenues and expenditures. Patti explained the difference in
the levy collection based on the new way Legislature directs the funds.
•

Budget Update 2019 - 2020
Patti Fairbanks shared the preliminary calendar for the 2019-2020 budget preparation process. Patty
provided a summary of enrollment over the past 5 years and the impacts on the budget when enrollment
comes in higher or lower than anticipated.

7. Board Workshop
• District Planning Committee
Jim Frey, Steve Jilk, and CJ Costanti shared the discussions that took place at the January 8th District
Planning Committee meeting. Topics of discussion at the meeting were Future Planning; School Safety and
Security; Bond and Levy Rates; Balanced Programs; and Impact Fees.
•

Capital Facilities Plan (CFP) Update
Jim Frey informed the Board about a draft CFP that will provide data on school facilities, enrollment, and
projected growth over a six-year period. After review of the CFP, Jim will provide more details about impact
fees.

•

Middle School Repurposing
Jim Frey provided schematics designs and associated costs for repurposing the old middle school. Jim
shared that a plan has been incorporated to create stages of remodeling by prioritizing based on student
needs and the highest priority is preschool classrooms and associated essentials such as a bus pull through,
storage, sprinkler system and HVAC system.

8. Action Items
• Resolution No. 01-19 – Apportionment Redirection
The Board reviewed the resolution which will allow funds to be transferred from the General Fund to
the Capital Projects Fund for installing new surveillance and access control systems at the high school.
David Vis motioned to approve Resolution No. 01-19 seconded by CJ Costanti. Motion carried by voice vote.
