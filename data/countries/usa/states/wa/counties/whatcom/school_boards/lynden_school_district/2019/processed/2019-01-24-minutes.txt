8. Board Work Session
• Glenning Street Property
Harlan Kredit, community member and spokesman of the “Save Glenning Street” property, provided an
update on the progress of community efforts to raise funds for the sale of the Lynden School District
Glenning Street property. The Board will review further information to be provided by Mr. Kredit with
further discussion at the February 14th School Board meeting.

•

Career Connected Learning
Kent Victor, CTE Director and Lois Mehlhoff, College and Career Coordinator shared the progress being
made to implement career connected activities that connect school to work. Kent and Lois gave examples
of how elementary grades explore career interests through curriculum and projects related to careers.
Secondary students participate in career fairs, job shadowing, structured field trips, participating in college
campus visits, and coordination of business “Road Shows” to visit the high school classrooms, along with
activity club exposure to different careers. Kent shared the outlook for aligning elementary and secondary
curriculum to provide support for career interests.

•

Distribution Policy and Practices
The Board reviewed the practice of informational material distributed to students and families. Further
review of policy and procedure will be addressed at a future meeting to align district distribution of
material.

•

Mid-Year Reflection and Update
Jim Frey provided a narrative of district priorities and a summary of progress towards achieving this
important work. The Board reflected on the broad categories and finer details of: College and Career Ready
Graduates; Family and Community Partnerships; Safe and Supportive Learning Environments; Responsible
Citizenship.
