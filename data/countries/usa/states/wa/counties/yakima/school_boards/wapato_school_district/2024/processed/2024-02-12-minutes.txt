3. Reports
Superintendent's Report: Superintendent Garza was not present and Tony Howard, Executive Director of Human Resources reported on his behalf. Mr. Howard reported:
Bond information has concluded. Mr. Garza and other administrators contributed to over 30 presentations, flyers, videos, media interviews, and two community demonstrations at International Market with the goal of getting information to patrons about the upcoming Bond.  Tomorrow is vote day and Mr. Garza will be present at the courthouse awaiting the preliminary election results. The Bond must receive 60% yes votes to pass.
The Board unanimously approved OAC, Services as the Project Management Consultant at the last board meeting. The District is working with and pending a complete contract proposal from OAC. Mr. Howard informed the contract will be reviewed by legal counsel prior to moving forward.
Mid-year Benchmark assessments such as Galileo and DIBELS have concluded. WIDA testing for K-5th grade is also concluded and WIDA testing for secondary grades will begin next week.
WHS Sports update: State Wrestling Competition will take place February 16-17 at the Tacoma Dome. This year will be the largest number of qualifiers competing at State for Wapato, with 10 Boys, 2 Girls and 3 alternates will be competing this weekend.  Basketball is currently competing in Districts. The Boys will play on tomorrow at home against Wahluke HS, a loser out game. The winner will continue on to loser out games on Thursday and Saturday. The Girls play in District Title game against Toppenish HS on Thursday.
2024-2025 Enrollment Projection: Sylvia Bazan, Director of Fiscal Services presented the 2024-2025 Enrollment Projection. Mrs. Bazan reported this year’s projected enrollment is more conservative than in years past. She is taking into consideration and adjusting due to current year low kindergarten enrollment, and smaller classed size in 8th and 11th grades. She compared the previous year enrollment to the current enrollment and found this year’s K-12th grade enrollment has decreased. She reported the trend the past few years is that enrollment has decreased and the enrollment projection recommendation reflects that trend. This projection is presented for action with a recommendation of 2,959 total enrollment for the 2024-25 school year.
Mrs. Bazan informed, once approved by the Board, the next step is to review staffing needs and will take into consideration, the District did not meet its projected enrollment funding this year, 2023-24. The focus in hiring is in Special Education and CTE and Mr. Bazan will work closely with Human Resources on the staffing process and review of positions. The Board engaged in discussion regarding the presented information.
