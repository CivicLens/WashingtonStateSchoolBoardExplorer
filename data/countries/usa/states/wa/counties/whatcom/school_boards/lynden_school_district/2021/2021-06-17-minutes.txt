2. Good News
•

The Ferndale/Lynden Masonic Lodge is here to present Bernice Vossbeck Teacher, Anne Marie Peach
with Educator of the Year Award. Anne Marie will receive $1,000 to use in her classroom and her name will
be added to the Educator of the Year plaque. Congratulations!
Lynden High School student, Mia Ward, was accepted to the 2020-2021 All-Northwest Choir, based on her
video audition.
The San Juan Music Educators Honors Festival was held virtually and included LHS students,
Zeke Falcone, Nehemiah Jones, Gabriella Rodriguez, Sydney Thorgeirson and Mia Ward. Mia's Soprano
Vocal Solo was given a Superior Rating. Congratulations to you all!
Lynden High School student, Sydney Thorgeirson was awarded the Troubadour Award! This is choir's top
honor and is given to a choir member to whom choir and singing are a top priority in their secondary
education. The recipient of this award shows how a person can be enriched through the dedicated pursuit
of music. Congratulations!

Teaching and Learning
• Instructional Materials Committee Recommendations
Elizabeth Hamming, Director of Teaching and Learning, provided the Board with the list of
recommended curriculum for the 2021-2022 school year which includes core instructional
materials requests from staff; health curriculum for grades 6-12; and library book lists.
Jim Frey shared his appreciation of Elizabeth’s dedication to the Lynden School District for the
years that she has served students and staff and wished her well in her future.

6. Board Workshop
• School Improvement Plan (SIP) Reports - Elementary
Elementary principals, Tim Doering, Courtney Ross, and Michelle Nilsen provided their plans to
provide support to students by reducing the achievement gap by using data; assessing health
and safety standards, and what their needs are from the district to help accomplish their goals.
They spoke of the elementary 5th graders visiting the middle school and meeting with teachers
to transition in the next school year, and the welcoming of kindergarten students and families,
and using the Character Strong social emotional learning for all elementary students.
The Board thanked and appreciated the elementary principals on the execution of how they
managed the 2020-2021 school year with caring for both the children and staff.
Jim Frey thanked Courtney for her service as Fisher Elementary School and the Board wished her
well in her future endeavors.
Brian Johnson motioned to approve the SIP Report seconded by CJ Costanti. Motion carried by voice vote.
•

Alternative Learning Education (ALE) Report
Jim Frey explained what needs to be board approved for the ALE report that includes ALE
student headcount and equivalent enrollment claimed for basic education funding;
identification of the overall ration of certificated instructional staff; a list of ALE program course
providers and a description of how the program supports the district’s overall goals for
academic achievement; and results of any self-evaluations.
