Regular Agenda, Action, Resolution, Motion: 1.
Community Development Director RJ Lott explained the West End Villas Planned Residential
Development is a proposal to divide approximately 5.26 acres into 65 lots. The PRD includes
the construction of 60 townhouses, four lots for open space, and one lot for a private road. The
site for the project is north of Pullman High School, located south of NW High Point Court, and
east of NW Valley View Drive on Military Hill.
The current zoning designation at this location is R-2 Low-Density Multi-Family Residential. The
R-2 zoning district allows for a maximum density of 15 units per acre.
A planned residential development allows for flexibility and creativity in the layout and design of
new residential developments.
The Planned Residential Development went through the site plan committee on May 30, 2023,
and had 45 recommended conditions outlined in the staff report.
The Planning Commission held a public hearing on this item on June 28, 2023, and voted
unanimously in favor of a recommendation of approval to the City Council. At the public hearing
on June 28, 2023, numerous individuals spoke against the proposal. They cited traffic, parking,
and neighborhood character as their chief concerns.
This proposal appeared before the City Council on July 11, 2023. The City Council remanded
the item back to staff with the direction that the applicant prepare and submit a traffic impact
analysis regarding potential impacts associated with this project proposal.
The traffic impact analysis was received on December 15, 2023. A review of the analysis by
staff from the Public Works Department concluded that there is no significant impact on traffic.

City Administrator Mike Urban stated Resolution No. R-12-24 will authorize a professional
services agreement with Welch Comer for the Downtown Pullman Improvement Project phase
three. The contract with Apollo was awarded on February 6, 2024. Phase three is part of the
original RFP. The City Council selected Welch Comer from four proposals in October 2021.
Phase zero was the original surveying piece. Having Welch Comer do construction management
on this project is vital to ensure the contractor maintains timelines. The City Staff's ability to
perform this work and keep other projects moving is not feasible; we need professional help.
Welch Comer Engineer Matt Gillis explained the generalities of the construction phase services
agreement and how they will help through the construction process. The City has contractual
ties with Apollo, Inc. as the contractor, and the City will have a separate contract with Welch
Comer as the project engineer. There is no contractual tie between the contractor and the
engineer. Welch Comer has an unbiased, unconnected relationship with the contractor because
they represent the City of Pullman.
Welch Comer's priority is the public's health, welfare, and safety. Welch Comer is responsible
for informing the public about what is happening with the project and is also tasked with enforcing
the contract between Apollo and the City of Pullman. Enforcing that document, Welch Comer is
ensuring that the construction of Main Street is done according to the contract documents, the
plans, the specifications, and all the associated requirements.
Matt explained he will serve the City as the overall project manager from the engineering side,
and his team has a specific level of expertise that's very important to the project.
Welch Comer has technical staff and engineers, as technical things will come up during this
project that must be addressed immediately. As important as the technical and administrative
services are with the project, Welch Comer must keep very close tabs on pay requests, change
orders, and funding, which is the project administrator's job. One of the most critical jobs on site
is the Resident Project Representative, who will be on-site every day the contractors are on site,
watching every move they make, documenting everything they do, and ensuring that it's being
done per the contract.
The Welch Comer Focused Public Outreach Coordinator will handle news releases, website
updates, weekly newsletters, and outreach to specific business people. The Public Outreach
Coordinator will interact daily with the on-site representative so that they know what the other is
doing because they both must know the project's status.
The Project's Broad Public Outreach Coordinator, Recraft, is an independent consultant who
does not promote the City of Pullman, the Project Downtown Pullman, or Welch Comer. The
Project's Broad Public Outreach Coordinator and his staff will work with the Focused Public
Outreach Coordinator to inform the public via photography, social media, and videography of
the project site and social media content production throughout the project.
Matt explained Welch Comer would host a neighborhood open house meeting in the next couple
of weeks where his team and the contractor could answer the public's questions. Welch Comer
will also host a workshop for Pullman businesses to talk with the design team and ask questions
about the project's design.
