  New Business
A. Student Advisory Council (SAC) Annual Report
Kerri Helgeson, Director of Equity and Student Well-Being, introduced the Student Advisory Council Report and shared that mental health awareness was selected by the students as the topic of interest that they wanted to focus on for this year. Group 1's students, Kathleen Azpiri, Adrienne Pounadeali, Arissa Matthews and Kiera Wilson shared that their group's focus was to provide mental health workshops for 5th grade students. Group 2's students, Alex Phillips, Sera Sabol and Rachael Tanner, reported that their group's purpose was to gather health resources for students and put them into easy-to-read posters and have the information readily accessible on ASB cards, resource stations and on websites. Group 3's students, Raven Kaloudis, Elena Twitchell and Nathan Nichols explained about their Tea Token idea as a mental health resource for students and teachers. Tea Tokens, when given to a teacher by a student, would let a teacher know in a private way that a student would like to schedule a time to meet to talk with them about a concern or issue. Kerri Helgeson talked about highlights of activities they participated in at this year's meetings. The students answered questions from the Board. At the end of the report, Kerri recognized and introduced all the graduating seniors. She asked each one to share about their future plans and presented them with a certificate and a treat.

B. Financial Highlights of Current Budget
Gina Zeutenhorst, Executive Director of Financial Services, provided the Board with highlights from the current budget as of April 2024 budget reports. Gina answered questions from the Board.
  C. Resolution 24-07 Interfund Loan
Gina Zeutenhorst, Executive Director of Financial Services, shared that the District has been carefully tracking cash flow. Projections indicate a cash shortfall is anticipated for the month of June. The District is requesting an interfund loan so that current obligations for June and potentially part of July can be met. Cumulative amounts will not exceed $2,800,000. The loan balance should be paid in full by the end of August 2024. Monthly financial reports will specify actual outstanding interfund loans and actual interest charges involved.
  Motion: That the Board approves Resolution 24-07 Interfund Loan.
  Motion by Matt Dimond, second by Erica Knapp.
Final Resolution: Motion Carries
Yea: Matt Dimond, Sheri Kelly, Mary Levesque, Erica Knapp
