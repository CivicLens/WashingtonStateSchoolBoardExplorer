Call to Order

A regular meeting of the Board of Directors was held on Monday, February 12, 2024, in
the Administrative Service Center Board Room and was available for live streaming.
President Lynn Goebel called the meeting to order at 6:02 p.m. and led the flag salute.

Roll Call

Board members Lynn Goebel, Jessica Aws, Nicole Tesch, Sharon Jensen, and Josh
MacLean were present. Student board representatives Josiah Villano and Maggie
Litvachuk were present.

Adoption of Agenda

The agenda was adopted by unanimous consent.

Consent Agenda

Vice President Aws moved to approve the consent agenda. Director Jensen seconded
the motion. The motion passed unanimously.

Recognition of
Kindergarten &
Transition to
Kindergarten Team

The district’s kindergarten teachers, paraeducators, and TTK teachers were recognized
for their dedication and teamwork on behalf of students.

Student
Representative
Report

Student Representative Josiah Villano provided an update on the many recent and
upcoming athletic, music, and drama events in the district, highlighting that several
students qualified for State Solo & Ensemble Festival, and the Oak Harbor High School
girls wrestling team won regionals. Student Representative Maggie Litvachuk spoke
about robotics and STEM activities. She shared about her experience in advisory and
planning for post-graduation.

Superintendent
Report

Superintendent Michelle Kuss-Cybula presented an update on the strategic planning
process, the Small Teams Budget Advisory Committee work, enrollment projections for
2024-2025, and the school construction projects.

Superintendent MidYear Evaluation

President Goebel read a statement explaining the superintendent goal-setting and
evaluation process. The board is pleased with Dr. Kuss-Cybula’s performance and is
confident that she is leading the district in the right direction. High points are that
students are making progress, the construction of two new schools has begun, and soon
the district will have a strategic plan.

Elementary Schools
Annual Report

Elementary School Principals Jenny Hunt, Bill Weinsheimer, Paula Seaman, Christina
Merritt, and Sara Lucero presented the work being done in all five elementary schools,
highlighting reading progress & goals, math progress & goals, behavior data, attendance
tracking, student voice, and celebrations.

Board Policy
Updates First
Reading

Assistant Superintendent Dwight Lundstrom presented a first reading of updates to
board policies recently recommended by the Washington State School Directors
Association (WSSDA).

Legislative Update

Director Tesch explained that there are conversations in the legislature about MSOCs,
(Materials, Supplies, and Operating Costs). The state’s allocation of funds for MSOCs
doesn’t reflect increases in costs. There are two bills related to education that have
been introduced. Lawmakers have expressed their desire to hear directly from students.

Board Comments

Vice President Aws reported that she will be taking a group of students to Olympia to
advocate about a bill related to fentanyl use. Director Jensen attended several district
athletic events in the last week and will be in elementary schools this week. Director
MacLean thanked Student Representative Maggie Litvachuk for bringing up postgraduation options and congratulated Crescent Harbor Elementary staff for a successful
family math night. Director Tesch spoke about STEM day, Read Across America, girls
basketball, the hi-cap process, parent teacher conferences, and thanked Mr. Lundstrom
for the work he does at OHHS athletic events. President Goebel enjoyed cheering on
the girls basketball team, is looking forward to visiting the CTE Culinary Arts program,
and thanked the elementary principals for their excellent work.

Superintendent
Comments

Dr. Kuss-Cybula praised the elementary principals for their great work and collaboration
to support staff and students.
