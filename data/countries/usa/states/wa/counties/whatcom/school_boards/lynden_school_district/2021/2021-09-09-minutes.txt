5. Information from the Audience
Topics of discussion from the audience included: Need for more mask breaks; Want medical and
religious exemptions accepted for all; Concerns of teaching Critical Race Theory (CRT); Overreach of
teacher ideology asking students to use pronouns to identify themselves; Ask that the Board of
Directors resign if they follow Governor Inslee’s mandates; Board must fight against mandates and
against CRT; Teachers should not be promoting vaccinations; Thanks to the Board for upholding state
law and the mandates; Mission for schools is to educate; Reminder of literacy struggles for children;
No locker use at LMS is unacceptable due to COVID; Lunch time at LMS is too short; Need of common
sense when or when not to wear masks; Students treated like prisoners during mask breaks; Unfair to
ask volunteers to vaccinate; Board followed constitution swearing in student director, not following
constitution for others rights; Blatant disrespect from school board to respond to those who have
written them; People will stand against the school board if they do not stand with their cause, vote
school board out if they do not stand with them; Parent choices are being taken away; Need to show
examples how to stand up for kids; Mask guidance from DOH is ineffective in wildfire smoke
situations and COVID situations; People need to have a choice, board needs to make changes; Need
to hear from lawyers that we can’t go against Governor Inslee on parent choices; School board needs
to answer to themselves on their choices; Standards of education are low; Masks are hard for
children to learn; Issues with wearing masks on bus; Concern of BTO mentors not being able to
volunteer; Student with special needs not given exemption on mask mandate.
The Board agreed to move the topic – Governors Order and Washington DOH Direction up in the
agenda.
6. Governors Order and Washington DOH Direction
Steve Jilk read a statement comprised by the Board of Directors addressing the different views from
the community on the mandates placed upon schools. The Board thanked the community for their
input and for the hard work from the students, teachers, and parents to manage the requirements.
The Board believes it is their responsibility to provide a healthy and safe learning environment for
all students and to ensure that they are being responsible stewards of the resources provided to
operate a financially healthy school system.
After the statement was read Director Kettels requested to draft a letter with Interim
Superintendent, Mike Stromme, to Governor Inslee on behalf of the Board of Directors.
David Vis motioned to accept the statement seconded by CJ Costanti. Motion carried 3 to 1 voice vote.
