  AGENDA
A.  LEARNING AND SCHOOL SUPPORT SERVICES UPDATE
Heather Bybee and Shawn Jordan provided an update on learning and school support services including registration and choice applications, second semester start and transition activities, maintenance & grounds, transportation, Bond & Levy, and Activities and Athletics.   B.  PRIORITY STRATEGY #12 UPDATE (TECHNOLOGY, SOCIAL MEDIA & MEDIA LITERACY)
Heather Bybee and Stephanie Kerwien provided an update on technology, social media, and media literacy, including the strategies utilized to evaluate and enhance learning experiences. Additionally they are reviewing supports for Generative AI issues.
  BOARD REPORTS
Director Wiser shared about his visit to Lincoln Heights where he observed a classroom with four SVA teachers where they are able to teach but also collaborate together easier. He also attended the Highly Capable Workgroup where they continued discussing how to provide more students with services and plan to have specific recommendations for improvement in the coming weeks.
  Director Kozel shared about attending her first Human Growth and Development Citizens Advisory Committee. A highlight was an advocate for Disability Action Services and how differently-abled students can participate whether it be in person or remote. She also shared about visiting Mullan Road Elementary where conversations concerned the multi-family units on 55th Avenue and the number of students who cannot be bussed to the school. She also enjoyed attending Coffee with SPS at Glover Middle School and Ice Cream with SPS at Sacajawea Middle School.
  President Lockwood attended the legislative session in Olympia, and the Day on the Hill where students shared firsthand their concerns for transportation and appreciation for mental health support that the legislature funded a few years ago.
