6. Information from the Audience
A community member addressed the board regarding the bond. He expressed that he believes
that the bond failed largely because of the board’s lack of involvement. He further expressed
that because the bond failed, the safety and security of campuses will need to be prioritized. He
specifically addressed the need for security officers on all campuses and asked the board to find
money in the budget to fund those positions.

9. Action Items
Letter to Legislators- Transgender Athlete Participation
Director Martinez motioned to approve the drafted letter to local legislators as part of a
collective effort to see change in girls’ sports- specifically with regards to the inclusion of
transgender athletes that are assigned male at birth. Seconded by Director Owsley.
Director Owsley made a motion to strike the third bullet point titled ‘Education and Awareness’.
Seconded by Director Brar. Motion carried by voice vote.
Advisory Vote: 0-2; Board Vote: 3-2. Directors Hickman and Martinez opposed.
Director Martinez motioned to approve the letter to legislators as amended. Seconded by
Directors Brar. Motion carried by voice vote.
Advisory- 2-0; Board Vote: 5-0
Letter to WIAA- Transgender Athlete Participation Policy
Director Owsley motioned to approve the drafted letter in response to the board’s interest in
communicating with the WIAA regarding the need for change to their current participation
policy for transgender athletes. Seconded by Director Martinez. Motion carried by voice vote.
Advisory Vote: 2-0; Board Vote: 5-0
10. Board Committee Reports

11. Information from Board Members
Director Brar shared information she received from Superintendent VanderYacht regarding
pursuing the banning of cell phones in schools. Director Verburg added that if we decide to
discuss this as a board meeting agenda item, it would be good to receive input from student
board representatives.

Director Brar requested further discussion on Initiative 2081.
Director Brar asked when the board should expect to receive information on General Assembly.
Director Martinez asked about eResources- specifically, what that contract includes and how it
came to be in existence.

able to provide input on how ASB Funds are used.
Director Verburg thanked Superintendent VanderYacht for sending AP/Payroll reports to finance
committee each month.

earlier in the meeting.

becoming defamatory during the meetings so that she can step in as chair.
Director Martinez asked for clarification on what “budget capacity” means in the finance report.

Director Owsley asked if the board had an interest in having a guest speaker regarding
transgender topic.
