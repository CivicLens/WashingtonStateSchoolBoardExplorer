WASHINGTON STATE DEPARTMENT OF

UNDERREPRESENTED POPULATIONS IN PULLMANS
HISTORY.

Meeting :

Mar 26, 2024 - Regular Council Meeting

Category :

L. Consent Agenda items

Type :

Action (Consent), Resolution, Consent Agenda

Fiscal Impact :

No

Budgeted :

Recommended Action :

Staff recommends approval of the grant application to the
Washington State Department of Archaeology and Historic
Preservation for the purpose of surveying African American
populations in Pullman's history.

Public Content
Certified Local Governments (CLG) annual grant application deadline is April 28,
2024. This annual grant allows for a comprehensive inventory of the historic
resources of Pullman. Earlier this year, the City of Pullman's Historic Preservation
Commission decided that this grant would be used to survey the history of African
American and Black populations in Pullman's history. Last year's grant application for
the same subject matter was not awarded to Pullman by the Department of
Archaeological and Historic Preservation (DAHP).
The grant application is requesting $21,002.50 in funding with a soft match of $6,100.
The match can be used by way of staff wages, supplies, and mailings. The draft grant
application, resolution, and staff memo are attached.
Attached is the final report for grant received for Fiscal Year 2023, the history of
Chinese individuals in Pullman.

Public Content
The scope of contract 23-08, Canyon View Trail, consists of constructing 2,155 linear
feet of 12-foot-wide new or re-built gravel trail and maintenance/refinishing of 1,190
linear feet of 10- to 12-foot-wide existing gravel trail in the vicinity of Conservation
Park, traversing from NW Canyon View Drive to NW Harrison Street. See the location

concrete curb and gutter and sidewalk, installing storm sewer pipe and two catch
basins, inclusion of temporary erosion and sedimentation control measures,
landscape restoration, and other miscellaneous related work.
Bids for this project were opened on March 14, 2024, with four proposals received.
The low bidder is East Slope Earthworks, LLC, at $270,525.50. The Engineers
Estimate is $305,930.00. A copy of the bid summary is attached for reference.
Funding for this project will be from the 2018 Parks and Paths Bond Fund and the
Sewer Maintenance Budget of the Utility Fund.
Onsite work is expected to occur this spring/summer, and be complete by midOctober.

($2,697.50)

Recommended Action :

By motion, adopt the attached resolution awarding
contract 24-01, Military Hill Resurfacing, to Motley-Motley,
Inc. for the bid amount of $655,854.22.

Public Content
The scope of contract 24-01, Military Hill Resurfacing, consists of a 2-inch grind and
hot mix asphalt overlay on the following streets:
NW Hall Drive from NW Orion Drive southeast to NW Stadium Way
NW Stadium Way from NW Nye Street to N Grand Avenue
Refer to the attached exhibit for a depiction of the project location. Work also includes
pavement patching and crack filling, as warranted, following pavement grinding
operations, miscellaneous striping, utility cover adjustments, and concrete sidewalk
/curb ramp construction. Minor spot repair of existing storm and sanitary sewer is also
included, along with the construction of a small segment of a new water main within
the proposed resurfacing limits.
Bids for this project were opened on March 7, 2024, and one proposal was received.
The low bidder is Motley-Motley, Inc., at $655,854.22. The Engineers Estimate is
$722,004.33. A copy of the bid summary is attached for reference. City Staff has
reviewed the bid and determined that the unit prices are fair and represent the work
involved.
A Transportation Improvement Board (TIB) grant is in place for this project, covering
much of the total project cost, which includes the construction contract amount, as
well as other anticipated construction engineering costs (e.g., construction materials
testing). The current project commitment by TIB is $530,288, with the City
responsible for covering the remainder of the total project cost ($150,566 of $680,854
total estimated). Local match/funding will be from the Arterial Street Fund,
Stormwater Fund, and the Water and Sewer Maintenance Budgets of the Utility Fund.
Onsite work is expected to commence as early as mid-June and be complete by the
middle of August, prior to the start of the WSU fall semester.
