CALL TO ORDER
The regular meeting of the Nooksack Valley School Board of Directors was called to order by
Chairman, Steve Jones, at 7:00 p.m. on Thursday, May 23, 2024.
MINUTES APPROVED REGULAR MEETING
Ty Elmendorf moved, and Tanya Silves seconded the motion to approve the minutes of the April 18,
2024, regular meeting.
M.C. 5-0
MASONIC EDUCATOR OF THE YEAR PRESENTATION
Superintendent Galley, alongside Dick Decima from North Whatcom County Masons, presented the
Masonic Educator of the Year Award to Jeanette Perry. Jeanette has taught music for 26 years and has
been a great mentor for many new teachers. Jeanette graciously accepted the award and showed her
gratitude to the board and district for so many years of support.

APPROVE MIDDLE SCHOOL MATH CURRICULUM
Joel Vanderyacht presented the math curriculum the middle school has been piloting, Open Up, and
recommended approval. He highlighted the tight alignment with standards and the iterative nature of
the curriculum as strengths. There was an open discussion about parental support, the rigor of the
curriculum, and the availability of resources online. Gwen Johnston and Rob Bouma, 8th grade math
teachers, strongly endorsed the curriculum as well.
Tanya Silves moved, and Halli Kimball seconded the motion to approve the Middle School Math
Curriculum
M.C. 5-0
APPROVE PRESCHOOL-GRADE 2 HANDWRITING CURRICULUM
Christine Vaughn presented on the (Size Matters Handwriting Program) SMHP preschool through
grade 2 handwriting curriculum, its strengths and research base. It was very well received by the board
and guests.
Halli Kimball moved, and Shelley Harder seconded the motion to approve Preschool to Grade 2
Handwriting Curriculum.
M.C. 5-0

EXECUTIVE SESSION
Regular session was recessed at 8:57 p.m. for executive session.
• The board discussed Superintendent Galley’s evaluation and contract.

OTHER BUSINESS
The board shared that superintendent Matt Galley received a distinguished evaluation and
commended him on another positive year. They recommended more board involvement in the
upcoming year regarding goal setting and strategic planning, as well as continuing to develop strong
leadership across the district.
Halli Kimball moved, and Shelley Harder seconded a motion to accept Superintendent Galley’s
evaluation.
