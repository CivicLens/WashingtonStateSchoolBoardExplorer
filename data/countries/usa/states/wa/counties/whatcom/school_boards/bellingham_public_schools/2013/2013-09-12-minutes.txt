1.3 Audience
Dr. Smith welcomed audience members. The following individuals addressed the Board:
Karen Hart, President of Service Employees International Union (SEIU) 925,
commended those involved in the bargaining process this year. She said the process was
respectful and collaborative. She acknowledged the work of district representatives Nora
Klewiada, Mike Anderson, and Mark Dalton, and SEIU representatives Allan Botts,
Theresa Bartl, Robert Munson, Peter Odushkin, Matthew Read, Mea Tyas, Eric
Weissenborn, and Linda Wiebusch.
SEIU Chapter President Matthew Read said the district and association worked well
together during the bargaining process. He asked the district to consider reestablishing
positions that were eliminated as a part of budget reductions in the past few years.
SEIU representative Robert Munson thanked the district for a mutually respectful
bargaining process. He asked the district to work with the association to get affordable
health insurance for employees who need it and to support SEIU in its efforts to
discourage foreclosures on locally owned real estate.

3.1

INFORMATIONAL ITEMS
School Board Directors’ Reports
Dr. Stockburger commended Dr. Baker and district staff for efforts to eliminate and/or reduce the cost of
school supplies and activities fees.
Ms. Hackler attended the Aug. 30 Staff Back-to-School Celebration, and she visited Columbia and
Parkview elementary schools on the first day of school. She attended the Columbia Elementary School
PTA meeting and the performance of Juyungo, a traditional Andean dance group from Ecuador, at
Kulshan Middle School.
Dr. Smith and Dr. Gass met with the Superintendent to discuss the district’s ongoing work in the area of
metrics.
Ms. Bashaw attended the Aug. 30 Staff Back-to-School Celebration and she attended an open house at
Sehome High School this week.

3.2

Superintendent’s Announcements and Reports
Dr. Baker said that he has visited all 22 schools since opening day. During the first week of school,
enrollment issues have been a primary focus.
Dr. Baker reported that the district will begin initial collective bargaining conversations with Bellingham
Education Association (BEA) this month.
Ron Cowan, Assistant Superintendent of Finance and Operations, reviewed current enrollment numbers
and updated the board on Birchwood Elementary School construction activity.
Tanya Rowe, Executive Director of Communications and Community Relations, distributed a Bond Fact
Sheet. The information is on the district website and will be shared with families, staff, students, and
local civic and community groups.
