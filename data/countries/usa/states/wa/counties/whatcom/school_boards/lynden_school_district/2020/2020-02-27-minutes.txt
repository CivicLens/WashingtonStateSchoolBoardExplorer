School Board Meeting
Minutes
Lynden High School Library
6:30 P.M.
1. Call to Order, Welcome, Pledge of Allegiance and Roll Call
Meeting called to order at 6:31 P.M. Board members in attendance were Steve Jilk, Brian Johnson, Kelli Kettels,
and David Vis. Director CJ Costanti was absent and excused. Student Directors Riley Anderson and Ruby
VanderHaak were absent and excused.
School Board President, Steve Jilk requested to amend the Agenda to move the topic of “Be the One Mentor”
program topic up in the Agenda and to include the topic of “Joint Meeting with the City of Lynden Topics” in the
Agenda.
Brian Johnson motioned to approve to amend the Agenda seconded by Kelli Kettels. Motion carried by voice vote.
2. Approval of Meeting Minutes
 February 13, 2019
Kelly Kettels motioned to approve the meeting minutes seconded by David Vis. Motion carried by voice vote.

4. Information from the Audience
None at this meeting.
5. Finance and Operations
The Board reviewed information on the budget for January containing a summary of financial status,
enrollment, general fund comparative, and revenues and expenditures.

8(A). Board Work Session
 Be the One Mentor Program
Denny and Nancy McHarness of Be the One mentoring program provided an update on the continuing
work taking place to ensure a successful mentoring programs in both the middle and high school
campuses, as well as other schools within our Whatcom community. They shared the needs of the
relationships with community support and community relationships with students. Denny & Nancy
provided testimonials by students, mentors, and supporters on the positive influence of this program in the
community. Denny and Nancy thanked Lisa Reynolds, Brian Clemmer, and the Lynden School District for
their support.
6. Action Items
 School Year Calendar 2020-2021
The Board reviewed two options of the 2020-2021 School Year Calendar voted on by the Lynden
Education Association (LEA). It was requested by the Board to add the term to the calendar “Subject to
Change” based on LEA negotiations.
Brian Johnson motioned to approve the 2020-2021 School Year Calendar with school start before Labor Day,
contingent on LEA negotiations seconded by Kelli Kettels. Motion carried by voice vote.


Resolution No. 01-20 – Surplus of Property – S. British Columbia Avenue Unit F-1, Unit F-2
The Board discussed the approval to surplus the property identified generally as the Special Education
Offices, and Community Transitions. Jim Frey explained the process to declare surplus of this property.
Brian Johnson motioned to approve Resolution No. 01-20 seconded by Kelli Kettels. Motion carried by voice vote.
7. Adjourn Meeting to go into Board Work Session
Meeting adjourned at 7:10 P.M.
8(B). Board Work Session
 Program Updates
David VanderYacht provided information on programs for English Language Learners (ELL), Tim Metz
provided information on Social Emotional Learning (SEL) and Elizabeth Hamming provided information on
Literacy Support for Dyslexia and Literacy Programs. They spoke of plans to provide support for
interventions and the general educational programs.


Joint Meeting with City of Lynden Topics
Jim Frey provided some topics of discussion such as: District Growth; Property Availability; What are the
needs of the City and School District.
