4. Information from the Audience
Topics of discussion from the audience included: Need proof from the local health officials why masks
are needed and how effective masks are; Concerns that government will enforce vaccines on children;
Mental health affects of wearing masks; Stand together to make change in mandates with other
districts and government entities; Loss of friends because of different beliefs; Board needs to step-up,
parent input falling on deaf ears; Mask wearing is child abuse; Beliefs that COVID-19 is a fake
pandemic; No science in the health data; Let parents raise their kids, not government; Be on the same
team with parents, drop the mandate; Out of school activities are happening without masks; Do what
makes sense, masks are not working; Prevention at homecare for recognized COVID by offering
supplements and foods of parents choice; Board needs to address OSPI and government; Masks
affecting staff at other entities; Masks affecting children between school and sport activities;
Concerns of bullying for kids that don’t vaccinate; Research more from legal counsel about
government mandates.
The Board of Directors thanked the audience for their time to bring concerns, questions, and
information to their attention. The Board notified the audience that the next regularly scheduled
meeting will be held on September 9th.
Board President, Steve Jilk, requested a 10-minute break from 9:55 to 10:05 P.M.
5. Governors Order and Washington DOH Direction – Face Coverings
The Board of Directors discussed the comments with the audience and expressed that moving
forward, decisions made are in the best interest of the community and aligned with public health
direction and guidance. Board Vice President, Brian Johnson motioned to put forward a draft
statement as to where the Board stands on the state mandates at a local level and gubernatorial
level. The draft statement will be reviewed by the Board.
Brian Johnson motioned to move forward with a draft statement seconded by Kelli Kettels. Motion
carried by voice vote.
