Recommended Action :

To approve "First Reading" of policies 2402, 2403, 2404,
2405, 2406, 2407, and 2408 related to credit for
competency/proficiency in required subject areas

Public Content
These policy adoptions are being considered at this time in response to the State Board of Education's
"Emergency Rules for Waiver of Certain Graduation Requirements in Response to Novel Coronavirus".
WAC 180-51-050 requires local school boards to adopt an authorizing policy before districts can award
competency-based credit to students. Therefore, WSSDA has provided a new model policy for each
required subject area, and the board is asked to approve "First Reading" of the following new policies:
2402 Credit for Competency/Proficiency in English Language Arts
2403 Credit for Competency/Proficiency in Math
2404 Credit for Competency/Proficiency in Science
2405 Credit for Competency/Proficiency in Social Studies
2406 Credit for Competency/Proficiency in Fine Art
2407 Credit for Competency/Proficiency in Health and Physical Education
2408 Integrated Environmental and Sustainability Education
Procedures 2402P, 2403P, 2404P, 2405P, 2406P, and 2407P do not require formal approval but are
provided for additional review and information. The board will be asked to approve "Second Reading" and
adoption of these policies at the May meeting.

3410, 3412, 3413, 3416, 3420, and 3424 related to
student health

Public Content
The board is asked to approve "Second Reading" and adoption of the following policies related to student
health. Updates to these policies were discussed at the March 23 meeting. Due to this meeting's unusual
format resulting from the current COVID-19 emergency, President Gibson has recommended the
adoption of these policies in a single motion for the sake of efficiency.
Policy 3410 Student Health - This policy is being updated in accordance with WSSDA
recommendations to provide additional clarity and specificity. There is no corresponding procedure.

Policy 3412 Automated External Defibrillators - This policy is being updated in accordance with
WSSDA recommendations and statutory requirements. The procedure, which is not being updated
at this time, is provided for additional review and information.
Policy 3413 Student Immunization and Life Threatening Health Conditions - This policy is
being updated in accordance with WSSDA recommendations and new statutory requirements. The
procedure does not require formal approval but is provided for additional review and information.
Policy 3416 Medication at School - This policy is being updated in accordance with WSSDA
recommendations and statutory requirements. The procedure does not require formal approval but
is provided for additional review and information.
Policy 3420 Anaphylaxis Prevention and Response - This policy is being updated in accordance
with WSSDA recommendations and statutory requirements. The procedure does not require formal
approval but is provided for additional review and information.
Policy 3424 Opioid Related Overdose Reversal - Adoption of this new policy has been
recommended by WSSDA as essential in response to Senate Bill 5380 "Opioid Use Disorder".
Pursuant to the board's request at the March 23 meeting, a cross reference has been added to
Policy 2121 Substance Abuse Program. The procedure does not require formal approval but is
provided for additional review and information.

7. Executive Session
Subject :

A. Executive Session

Meeting :

Apr 20, 2020 - Regular Board Meeting

Category :

7. Executive Session

Type :

Discussion

Public Content
As per legal guidelines, the board may choose to recess into executive session to consider employment
or dismissal of personnel, to review the performance of a public employee, to consult with legal counsel,
to consider the position to be taken in collective bargaining, or to consider acquisition or sale of real
estate.
Prior to the start of executive session, the president will announce the purpose and length of the
session.
At the close of the executive session, the directors will reconvene in regular session at which point
they may take formal action or the meeting will be adjourned.
The Board will move into executive session for the purpose of considering the employment of personnel. The executive session is
anticipated to last for up to thirty (30) minutes. No action is anticipated following the executive session.
