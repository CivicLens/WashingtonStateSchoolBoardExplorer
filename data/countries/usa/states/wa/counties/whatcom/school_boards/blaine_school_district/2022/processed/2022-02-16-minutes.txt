Blaine School District No. 503
  Special Board Meeting
Wednesday, February 16, 2022
  Minutes
  1. Special Board Meeting
A. Call to Order
In accordance with guidelines related to the COVID-19 pandemic emergency, this meeting was conducted with both virtual and in-person components. President Thomas called the meeting to order at 5:00 p.m. The Director District 5 position was vacant at the time of this meeting. The other four directors were in attendance.
     Todd Nunamaker, Dougal Thomas, Charles E Gibson, Erika Creydt

Superintendent Dr. Christopher Granger described the format to be used in conducting this meeting.
  2. Director Candidate Interviews
A. Director Candidate Interviews
The directors interviewed two candidates for the vacant board position representing Director District 5. Each candidate was given the opportunity to be interviewed individually.

Paul L. Berg was interviewed from approximately 5:05-5:40 p.m. Mr. Berg is a retired educator residing in Semiahmoo. He worked as a High School Art Teacher in Bellingham Public Schools for over 40 years. During that time, he also completed a Masters degree at WWU in Art Education, as well as additional graduate work in School Administration. Mr. Berg also served as a volunteer firefighter for many years in Skagit County. Since his retirement in 2010, Mr. Berg has continued to volunteer in a variety of ways to support educational opportunities for children and youth. Mr. Berg understands the complexities of public education, and he has experience advocating for schools at the state level.

Donald J. Leu was interviewed from approximately 5:45-6:14 p.m. Dr. Leu is also a retired educator with a wide variety of experience, starting with teaching positions in the Peace Corps overseas and in a Bay Area elementary school. After completing his Ph.D., he spent the remainder of his professional career in higher education as a professor and researcher with a special focus on language and literacy driven by his passion to prepare youth for their future. Dr. Leu's family has resided in Whatcom County for more than a hundred years, and although he spent much of his life in other areas, he was born in Bellingham. Since retirement, he has settled in the Semiahmoo community, and he desires to continue supporting the advancement of youth through volunteer service and give back to public education that has meant so much to his life.

Both candidates were asked the following questions:
Please talk about your interest in serving as a School Board member.
What would you consider to be the overall, greatest strengths of the Blaine School District?
What challenges and opportunities do you see as most prevalent in the work of educating students?
What do you see as your role as a board member and the role of the board as a whole?
What experiences, personal or professional, do you believe will most positively impact your role as a School Board member?
We have a number of facilities under consideration that are in need of modernization or replacement. What information do you believe will be of greatest value to the voters in considering a bond initiative in the future?
The K-12, public education funding challenge continues to generate much air time in the State of Washington. What do you believe it will take to accomplish/reach the full funding goal?
The board and the superintendent annually work to align their goals and action plans with those in place at the building and program levels. How can the board best assure that limited resources remain focused on the improvement of learning opportunities for all students?
What concerns do you have specific to service as a School Board member?
Do you have any prior experiences that may shed negative light on your service as a School Board member in the Blaine School District?
What strategies might you suggest for involving more parents and community members in their schools?
What questions might you have for current members of the board?
  3. Executive Session
A. Executive Session
At 6:15 p.m., President Thomas announced that the board would move into executive session for the purpose of discussing the appointment of a candidate to the vacant board position. The executive session was anticipated to last approximately thirty (30) minutes. If there was consensus, the board may take action following the executive session.

At 7:00 p.m., it was announced that the board would continue to meet in executive session until approximately 7:30 p.m.
  4. Reconvene and Action
A. Appointment of Director
The board moved back into regular session at 7:29 p.m.

President Thomas made a motion to appoint Don Leu to the board position representing
Director District 5 until the next general local election in 2023.
The motion was seconded by Director Gibson.
There was no further discussion.
Final Resolution: Motion Carries 4-0
Roll Call Vote: Directors Nunamaker, Gibson, Creydt, and Thomas voted in favor of this appointment.
  5. Adjourn
A. Adjourn
There being no further business, the meeting was adjourned by a unanimous vote at 7:31pm.
Motion by Todd Nunamaker, second by Erika Creydt
Final Resolution: Motion Carries 4-0
Yea: Todd Nunamaker, Dougal Thomas, Charles E Gibson, Erika Creydt
