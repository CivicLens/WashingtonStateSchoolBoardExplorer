3. Reports
Yakama Tribal Council Update: Elese Washines, Tribal Council Liaison, reported JOM funds have been
calculated and distributed to schools. She explained the purpose of JOM funding and how it is used to
help Native American families. Elese talked about the modernization act and its implementation. She
informed Wapato Schools represent the largest number of students on JOM in the State. She learned
that this year funding was increased due to inflation and districts will receive $80 per student. The funds
are managed by the JOM parent committee. The modernization act has revised its JOM eligibility
requirements and more students may be eligible for JOM funding.
She reported Title VI will receive a budget proposal to match funding similar to JOM program.
She is looking forward to Tribal Consultation in December. She encouraged the District to schedule a
time and date to present Tribal Consultation to Tribal Council.
Superintendent's Report: Kelly Garza, Superintendent, reported:
• Conferences held last week, October 17-18. This is the second year the District has held all grades
conferences on same days. He reported parent attendance was: Adams 96%, Satus 90%, Simcoe
94%, Camas 92%, WMS 81.4%, WHS 18.3%, PACE 82%.
• He hosted the 1st Superintendent Student Voice Matters Group on October 23rd. Students from
Wapato High School, Middle School and PACE participated in activities and discussion regarding
discipline, student disrespect in the classroom and sporting events, school lunch, and cell phone use.
The next student meeting is scheduled to take place in January 2025.
• WMS 6th graders will have the opportunity to participate in the new RISE UP GEAR UP grant that
focuses on college preparedness, visits to colleges and provide support through senior year.
• The Board asked about the Districts process for student cell phone use. The Board engaged in
discussion regarding the current policy regarding cell phones.
