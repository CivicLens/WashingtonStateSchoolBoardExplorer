3. Reports
Superintendent's Report: Kelly Garza, Superintendent, reported:
He will host the last Student Voice Matters Group meeting this week on Wednesday. This will be the final meeting of the current school year. The agenda topics are Maintenance/Transportation and Food Services. The student group will have met 4 times this year with the purpose of gathering input, thoughts and ideas about education and impact from a student’s perspective.
Tomorrow Wapato High School will host the 3rd annual CTE Construction Trades Competition.  There will be 6 teams from schools of the lower valley participating.
Update of the FFA Competition last week, where students did very well and will be applying what they are learning in the classroom.
An update of post season athletic results: Boys Soccer tied their last game but lost in PK’s. It was a great season for Boys Soccer. Baseball is moving onto playoffs. The last time HS Baseball advanced onto playoffs was in 2004. Also moving onto Districts are Tennis, Track and Golf. He reported Wapato Middle School had a great successful spring season.
As of today, there are 102 students registered for Kindergarten for the coming school year. This is an increase of 83 students from same time last year.
The next regular board meeting will be held at Simcoe Elementary to celebrate the Superintendent Awards.
Shannon Torres, Executive Director of Teaching and Learning, shared a video from Middle School 7th and 6th grade GearUp students at UW learning workshop, where students toured the grounds, visited campus and participated in hands on activities focused on engineering.
Construction Update: Kevin Hill, Executive Director, reported he meets with OAC every Wednesday to plan for the upcoming meeting with the PRC Board on 5/23 to present the District’s design model build recommendation for approval. Mr. Hill explained the process for requesting qualifications and selection process of companies once the model is approved.  The board engaged in discussion regarding high quality construction and architect companies.  Mr. Hill commented different companies have reached out to him to tour the district and review the scope and sequence of the project. He explained the goal of the district is to work with high quality companies that will deliver a project with in the needs of the district. The next step is to wait for approval of design build recommendation, move forward with request for proposals and then meet to select a construction and architect company.
Mr. Hill presented an update of the potential new electric bus. He reported still gathering information and then will review if beneficial and cost effective for the District to purchase an electric bus.  He commented the main concern is the housing, maintenance, and sustainability of an electric bus.
Mr. Garza reported an update on seatbelts in busses as the Board has requested more information. He has begun to gather data from neighboring districts of pros and cons of seatbelts.  He noted many districts have chosen not to purchase busses with seatbelts.  Mr. Hill explained some reasons are of safety concerns such as if in an accident, students may not be able to free themselves and the traffic citation to a bus driver if students is not wearing seatbelts are the same as in a personal vehicle.
