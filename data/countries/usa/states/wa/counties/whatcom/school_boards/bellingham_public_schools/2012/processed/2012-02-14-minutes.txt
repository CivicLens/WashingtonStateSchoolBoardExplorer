The Bellingham School Board of Directors met in regular session February 14, 2013 in the
Board Room of the District Office, 1306 Dupont Street, Bellingham, Washington.
OPENING ITEMS
At 6 p.m. President Ken Gass called the regular meeting to order.
Roll Call
Present: Directors Kelly Bashaw, Camille Hackler, Steve Smith, Scott Stockburger, President
Ken Gass, Superintendent Greg Baker and recorder Nancy Merry.

REGULAR AGENDA ITEMS
Audience
No audience presented.
School Board Directors’ Reports
Each Board member reported on attending the linkage with the Mayor and Bellingham City
Council and expressed a desire to hold future joint meetings. Each also reported on attending the
School Board site visit to Roosevelt Elementary School, and participated in the Board retreat on
January 17.
Additionally, Camille Hackler attended the Barnett Berry lecture at the Whatcom Museum. Mr.
Berry is the Founder and President of the Center for Teaching Quality ( CTQ), Inc. Ms. Hackler
attended a Professional Education Advisory Board (PEAB) meeting at Western Washington
University; Alderwood Elementary School Martin L. King event; Alderwood Elementary School
PTA meeting; and a breakfast meeting with Greg Baker and Steve Smith.
Steve Smith also attended the Barnett Berry lecture, and heard a talk by Dr. Bette Hyde, Director
of the Department of Early Learning.
Scott Stockburger met with Greg Baker and Ken Gass for a luncheon meeting. Dr. Stockburger
attended a leadership training in Olympia sponsored by Washington State School Directors
Association. He felt the workshop was valuable and included a session on values-driven
leadership.

Superintendent’s Announcements and Report
Dr. Greg Baker reported on attending a meeting at the Gates Foundation on support for early
childhood learning. He shared with attendees how Bellingham was able to establish full-time
kindergarten for all students. Dr. Baker also reported on attending a meeting sponsored by
Microsoft on technology and how other school districts have built their technology and
infrastructure.
Dr. Baker gave an update on the work of the Facilities Planning Task Force. Dr. Baker shared the
Task Force recommendations and the brochure with the Board and discussed the timeline and
next steps. Assistant Superintendent Ron Cowan, Executive Director Tanya Rowe and Executive
Director Steve Clarke were present to respond to questions from the Board. Superintendent
Baker will share his reflections with the community and gather more input before bringing a
formal recommendation to the Board at a future date after which there will be additional
opportunity for public comments.

CONSENT AGENDA ITEMS
Steve Smith moved to approve the consent agenda with the exception of item 3.11, Revision of
Policy and Procedure 3142, International Student Exchange. Dr. Smith requested clarification of
students visiting Washington State under a B-2 visa. Camille Hackler seconded approval of the
motion as submitted. The motion carried unanimously.
Executive Director Steve Clarke will do additional research on whether students with a B-2 visa
can be omitted from the policy and bring it back to the Board at the next meeting.
