8. Board Work Session
 Secondary School Improvement Plans (SIP’s)
Jim Frey introduced the Secondary Principals and explained how they will be presenting their School
Improvement Plans (SIP) to the board. High School Principal, Ian Freeman spoke about the achievement

gap and how to be responsive to the achievement gap. Ian talked about the interventions and
professional development being used to support students, and shared some of the challenges of
providing assistance to student needs. High School Assistant Principal, Lisa McKeen spoke about the
teaming efforts between administrators, counselors, teachers, interventionists, and special programs to
support students in closing the achievement gap. Ian shared a story about a student engaged in the
Round-About program as well as a CTE engineering program and how he successfully progressed from
middle school to high school with these programs, along with caring school staff. Middle School
Assistant Principal, Patrick McClure spoke to the question if there are similar programs at the middle
school. Patrick explained that there are similar programs. However, the support is different based on
core class requirements, but that the middle school is currently expanding more support programs.
Lynden Middle School Principal, Molly Mitchell-Mumma explained the focus on instruction (SIOP, 5D,
and improving other Tier 1 Instruction). Molly spoke about collaboration of lesson plans between schools
and outside support entities. Molly & Patrick shared a success story of a teacher utilizing a collaborative
lesson plan and how it encouraged the students to engage more in the class. Patrick shared a student
success story about how the student’s teacher based her instructional practices on the collaborative
lesson planning and it resulted in a positive outcome for the student. Molly and Patrick addressed the
challenges that are barriers to improving instruction.
Lynden Academy Principal, Chris Jorgensen provided information about how work is taking place in the
Professional Learning Community (PLC) to establish regular collaboration time and the vision for lesson
collaboration. Chris also shared about some of their intervention programs. Lynden Academy Teacher,
Ellie Mink provided examples of how Lynden Academy staff is sharing lesson plans and the positive
impact on both students and staff.
