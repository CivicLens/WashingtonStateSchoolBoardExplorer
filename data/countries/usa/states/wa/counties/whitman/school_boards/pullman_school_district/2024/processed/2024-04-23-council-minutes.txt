J. Presentations
Presentations: 1. Downtown Pullman Association Update
Downtown Pullman Association Executive Director Mallory Nash shared updates
on the mission, values, and achievements over the past year. Mallory explained
that affiliation with the Washington Main Street and Main Street American program
provides resources and a proven approach to boost local business sales and
operations.
Presentations: 2. United Way of Whitman County Update
United Way of Whitman County Executive Director Deanna Bren gave the Council
an overview of the work the United Way performs and what the organization has
accomplished over the past year.
Presentations: 3. Welch Comer Downtown Pullman Project Update
Welch-Comer Vice President Matt Gillis provided updates on the Downtown
Pullman Project. The work completed thus far includes turning Olsen Street into
angle parking and removing trees, vegetation, and asphalt on Main Street.
Matt Gillis spoke about the request from Pullman Civic Trust to extend the pavers
in Pine Street Plaza, the difficulties with the request, and the cost associated with
the design change. Welch-Comer does not recommend moving forward with a
design change due to the significant increases in construction costs and the
additional time required to complete the design change.

N. Regular Agenda
Transit Manager Wayne Thompson explained that the transit service is scheduled to
expand to the Pullman-Moscow Regional Airport in mid-May 2024, which coincides with
the opening of the airport's new terminal. With the additional hours required to serve all
flights, added Driver, Dispatcher, and supervisory staffing are also necessary. The
service expansion is grant-funded through June 2025, and the continuation of the service
and associated staffing will rely on verifying additional revenue sources as that date
approaches.
