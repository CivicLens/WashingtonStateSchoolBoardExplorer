1. Call to Order, Welcome, Pledge of Allegiance and Roll Call
Meeting called to order at 6:30 P.M. Board members in attendance were Steve Jilk, Brian Johnson, CJ Costanti, and
David Vis. Director Kelli Kettels was absent and excused. Student Directors Riley Anderson and Ruby VanderHaak
were present.
2. Oath of Office
Jim Frey administered the Oath of Office to elected Board of Directors, Brian Johnson, CJ Costanti, and David
Vis.
3. Good News






The District Office would like to recognize the leadership of Narlene VanBeek, Shane Stanfill and Reed
Gillig, and the services of the 65 food service, maintenance & facility, and transportation department
employees. The community of Lynden is served well by these high functioning departments.
The Main Street Campus renovation has begun! Tiger Construction is beginning work in anticipation of a
new Lynden Pre-school location and the relocation of District Office Administration Offices from the overcrowded high school. High school programs will expand into the space currently occupied by District Office
staff.
Thank you to the Whatcom County Health Department for teaming with our staff and schools to provide a
variety of immunization vaccinations to our students and families at an Immunization Clinic held on
December 18th at the Lynden Middle School.

6. Information from the Audience
Danelle Alexander provided information on studies of dyslexia and programs that are used to teach students
with dyslexia, she request that the District review these programs. Nancy Ruhl spoke about her personal
experiences with dyslexia and programs that she has used and has encouraged the Board to review program
options to incorporate in schools. Dante Alexander spoke about Washington Legislature that is in review to
address dyslexia programs and encouraged the Board to move forward to review and implement a program.
7. Reports
Superintendent
 Special Programs Update
Tim Metz, Special Programs Director, provided an update on recent professional learning activities and the
collaboration between colleagues. Tim spoke about the training for the elementary schools on Safe and
Civil School trainings and observations that took place. Tim informed the Board on special education
student counts and eligibility, certificated staff, and required program audits.
Teaching and Learning
None at this meeting.
Finance and Operations
The Board reviewed information on the budget for November containing a summary of financial status,
enrollment, general fund comparative, and revenues and expenditures.
Board Reports
 School Improvement Plans (SIP) Reports
The Board discussed the format of the SIP presentations provided by school principals and the types of
information they would like to review and engage in discussion with the principals at future meetings.


Legislative Priorities
Jim Frey spoke about the upcoming Legislative Session and possible topics to be addressed. Jim provided
the Legislative Priorities from WASA and WSSDA.



School Board Listening Post
The Board discussed their evening with community members where discussion on budget, bonds and
levies, district growth took place, and dyslexia. The Board will discuss options for future Listening Posts.

8. Action Items
 Sale of Surplus Property – B.C. Avenue
Jim Frey explained the process to surplus the B.C. Avenue property. The Board needs to approve a
motion to notify the public of the proposed sale for public comments at a future meeting.
Brian Johnson motioned to approve a Notification to Publish Sale of Surplus Property seconded by CJ Costanti.
Motion carried by voice vote.
