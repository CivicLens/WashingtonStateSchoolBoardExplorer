DRAFT MINUTES OF THE SPECIAL MEETING/PUBLIC HEARING
BELLINGHAM SCHOOL DISTRICT BOARD OF DIRECTORS
August 9, 2017
OPENING ITEMS
Meeting Date and Location:
5:30 p.m. August 9, 2017 – 1306 Dupont St., Bellingham, Washington
Call to Order/Roll Call:
Mr. Benjamin called the meeting to order at 5:30 p.m. Present: Directors Kelly Bashaw,
Douglas Benjamin (President), Camille Diaz Hackler, Quenby Peterson, Steven Smith,
Superintendent Greg Baker, and recorder Ruth Rasmussen.
Audience – n/a
Public Hearing:
Mr. Benjamin explained the purpose of the public hearing: to provide an opportunity for
anyone who would like to speak for or against the adoption of the 2017-18 budget to
comment before the board takes action on the budget this evening.
No audience presented and no public testimony was offered relating to the proposed
amendment.
Superintendent’s Announcements and Report: Summary of Dr. Baker’s comments:
 Back-to-School Activities: The district is now operating in full gear in preparation
for the first day of school on Aug. 23. Administrators are back, hiring continues,
employee orientations and professional development activities are underway, and
the 1:1 technology initiative is on schedule.
 Options High School: Construction crews are working long hours to ensure the
school will be open on time. The grand opening celebration/ribbon cutting is
3 p.m., Aug. 21.
 Back to School All Staff Celebration: The event begins at 7 a.m. Aug. 22 with
breakfast at Options High School and a tour of the building. The formal program
begins at 8 a.m. at Bellingham High School.
 Central Kitchen Update: Whatcom Community Foundation continues to be a
valued community partner, providing the district with resources and visioning
ideas.
 2017-18 Budget: Dr. Baker reviewed the budget proposal, discussed highlights of
the budget, and answered questions from the board. Discussion items included:
o the anticipated impact, still being analyzed, of the recently approved state
budget;
o last year’s key accomplishments;
o a review of the budget development process; and
o noteworthy changes in this year’s budget, including increased special
education and counseling support.
