October 26, 2017
School Board Meeting – Work Session
Minutes
Lynden High School Library
6:30 P.M.
1. Call to Order, Welcome, Pledge of Allegiance and Roll Call
Meeting called to order at 6:32 P.M. Board members in attendance were Brian Johnson, Steve Jilk, Kevin Burke,
and Kelli Kettels. Student Directors Leslie Strandgard and Rubia Orellano were present.
2. Approval of Meeting Minutes
• Meeting Minutes October 12, 2017
Kevin Burke motioned to approve the meeting minutes seconded by Kelli Kettels. Motion carried by voice vote.
3. Consent Agenda
• Personnel Action Items – October 26, 2017
Steve Jilk motioned to approve the consent agenda seconded by Kelli Kettels. Motion carried by voice vote.
4. Information from the Audience
None at this meeting.
5. Finance and Operations
• Budget Update – September Preliminary
Jim Frey explained the process to finalizing the 2016-2017 budget. Patti Fairbanks provided information
on the preliminary budget for September containing a summary of financial status, enrollment, general
fund comparative, and revenues and expenditures.
6. Adjourn Meeting to go into Board Work Session
Meeting adjourned at 6:35 P.M.
6. Board Work Session
• District Planning
The Board reviewed planning for the future regarding current facilities and providing information to the
community about future new facility needs. The Board considered program space, classroom space,
community space, property requirements, and levy needs. Jim Frey will bring further information at a
future meeting to continue moving discussion forward.
•

Early Learning
Tim Metz spoke about the challenges of preschool funding and special education needs growth in
the Early Learning programs. Tim shared about the current Community Playgroup taking place and
the outlook for developing this program further in the future. Tim explained the reason for a
proposed Early Kindergarten program and provided a timeline to possibly begin the program.

•

Social Emotional Learning
Tim Metz informed the Board of staff training, student programs and community outreach
regarding Harassment, Intimidation and Bullying (HIB) and suicide awareness. Tim shared results
of previous data from the Washington State Healthy Youth Survey and provided examples of how
the district is currently communicating with staff, students, and community youth organizations
about the opportunities to be educated and informed of these important topics.
