Kevin Richins, LHS Teacher was selected by the Lynden School District Administration as the Educator of
the Year presented by the Lynden/Ferndale Masonic Lodge. At the May 23rd Board
Meeting, Mr. Richins will be presented with $750 to be used for curriculum/student learning.
Congratulations and well deserved!
Lynden Academy Freshman student, Matthew Enertson, just finished the Washington State Legislature
Page Program in Olympia. What a great accomplishment!
Lynden High School student and Student Board of Director, Cynthia Torres attended a two day "Future
Cougars Embracing Diversity" program which allows students an opportunity to see firsthand what WSU
has to offer. For attending and participating in the program, she received a $1,000 visitation award for her
to apply to fall quarter.
Reading Month at Fisher wrapped up with their 6th annual Character Parade. Students and staff dressed
as their favorite book characters and some appearances included Waldo, Harry Potter and friends, Piggy
and Gerald, Ms. Frizzle, Splat the Cat, the Lorax, Fancy Nancy and many others. Additionally, their reading
contest was a major success. Fisher read more than 170,000 minutes!!
Fisher hosted their 2nd annual Multicultural Night with more than 30 families attending. The potluck
included foods from a variety of regions including Guatemala, Mexico, India, Italy, Ireland and different
regions of the United States. Additionally, students attending Extended Day shared personal boards about
their own culture and family backgrounds.
Congratulations to LHS student, Emira Jongema for receiving the Congress-Gundestag Youth Exchange
scholarship for 2019-2020, this will allow her to attend the academic school year in Germany! Many
teachers and staff at LHS helped her to achieve her dream!
LHS Plant sale is going on!
The Spring Play practice of The Hobbit is taking place and a lot of students are involved.
LMS
Teacher Appreciation Week was celebrated with many events taking place
