no other nominations. The motion carried unanimously.
Steve Smith nominated Scott Stockburger as Vice-President. Ken Gass seconded the motion.
There were no other nominations. The motion carried unanimously.
President Bashaw appointed Ken Gass to serve as legislative representative for the upcoming
year and Dr. Gass agreed to serve in this capacity.

REPORTS
2010–11 Annual Financial Report
Brett Greenwood, Business and Finance Manager, presented the district financial summary for
the 2010–2011 school year. Enrollment in the K–12 regular program decreased by 28 full-time
equivalent (FTE) students and enrollment in the Running Start Program increased by 52 FTE, for
a total increase of 24 FTE. Revenues totaled $101,877,000. Expenditures were $103,177,221.
The percent of expenditures spent on teaching and teaching support was 72.3%; 16% was spent
on support services; and 11.7% was spent on administration. Greenwood reported $3,095,316 is
committed to minimum fund balance leaving the unassigned fund balance at $2,786,952, which
aligns with the Board’s policy to keep the fund balance between three and five percent. Mr.
Greenwood thanked the technical and accounting staff for their work on preparing the report.

EL-4, Staff Treatment
Executive Director of Human Resources Nora Klewiada gave a monitoring report on EL-4, Staff

BOARD RESPONSE TO MONITORING
EL-9, Facilities Program
The Board heard the Monitoring Report for EL-9, Facilities Program, at the November 10, 2011
Board meeting. President Bashaw stated the Board determined the Superintendent is in
compliance. President Bashaw thanked Assistant Superintendent Ron Cowan and staff for their
work to maintain the district facilities. It was suggested future reports include any logs for
facilities of work that has been requested frequently or not done.
Ends 2.1, Academic Competence
The Board heard the Monitoring Report for Ends 2.1, Academic Competence, at the November
10, 2011 Board meeting. President Bashaw stated the Board determined the Superintendent is
working toward compliance. President Bashaw indicated the Board will discuss Ends 2.1
Academic Competence, at the Board retreat in January to discuss possible modifications to this
Ends.

ACTION ITEMS
Birchwood Elementary School Project Architect Selection
Assistant Superintendent of Business and Operations Ron Cowan reported that a team
interviewed five architectural firms for the pre-planning, design and contract administration
associated with the Birchwood Elementary School project. Based on the interviews, Mr. Cowan
is recommending the Board select Dykeman Architects as the architectural firm. Ken Gass
moved and Steve Smith seconded the motion to select Dykeman Architects as the architectural
firm for the pre-planning, design and contract administration associated with the Birchwood
Elementary School project. The motion carried unanimously.
Resolution 10-11, Acceptance of Maintenance and Operations Levy
Resolution 11-11, Technology Levy
Superintendent Greg Baker presented the recommendations that the School Board place two
proposals before the voters at a special election on February 14, 2012. Resolution 10-11,
Maintenance and Operations Levy would be a four (4)-year Levy to continue to support general
fund expenditures in the following amounts—$28 million in 2012; $29.3 million in 2013; $30.6
million in 2014; and $31.9 million in 2015. This levy will replace the current one which is
expiring. The other is a four (4)-year Technology Levy for technology improvements, equipment
and related costs in the following amounts—$5.5 million in 2012; 6.75 million in 2013; 6.75
million in 2014; and 6.75 million in 2015. This levy will replace the current one, which is
