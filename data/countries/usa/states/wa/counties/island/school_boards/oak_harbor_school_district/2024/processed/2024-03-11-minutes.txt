Call to Order

A regular meeting of the Board of Directors was held on Monday, March 11, 2024, in
the Administrative Service Center Board Room and was available for live streaming.
President Lynn Goebel called the meeting to order at 6:00 p.m. and led the flag salute.

Roll Call

Board members Lynn Goebel, Nicole Tesch, Sharon Jensen, and Josh MacLean were
present. Jessica Aws was absent and excused. Student board representative Maggie
Litvachuk was present. Student representative Josiah Villano was absent and excused.

Adoption of Agenda

The agenda was adopted by unanimous consent.

Consent Agenda

Director Jensen moved to approve the consent agenda. Director Tesch seconded the
motion. The motion passed unanimously.

Recognition of
Secondary Schools
Staff

Principals Nate Salisbury, Russ Peters, and Rae Wood recognized and thanked staff
members for their dedication and service to the students, staff, and families of Oak
Harbor Public Schools.

Student
Representative
Report

Student Representative Maggie Litvachuk spoke about spring sports and a recent event
for juniors to find out about options after high school which she found helpful. She
highlighted the recent OHHS Art Show, community events, and an upcoming multicultural assembly at OHHS on Friday.

Superintendent
Report

Superintendent Michelle Kuss-Cybula, while attending the NAFIS Conference in
Washington D.C., shared about celebrations happening this month including Classified
Staff Recognition. She provided an update on the strategic planning process, MTSS
work, and upcoming Instructional Reviews on June 6. Dr. Kuss-Cybula gave progress
updates on safety measures, community engagement work, budget reduction efforts,
and construction projects. Assistant Superintendent Dwight Lundstrom explained
enrollment numbers.

Secondary Schools
Annual Report

Oak Harbor High School Principal Nate Salisbury, North Whidbey Middle School
Principal Russ Peters, and Oak Harbor Intermediate School Principal Rae Wood
presented an update on the work of their schools for the 2023-2024 school year. Also
on hand to present were North Whidbey Middle School Assistant Principal Beez Lucero
and Oak Harbor Intermediate School Assistant Principal Jeff Beckley. Information
presented included SEL goals, attendance, behavior indicators, and celebrations.

Legislative Update

Director Tesch stated that the legislative session ended on March 7. There were a
couple of very small wins, and she is looking forward to the next session to see more
progress.

Board Comments

Director Jensen reported that she attended several district events, including reading to
students for Read Across America Day. Director Tesch shared that she read at several
schools and assisted with the recent Miss Oak Harbor event. Director MacLean thanked
the principals for their presentation. He is impressed with the variety of programs
offered to students such as CTE and AVID. He appreciates the added security measures
coming to more buildings. President Goebel attended the exit conference with the State
Auditor and was pleased to learn the district will be recognized with an award at an
upcoming meeting. She went to the Robotics robot reveal and commented on the extra
efforts of teachers on behalf of students. She also praised the classified staff for their
work in the district.

Superintendent
Comments

Dr. Kuss-Cybula thanked the secondary schools for their work and for exemplifying
#OneOakHarbor.

Upcoming Meetings

Regular meetings are scheduled for Monday, March 25, April 8, and April 29 at 6:00
p.m. in the Administrative Service Center Board Room. There will be a study session for
strategic planning work prior to the April 29 meeting at 5:00 p.m.

Adjourn

The meeting adjourned at 7:56 p.m.
