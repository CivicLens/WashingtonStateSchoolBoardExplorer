    STUDY SESSION 
  Call to Order 
President Levesque called the study session to order at 4:00 pm.

The clerk called roll. Directors Mary Levesque, Erica Knapp and Matt Dimond, Student Advisor Nathan Nichols and Superintendent Sweeting were in attendance.

  Overview 
A. Strategic Goals
B. District Norms
C. Learning Outcomes for this Study Session
Superintendent Sweeting shared an overview of the learning outcomes and activities for the study session.
  Activities & Topics for Discussion
A. Public Information Program Report (Policies 4000 & 4040)
Gary Sabol, Director of Communications, shared an annual report regarding the status of the Public Information Program and Public Records Requests received during the past year. Gary answered questions from the Board.
  B. Withdrawal Prior to Graduation Annual Report (Policy 3123)
Superintendent Sweeting presented the early withdrawal report, which outlines the age, and grade level for each student, the reason(s) for leaving and any follow-up data that has been collected after the student has withdrawn as required by Board Policy 3123. Dr. Sweeting answered questions from the Board.
  C. Conflict of Interest Annual Report (Policy 1610)
Superintendent Sweeting reported that there have not been any new abstentions due to conflicts of interest since our last report in August of 2023.
  D. Report of Donations Approved by Superintendent - June 2024
Superintendent Sweeting reported that she approved two donations in June 2024, a donation from Janet Iverson to Presidents Elementary in the amount of $2,000.00 for 5th grade classroom supplies and a
donation from Pioneer PTSA to Pioneer Elementary in the amount of $1,225.56 for headphones and audio jack extenders.
  E. Annual Review of Geographic Attendance Areas
Brian Lewis, Executive Director of Operations, reviewed the geographic attendance areas designated for each building in the district as required by Board Policy 3130. Brian answered questions from the Board.
  F. Annual Review of Risk Management Program
Brian Lewis, Executive Director of Operations, reported the amounts and terms of the liability insurance policy maintained by the district as required by District Policy 6530. Brian answered questions from the Board.
  G. Report of Emergency Response Drills and Emergency Readiness
Board Policy 3432 requires the school district to conduct emergency response drills and maintain plans and procedures to respond to emergency events. Brian Lewis, Executive Director of Operations, shared high-level details about the district's emergency response drills and emergency readiness. There was discussion.
  H. Financial Highlights of Current Budget
Gina Zeutenhorst, Executive Director of Financial Services shared highlights from the current budget as of May 2024 budget reports. Gina answered questions from the Board.
  Next Meeting & Adjournment
President Levesque shared that the Board's Regular Meeting will convene following the end of this study session in the Board Room at 6:00 pm.
  With there being no further activities before the Board, the study session concluded at 4:55 pm.
  Motion: That the study session be adjourned.
  Motion by Erica Knapp, second by Matt Dimond.

  New Business
A. Interview Candidates for the Vacant Position of School Board Director - Director District No. 3
President Levesque welcomed the four candidates for the Director District No. 3 vacancy and explained the interview process for the meeting. Kelsey Ramirez, Nicole Watts, Meghan Galvin and Ronald Fisk were in attendance. Each candidate was given 20 minutes to answer six (6) questions from the Board. Public feedback was collected by forms at the meeting or via a form link for those attending the meeting virtually. The form link was available to the public at the beginning of the interviews and could be accessed until the Board recessed the meeting to convene an Executive Session to evaluate the qualifications of candidates for appointment to vacant School Board Director Director District No. 3. At the beginning of the New Business portion of the meeting, candidates moved to a waiting area. After each interview was completed, candidates returned to the waiting area and stayed there until all interviews were concluded, at which time they all returned to the board meeting in progress.
  B. City of Arlington for School Resource Officer (SRO) Services Interlocal Agreement 2024-25
Brian Lewis, Executive Director of Operations, presented the City of Arlington School Resource Officer (SRO) Services Interlocal Agreement for the 2024-25 school year. Arlington School District has contracted with the City of Arlington for School Resource Officer (SRO) services since 1999. The most recent agreement for these services expires in June 2024. The proposed agreement begins September 1, 2024 and ends June 30, 2025. Brian answered questions from the Board.
  Motion: That the Board approves the agreement with the City of Arlington for School Resource Office services as presented.
  Motion by Sheri Kelly, second by Erica Knapp.
Final Resolution: Motion Carries
Yea: Matt Dimond, Sheri Kelly, Mary Levesque, Erica Knapp

  C. 2024-25 Student Fee Schedule

  D. 2024-25 Budget Development Briefing
Gina Zeutenhorst, Executive Director of Financial Services, shared a presentation summarizing the contents of the draft budget. The final 2024-25 Arlington Public Schools budget resolution will come to the Board for a public hearing and consideration of adoption on August 12, 2024. Gina answered questions from the Board.
  E. Resolution 24-10 Interfund Loan
Gina Zeutenhorst, Executive Director of Financial Services, reported that projections indicate continued cash shortfalls may be experienced into the month of July and August of this year. The District is requesting an additional interfund load of up to $1 million, so that current obligations for July and through the end of the fiscal year can be met.
  Motion: That the Board approves Resolution 24-10 Interfund Loan.
  Motion by Erica Knapp, second by Sheri Kelly.
Final Resolution: Motion Carries
Yea: Matt Dimond, Sheri Kelly, Mary Levesque, Erica Knapp
