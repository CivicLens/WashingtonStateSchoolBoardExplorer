5. FOCUS ON DISTRICT OPERATIONS
Information: 5.01 Report on Capital Projects Bond
Board members heard several presentations with regard to the bond. The
first focused on the security upgrades the district is hoping to accomplish
during this coming summer. The second was a progress report from
Dykeman Architects on the design of the new high school. The third was
an idea in utilizing the CTE and other spaces for some specific programs.
For the safety and security vestibule project, Mr. Deebach said the district
has worked with Dykeman to put together a bid package. The bid opening
is scheduled for June 10. It is hoped the projects will be completed before
school starts in the fall. There was some discussion about the Skyline
project with regard to the additional cost and what barriers would be in
place for the central corridor. Mr. Deebach noted that the additional cost
for interior work is not significant and he expects it to stay within budget.
He said a decorative iron fencing and sliding gate would be placed in the
corridors. On the outside to the east, chain link fence would be used. He
noted panic hardware would be installed for quick evacuation of the site.
Ms. Riddle asked if this work included North Bellingham. Mr. Deebach
said no, nothing is planned except for the possibility of fencing.
At this time, Dr. Quinn noted that a student had been left off the list of
outstanding students that were recognized earlier in the meeting. She

Mr. Deebach introduced Tim Jewett who reviewed the details of the site
plan and drawings for the new high school. He said a lot of hard work has
gone into the project to this point. He said he is confident the school will
be delivered within budget. As he reviewed the plans, he noted that the

Mr. Vincent presented a new idea for the CTE program. He explained
about the use of the current CTE building to house the Golden Eagle
Academy and Ferndale Virtual Academy Programs. This would facilitate
the move of the video/web design program from the CTE building to the
Performing Arts Center where the two academies are currently located.
Thus, more square footage would be available for CTE programs and an
additional classroom. He noted the district recently was informed that the
current CTE building was not eligible for state match funding until 2024
and that the building could continue to be used as an educational space.
Mr. McLaurin thanked Mr. Vincent for his presentation. He noted the
Bond Oversight Committee had discussed the CTE building at its last
meeting. He suggested the matter be referred to the committee in order for
the committee to provide a cost benefit analysis to the board.
Ms. Cool asked what funding the district gets for students in these two
programs. Dr. Quinn said the district receives full apportionment. Also,
Ms. Cool asked what it would cost to upgrade the CTE building for these
programs.
Mr. Vincent noted the programs would utilize two classrooms. The only
cost is a new furnace. Mr. Deebach noted the roof needed some work, but
not at a whole lot of additional cost.
It was the consensus of the board to refer this matter to the Bond
Oversight Committee for recommendation.

Dr. Quinn provided an update to the school board on what the district has
done in response to the COVID-19 situation. The district: 1) continues to
provide food service and childcare. Last weeks over 10,000 meals were
served. Childcare is up to serving 14. 2) Administered a survey to staff,
students, families. The overall theme from families is frustration: how to
get kids to do the work and manage it all. 3) Making plans for next year;
the seven county district have organized a county team and are
collaborating on options.
Ms. Riddle said the statewide task force she is part of is looking at
different options for delivering education next fall. Small group meetings
are being held this week, with reports back to the large group. Then the
options will be presented to school district.
Ms. Cool, addressing Ms. Riddle, asked if the options and changes would
require added work to the district and how the district was going to fund
such things. She asked if the state would be providing additional dollars
to districts. Ms. Riddle said that all the options would involve a lot more
staff. Once the small groups have had a chance to meet, we'll now more.
Dr. Erickson asked if, in the building of the new high school, the district is
looking at new models for learning. Ms. Riddle said there will probably be
some changes however not drastically different.
6. MATTERS RESERVED FOR BOARD ACTION
Action: 6.01 Adopted Resolution No. 2019R Approving Continuous Learning
Plan
Mr. Brittain and Dr. Quinn presented Resolution No. 2019R approving
Continuous Learning Plan for the board's consideration. Mr. McLaurin
noted that the superintendent was required by law to prepare continuous
learning plans and thus consideration of the resolution.
Mr. Brittain said the resolution is a model provided by iGrants. It is
needed due to the fact that the district will fall short two days of the 180
school days required, and also will not meet the 1027 average learning
hours. Dr. Quinn noted that almost every district in the state is choosing
this option. It will allow the district to continue to receive its
apportionment this year.
Lee Anne Riddle moved to adopt Resolution No. 2019R Approving
Continuous Learning Plan, as attached hereto and made a part of the
minutes.

Action: 6.04 Adopt Administrative Policy No. 6023 Capital Bond-Levy
Mr. Deebach presented Adopt Administrative Policy No. 6023 Capital
Bond-Levy for the board's review and action. He reported that the Bond
Oversight Committee recommended that the district have a debt policy.
He said the committee has not reviewed the policy. He said the model
before the board is based on a Tacoma School District policy.
Dr. Quinn said the district is governed by many laws and having a debt
policy is not required. However, she said the oversight committee believes
it is important. Ms. Cool asked why the policy was needed. Mr. Deebach
said it does provide for more oversight. Mr. McLaurin said it is a
framework for our process.
Ms. Cool asked about the notion of a finance and audit committee that
was mentioned in the policy. Mr. Deebach said a committee would need
to be created.
It was the consensus of the board to have the oversight committee review
the policy and provide a recommendation to the board prior to the district
adopting it.
Information: 6.05 Discussion About the School Programs and Operations
Replacement Levy
Board members continued their discussion about the School Programs
and Operations Replacement Levy ballot measure.
Mr. McLaurin reviewed how the school board got to this point. He
explained the failure of the February 2020 levy, the board's decision to run
it again in April but then COVID struck. The board decided to remove the
measure from the April ballot and place it on the November 3 ballot. At
this time, the board needs to discuss the rate and duration for the levy.
Mr. McLaurin further noted that a resolution placing the matter on the

ballot is not due to the county auditor until the first week of August.
Ms. Riddle said she appreciated the thoughts of the levy community
support committee. She supports the $1.50 rate and a duration of two
years.
Mr. McLaurin agreed with Ms. Riddle.
Ms. Cool asked if the district has made enough in cuts in case it receives a
reduction of 15-20% in state apportionment. Dr. Erickson said that is an
unknown at this time. He said he supported the committee's
recommendation of the $1.50 rate. He said he wanted to be respectful of
people's financial situations. Ms.Cool agreed with the $1.50 rate.
Ms. Riddle reminded everyone that Intalco was closing too and that
people are upset. She said that supporting the levy is one way to keep jobs
in Ferndale.
Dr. Quinn noted that the board could run a supplemental levy during the
second year of the levy.
It was decided that a resolution indicating the $1.50 rate and two year
period for the levy would be placed on the June 16 meeting for action by
the board.
