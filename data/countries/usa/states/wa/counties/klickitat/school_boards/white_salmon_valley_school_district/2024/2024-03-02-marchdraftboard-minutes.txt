The regular meeting of the Board of Education, White Salmon Valley School District, Klickitat and Skamania Counties, White
Salmon, WA was called to order at 7:00 p.m. Thursday, March 28, 2024, at the WSVSD Board Room, Park Center Building, located
at 170 NW Washington St., White Salmon, WA 98672, and via a ZOOM public meeting. Present: Board Chair Laurie Stanton,
Vice-chair Peter Harkema, Kris Ostness, Matthew Byrne, Pat Dempsey, Student Representatives, Superintendent Rich Polkinghorn,
and guests.
Board Chair Laurie Stanton led the Board in the flag salute.
Additions/Deletions to Agenda: None.
Matthew Byrne moved to adopt the agenda. Seconded by Peter Harkema. Carried.
Reports:
a) Superintendent Update: Superintendent Rich Polkinghorn shared an update with the Board. Superintendent
Polkinghorn’s update included a review of the State of Public Education Funding in WA State. Superintendent
Polkinghorn shared that the district is in stable position now, but is continuing to see the decline in enrollment numbers.
It was mentioned that unless the funding model at the state changes, WSVSD will be back in a budget crisis along with
other districts in the future. Superintendent Polkinghorn informed the Board about the WSVEF Career Day that was
recently held at the High School. The Superintendent thanked the community for their time and energy participating in
this event as well as the Education Foundation, participants, staff, and the student leadership for all their work.
Superintendent Polkinghorn shared an update on the literacy instruction four-part series professional development
covering core instruction related to reading on the MTSS system. This is led by ESD 112 and staff throughout the district
are participating. An update was also shared about a CTE Rural Collaborative opportunity that WSVSD will be eligible
to participate in for the coming school year. White Salmon would serve as the hub for the collaborative, allowing CHS
students to participate in the offered courses. Superintendent Polkinghorn also informed the Board that Kevin Olin will
be retiring at the end of the school year. He thanked Kevin for his years of service and congratulated him on his
retirement.
b) Members of the Board: Kris Ostness gave a brief update regarding the Wellness Committee, sharing that they are
looking for more members from the schools and community to help guide their work on Policy 6700. Pat Dempsey
inquired regarding the Oak Ridge Rd. safety concerns due to the planned future development.
c) Student Representatives Report: The Student Representative shared an update on the Career Fair stating it was a good
experience for students. They also provided an update on the track and boys’ soccer teams. An update was also given on
Mock Trials, Knowledge Bowl, FBLA, and SOS. One representative also shared concerns regarding advertising to
students.
Recognition and Good News:
a) Wallace & Priscilla Stevenson Intermediate School: Wallace & Priscilla Stevenson Intermediate School Teacher,
Laura Meyer and several sixth-grade students shared a presentation with the Board about their recent Outdoor School
Experience at the OMSI Camp Grey Costal Discovery Center. The students shared their favorite experiences at camp.
The Board thanked Laura for her work and the students for coming and sharing their experiences.
Audience Comments: None.
