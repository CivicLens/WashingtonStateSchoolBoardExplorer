MERIDIAN SCHOOL DISTRICT #505
WORK SESSION & REGULAR SCHOOL BOARD MEETING
MEETING HELD IN PERSON & VIA ZOOM
February 21, 2024

REGULAR MEETING
The regular meeting was called to order by Board President Steve Cowin at 5:00 p.m.
Board members present at the meeting were Steve Corwin, Jana Haugen, and Michelle Simmons.
BOARD WORK SESSION
The board moved immediately into a WSSDA Board Bootcamp work session. No action was taken during
the work session.
RECONVENE REGULAR BOARD MEETING
Board President Steve Corwin welcomed everyone to the regular board meeting at 6:00 pm.
Board Director’s Marty Gray and Jarrod Karuza joined the regular meeting at 6:00 pm.
Student Board Representative Kennedy Brzozowski and Morgan Adams were present at the regular
meeting.
The pledge of allegiance was recited.
Jarrod Karuza made a motion to amend the agenda to add discussion and possible action to opposition
of HB 5462 and ESHB 2331 as item G under new business. Jana Haugen seconded the motion. The
motion passed 5-0.
A motion to adopt the agenda was made by Jana Haugen and seconded by Marty Gray. The motion
passed 5-0.
CONSENT AGENDA
A. Approval of Meeting Minutes
B. Accounts Payable
C. Personnel Report
D. Whatcom County Sheriff's Office Sub-Station Lease
E. Student Travel
A motion to adopt the consent agenda was made by Jarrod Karuza and seconded by Jana Haugen. The
motion passed 5-0.
REPORTS
Public Relations Update
Director of Public Relations, Lisa Gilchrist presented a Public Relations Update to the Board. Lisa shared
the school district communications goals, updates that are coming to the systems we use, other areas of
work, and professional development engagement.
Curriculum Report
Director of Teaching and Learning, Adrienne Somera presented a Curriculum Report to the Board.
Adrienne shared information about the selection and adoption of instructional materials, Washington

State Learning Standards, current courses that are in the review process, as well as details about recently
adopted core materials.
PUBLIC COMMENT
There was no public comment.
FINANCE REPORT
Finance Report
Director of Finance, Daniel Yorton reported to the Board on our enrollment projections, budget status
report, and budget projections.
OLD BUSINESS

Second Review and Approval: Facilities Task Force Recommendation

Dr. Everett presented the Facilities Task Force Recommendation for second review and approval. A motion
to approve the Facilities Task Force Recommendation as presented was made by Jana Haugen and
seconded by Michelle Simmons. The motion passed 5-0
NEW BUSINESS

First Reading: Policy and Procedure 5271 – Reporting Improper Governmental Action

Assistant Superintendent Kurt Harvill presented Policy and Procedure 5271 – Reporting Improper
Governmental Action to the Board for a first review. This will be brought back before the Board for
second review and approval at the March 6, 2024, Regular Board meeting.
First Reading: Policy and Procedure 5282 - Civility

Assistant Superintendent Kurt Harvill presented Policy and Procedure 5282 - Civility to the Board for a
first review. This will be brought back before the Board for second review and approval at the March 6,
2024, Regular Board meeting.
First Reading: Policy 6111 - Tuition

Assistant Superintendent Kurt Harvill presented Policy 6111 - Tuition to the Board for a first review. This
will be brought back before the Board for second review and approval at the March 6, 2024, Regular
Board meeting.
First Review: 2024-2027 School Year Calendars
Dr. Everett presented the 2024-2027 School Year Calendars to the Board for a first review. This will be

brought back before the Board for second review and approval at the March 6, 2024, Regular Board
meeting.
Resolution 01-24: OSPI 180 Day Waiver

Dr. Everett presented Resolution 01-24: OSPI 180 Day Waiver to the Board for a first review. This will be
brought back before the Board for second review and approval at the March 6, 2024, Regular Board
meeting.
Partnership Opportunity with UW

Dr. Everett shared details about a Partnership Opportunity with University of Washington, Amplify Learn:
A National Research and Development Initiative of Leveraging Generative AI to Enhance Mathematics
Instruction.
Opposition of HB 5462 and ESHB 2331
The Board Directors engaged in a discussion regarding opposition to HB 5462 and ESHB 2331. The board
decided to seek additional information from local legislators. A Special Session Meeting was scheduled for
Friday, February 23, 2024 at 3:30 pm to and continue the discussion and possibly move forward with
action.

SUPERINTENDENT’S REPORT

INFORMATION FROM STUDENT BOARD REPRESENTATIVES
Student Representative Kennedy and Morgan shared excitement about being engaged in the upcoming
principal interview process. Kennedy and Morgan also spoke about the items that were recently discussed
during the Superintendent Advisory Council meetings.
INFORMATION FROM BOARD DIRECTORS
No information was shared.
BOARD SIGNATURE OF OFFICIAL DOCUMENTS
ADJOURNMENT
There being no further business, the Board meeting was adjourned at 7:51 pm. by Board President Steve
Corwin.
