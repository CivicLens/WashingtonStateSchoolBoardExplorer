Leslie Olson, Principal at Post Middle School, and students Rachel Breitenstein and Chloe Draper shared with the Board about why the arts and electives are important to students at Post Middle School. Principal Olson answered questions from the Board.
  B. Teacher and Educator Appreciation Week
Superintendent Sweeting asked all teachers and educators in attendance at the meeting to come to the front of the board room. She expressed her gratitude to the group and shared that this idea of celebrating educators began 70 years ago by First Lady Eleanor Roosevelt. Cabinet delivered Famous Amos cookies to each educator last week as a small token of their appreciation. Chrys stated that she appreciates all that our district educators do and that they are simply amazing to her. You are dedicated, passionate and committed to engaging all students in high levels of learning while at the same time tnuturing their well being. Thank you for taking on this calling. Jason Klein, AEA President, expressed his thanks for the kind words of acknowledgement. He commented that it's great to work for this community and district. Eric DeJong, Executive Director of Human Resources, shared his thankfulness for their service as well. A presentation was shown in their honor.

  New Business
A. Report of Donations Approved by Superintendent - April 2024
Superintendent Sweeting reported that in April she approved three donations from Pioneer PTSA to Pioneer Elementary. The first in the amount of $2,478.92 for 6 iPads and 6 3-Year AppleCare Plans for use in the library, a second donation in the amount of $1,378.44 to purchase a projector for use during assemblies, staff meetings and events, and a third donation in the amount of $1,807.35 to purchase vinyl-wrapped bulletin boards. She also approved two donations from 3on3x to the Arlington High School Boys Basketball Program. One in the amount of $1,500.00 for miscellaneous equipment as needed or to go towards summer uniforms and the second in the amount of $1,250.00 for miscellaneous needs, summer program needs and general items as needed.
  B. Annual Health Room Update
Krissa Cramer, District Nurse, presented an annual health room update that included the volume and type of health related services that are provided to our students district-wide on a daily basis and the complexity of this work, along with the number of students with both significant and chronic health conditions continues to grow with our health rooms running at full capacity. She thanked all of the nurses and health room assistants in the district in recognition of National School Nurses Day that was observed on May 8th. Dr. Sweeting expressed her appreciation for Krissa and her fantastic group of nurses that support our students' health and well-being so that they can learn.

C. Policy 3246 Restraint, Isolation, and Other Uses of Reasonable Force Annual Report
Dave McKellar, Director of Special Education, reported to the Board on Policy 3246 Restraint, Isolation, and Other Uses of Reasonable Force, providing data on the number incidents involving the use of force. Dave answered questions from the Board.
