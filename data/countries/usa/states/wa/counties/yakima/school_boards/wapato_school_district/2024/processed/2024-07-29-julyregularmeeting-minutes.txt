3. PUBLIC HEARING
2024-25 District Budget and 4-Year Plan: Sylvia Bazan, Director of Fiscal Services presented the 2024-25
District Budget and 4-Year Plan and explained the purpose of the public hearing to review as required by
OSPI and legislative action. She explained in detail the proposed budget expenditures and revenues for
each fund category: General, Capital Projects, Debt Service, ASB, and Transportation. She presented the
Enrollment Projects including, kindergarten, transitional kindergarten, and alternative learning student
enrollment projection, the projected fund balance and the General Fund 4-year budget plan. The Board
engaged in discussion regarding the presented information.
Public Comment: Elese Washines commented she was present during this report on behalf of Tribal
Council.
4. Reports
Superintendent's Report: Kelly Garza, Superintendent, reported:
• RCW 42.30.030 has been revised to require school board meeting to now be recorded. Mr. Garza
noted the District has implemented the practice and is now recording all open to the public-school
board meetings.
• End of summer school was last week. There will be more information about this years program at a
future board meeting.
• The District will be participating in the National Night Out event on August 5 at the Wapato
Community Park.
Construction Update: Kevin Hill, Executive Director, reported the District received 10 different group
applications for the Construction/Architect team. Those have been reviewed and narrowed down to 5.
They will be interviewed beginning tomorrow and over the next 2 days. He listed the components of the
interviews and clarified the goal is not pick the lowest bid but to select for a team that will meet the
needs and goals for this project. The goal is to narrow the candidate teams down to 2-3.
The WHS well project continues to make progress and there is an estimated deadline to be completed
within the next 3 months.
Yakama Tribal Council Update: Elese Washines, Tribal Council Liaison, reported on August 8th there will
be a backpack giveaway at the Yakama Nation Cultural Center for all native American students. She
noted that all of WSD Board members have completed Tribal Consultation training as required by law.
