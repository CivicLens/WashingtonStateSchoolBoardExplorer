Public Content
Land acknowledgement and commitment to native students and families:
The board of directors of Lake Washington School District acknowledges that our district is on the
Indigenous Land of the Coast Salish Peoples, who have reserved treaty rights on this land, including the
Duwamish (dxdwab) and the Snoqualmie Indian Tribe (sdukalbix). We thank these caretakers of this land
who have lived here since time immemorial. We commit to continue collaborating with our native
community, to teach the indigenous history of this land and these local tribes to all children of the district.
We also commit to provide the best possible education for all children of native descent who attend our
schools.

SITUATION:
Educational specifications represent the basic outline from which an architect team works to design a
building. In the case of the Norman Rockwell Elementary School, the educational specifications are
composed of the elementary school template developed for the 2016 bond program and the specific
square footage requirements throughout all the 2016 Bond program elementary school buildings. These
specifications are the result of the comprehensive process the district followed to obtain input and
information from principals, teachers and classified staff.
The District has applied to the Office of the Superintendent for Public Instruction for State Construction
Funding Assistance Program (SCAP) dollars for the projects to replace Norman Rockwell Elementary
School. WAC 392-342-015 requires approval by the Board of Directors of the educational specifications
for this project. Resolution No. 2385 and the educational specifications are attached.
RECOMMENDATION:
The Board of Directors approves Resolution No. 2385approving the educational specifications for the
replacement of Norman Rockwell Elementary School.
