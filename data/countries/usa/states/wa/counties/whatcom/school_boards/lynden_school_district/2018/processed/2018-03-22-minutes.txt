8. Board Work Session
• Elementary School Improvement Plans (SIP)
The Elementary Principals shared information about the work they are doing with staff to improve student
performance. Each school summarized how they are focusing on closing the achievement gap, providing
time and support necessary for students, focusing on instruction, and implementing Professional Learning
Communities. The Board and Elementary Principals reviewed data for each school’s demographic
breakdown of student population.
Fisher Elementary Principal, Courtney Ross presented on - Closing the Achievement Gap. Courtney shared
how assessment of social, emotional, learning skills & interventions, as well as motor skills are important
tools to identify the needs of student. Courtney spoke about how family influence and involvement in school
programs make a difference.
Lynden Academy Principal, Scott VandenBerg spoke about - Ensuring that Students Who Need More Time
and Support Get the Assistance. Scott shared data information and places to identify student needs based
on school assessment and home assessment that is shared to create intervention and respond to unique
learners.
Isom Elementary Principal, Patrick McClure responded to How is Your School’s Focus on Instruction? Patrick
expressed how impactful it is to observe each other in classroom instruction and the importance of
collaborative professional development between colleagues. Patrick spoke to the growth process with the
evaluation structure of the 5D Evaluations and the Professional Learning Community (PLC) support.
Bernice Vossbeck Principal, Becky Midboe provided information on – How is Your School Working to
Implement Professional Learning Communities’ Structure to Ensure a Guaranteed and Viable Curriculum?
Becky spoke about alignment between the elementary schools for curriculum and resources to address the
gaps in achievement. Becky shared the elementary principals desire to align assessments and how to
evaluate the data.
The Board and principals discussed challenges in resources and options of school interventions. The Board
thanked the elementary principals for their input on the School Improvement Plan process.
