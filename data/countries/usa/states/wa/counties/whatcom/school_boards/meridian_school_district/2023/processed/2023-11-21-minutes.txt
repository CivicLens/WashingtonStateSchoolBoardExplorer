MERIDIAN SCHOOL DISTRICT #505
REGULAR SCHOOL BOARD MEETING
MEETING HELD IN PERSON & VIA ZOOM
November 21, 2023

REGULAR MEETING
The regular meeting was called to order by Board Chair Sara Bernardy at 6:00 p.m.
The pledge of allegiance was recited.
Board Chair Sara Bernardy welcomed everyone that was in attendance.
Board members present at the meeting were Sara Bernardy, Craig Mitchell, Marty Gray and Jarrod Karuza.
Steve Corwin was excused from attending.
Student Board Representatives Morgan Adams and Kennedy Brzozowski were present at the meeting.
A motion to adopt the agenda was made by Marty Gray and seconded by Jarrod Karuza. The motion
passed 4-0.
CONSENT AGENDA
A. Approval of Meeting Minutes
B. Accounts Payable
C. Personnel Report
D. Approval of Superintendent Contract Extension
A motion to adopt the consent agenda was made by Craig Mitchell and seconded by Marty Gray. The
motion passed 4-0.
FINANCE REPORT
Finance Report
Director of Finance, Daniel Yorton reported to the Board on our enrollment projections, budget status
and budget projections.
PRESENTATIONS
Review of District Technology Plan
Director of Technology, Joe O’Brien gave a presentation on the district technology review to the Board.
Joe’s presentation included an update and review of the current EdTech Levy as well as a plan for a
proposed 2025-28 replacement levy.
PUBLIC COMMENT
There was no public comment.
OLD BUSINESS
Second Review and Approval: NWESD Behavior Specialist Contract
The NWESD Behavior Specialist Contract was presented for a second review and approval. Student
Representatives favor approval. A motion to approve the NWESD Behavior Specialist Contract as
presented was made by Jarrod Karuza and seconded by Craig Mitchell. The motion passed 4-0.

Second Review and Approval: 2024 EP&O Levy Resolution 08-23
The 2024 replacement EP&O Levy Resolution 08-23 and explanatory statement was presented to the
Board for second review and approval. Student Representatives favor approval. A motion to approve the
2024 replacement EP&O Levy Resolution 08-23 and explanatory statement as presented was made by
Craig Mitchell and seconded by Marty Gray. The motion passed 4-0.

Second Review and Approval: 2024 Capital Levy Resolution 09-23
The 2024 replacement Capital Levy Resolution 09-23 and explanatory statement was presented to the
Board for second review and approval. Student Representatives favor approval. A motion to approve the
2024 replacement Capital Levy Resolution 09-23 and explanatory statement as presented was made by
Jarrod Karuza and seconded by Marty Gray. The motion passed 4-0.

NEW BUSINESS
First Review: Youth Care Services Contract

Dr. Everett presented a Youth Care Service Contract for a first review. This will be brought back for a
second review and approval at the December 13 Board meeting.

SUPERINTENDENT’S REPORT
Dr. Everett began his report by sharing information about his recent travels to the WASA Milliken
Leadership Academy in Spartanburg, SC. Dr. Everett also spoke about the MSD partnership with the Gates
Foundation and the four-year Limitless grant. Dr. Everett spoke about the two days of learning that he
and the School Board of Directors recently engaged in at the WSSDA conference as well as upcoming
Board Director training opportunities.
INFORMATION FROM STUDENT BOARD REPRESENTATIVES
Student Representative Kennedy spoke about the superintendent advisory council meetings.
INFORMATION FROM BOARD MEMBERS
Marty spoke about upcoming basketball games. Sara mentioned that bowling is starting too.
BOARD SIGNATURE OF OFFICIAL DOCUMENTS
ADJOURNMENT
There being no further business, the Board meeting was adjourned at 7:10 pm. by Board Chair Sara
Bernardy.
