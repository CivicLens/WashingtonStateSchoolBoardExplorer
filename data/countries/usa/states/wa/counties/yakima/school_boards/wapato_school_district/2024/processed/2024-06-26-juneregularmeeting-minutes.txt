4. Reports
Superintendent's Report: Kelly Garza, Superintendent, reported:
• The Equitable Continuous Growth Plan is up for board approval. He explained the details of the
recommendation; the districts process for gathering input and data to revise and determine the
District’s 4 areas of focus for the next 4 years.
• Summer School is schedule to start on Monday. There are 425 students are registered to attend
summer school and Ken Olden will be the director to oversee the program.
• The District is recommending the review and Board action to purchase handheld radio
infrastructure. Mr. Garza explained the purpose of the recommendation and goal to update the
District current communication to meet the needs via radio communication. Tavis Peterson
explained updating the infrastructure and purchasing new radios will allow efficient communication
throughout the district between schools, transportation, drivers on bus routes and SRO Miranda.
Mr. Peterson said this project will be ESSER funded and explained be towers installed throughout
areas to assure clear communication. He provided details of the work, installation and
implementation of this project. The Board engaged in discussion.
• The District received a letter form OSPI and WA State Patrol notifying the passing of fleet inspection.
Mr. Garza read the letter to the Board and congratulated the Transportation department for 2 years
of exemplary inspections and outstanding work in the safety of transporting students to and from
school. Mr. Garza commented the District has received 3 new 84 passenger busses with A/C and
drivers are excited to transport students in the new buses.
• Congratulations to PACE HS for closing the achievement gap. Mr. Garza attributes this to great staff
and the improved graduation rates over the last few years.

Construction Update: Kevin Hill, Executive Director, reported he met with ASPECT Engineering and
representatives from Yakama Nation to collaborate a discuss what can be done to get a new well for
Wapato High School. He said the meeting was positive and the district received a letter of
recommendation to move forward with next step and get authorization to drill for a well. This letter will
be presented to Yakama Tribal Council for review and approval. Mr. Hill this meeting was also an
opportunity to partner with ASPECT Engineering to work on new grants to fund the well project. The
possible grants are from the Department of Commerce and the Yakama Nation and he explained the
details of the project and grant funding. He explained this well project would completely replace the
well for the heating and cooling system, a fully engineered well and he explained the specs of the
possible new well. The board engaged in discussion and Kevin explained the next steps are to get
approved by the Tribe to move forward and find a site to drill the new well.
Mr. Hill reported he and Mr. Garza participated in a Q&A session at OAC to interested firms regarding
the Districts construction project. The session was an opportunity for firms to ask the District specific
questions, clarify and gather information and data for potential bids. The focus of the information
distributed was focused on the District needs, wants and expectations for this project. Kevin reported
58 people attended this session with showed a big interest in the project. He explained the next steps
to received RFQ that are due this week. He is expecting 15-20 interested teams and the next step will be
to review the qualifications and narrow down to 3-4 teams, set up interviews, score using a rubric and
select a final team. Mr. Garza opened the opportunity to the Board for a member to participate in the
process to review and selection of the construction/architect team that will work on this project. Mr.
Garza explained the tasked and dates of this work. Sarah Divina volunteered to participate in this
process.
Yakama Tribal Council Update: Elese Washines, Tribal Council Liaison, was not present and did not
