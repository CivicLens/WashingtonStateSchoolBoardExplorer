and CJ Costanti via teleconference. Board member Kelli Kettels was absent and excused. Student Director’s Rubia
Orellana and Leslie Strandgard were absent and excused.

Lynden High School students attended "Mini-Med School" at the Washington State Convention Center
sponsored by and international group of endoscopic surgeons. They learned how surgical procedures are
changing and were given hands-on experience in using surgical instruments as well as tying sutures using
video monitors in the same way surgeons do. Attendees were Hannah Jennings, Ellie
Meyer, Sanjna Bawa, Jeetika Pawar, and Taylor Reams.
Lynden Middle School successfully completed another Strengthening Families Program dual-language in
Spanish and English. Twelve families participated along with amazing support staff. Lynden is the only
school district in Whatcom County and the Northwest who has (1) two programs running in conjunction with
each other dual-language. The goal of the Strengthening Families Program is to help support parents,
caregivers and youth throughout the teenage years with communication, combating peer pressure and
encouraging youth in pursuing their dreams and goals.
Lynden Academy had a "Share Fair" and over 30 students volunteered to participate. Students shared
examples of Science experiments, research projects, art pieces, music, sports displays, or other topics of
interest. Lynden Academy music and choir classes performed during the event.
It was a grand celebration at Bernice Vossbeck Elementary for Mrs. Mary Roebuck's retirement party. We
celebrated over 40 years of her dedicated service to her students and the Lynden School District!
Bernice Vossbeck 4th graders were excited to welcome in presenters to learn about a scientific
presentation on watersheds. After time in the classroom they were able to adventure outside to a mobile
watershed to see how one works!
Fisher's generous PTA has funded a grant for Fisher Extended Day students to attend a field trip to
Woodland Park Zoo. The Extended Day students come to school each morning at 7:30 for an additional
hour of English Language support. this activity will culminate with an animal presentation that will
encompass each student's speaking, reading and writing growth!
Lynden School District recognized NCCTK at the WASA 108 Awards Luncheon for their service and
partnership over the years.
Superintendent read Teacher Appreciation Week Proclamation from Governor Jay Inslee.
The Board recognized the long tenure of announced retirements: Mary Roebuck, Nadine Hoekstra, Bev
Voss-Cyr, Cindy Ross, Sue Murray, and Steve Herrick.

Superintendent
• IT Services
Jim Frey updated the Board on the work being done to upgrade the network system by working with a thirdparty vendor and he also provided information about the possibility of partnering with an organization to
help provide support for IT services.
Teaching and Learning
• Middle School Math M2P Report
Molly Mitchell-Mumma reported that middle school teachers have been part of a grant-funded
professional learning partnership with Western Washington University. Molly presented summaries of
their professional learning and the impact on student learning.
•

Health Committee Update
Elizabeth Hamming provided information about the District Health Committee and their review of the K-5
Botvin Materials, the 9-12 Health binder, and the Social Emotional Learning supplement materials.
Elizabeth shared the materials with the Instructional Materials Committee. The committee will also review
HIV-AIDS videos in the near future.

7. Board Workshop
• School Board Committee Updates
The Board of Directors gave an update on the School Board committees: District Planning – Steve Jilk
provided information on District Planning; Communications – CJ Costanti provided information on
Communications; Finance – Brian Johnson provided information on Finance; Superintendent Evaluation –
Brian Johnson provided information on Superintendent Evaluation; Curriculum and Professional
Development - Steve Jilk provided information on Curriculum and Professional Development.
•

School Safety
Jim Frey provided priorities for improving safety and security in schools. The Board agreed that safety and
security was a common priority expressed at both student and staff Linkage Activities. Information was given
about future upgrades to older schools with up-to-date safety feature infrastructures. Jim and David
VanderYacht reported that work continues with local first responders to improve coordination and response
to emergencies. The Board discussed the possibility of adding a School Resource Officer (SRO) or added
security as is now in place at the high school.
