RECOGNITION
School Board Recognition
Dr. James Everett shared appreciation from our district buildings for the work and guidance provided by
our MSD Board of Directors and read the Governor's Proclamation. Dr. Everett shared appreciation for
the volume of time all the Board members give to support our schools. The Board members were
presented cards that were made by our students, a bouquet of flowers and a wooden boxed pen.
Meridian Middle School Staff Recognition
Meridian Middle School Principal, Todd Torgeson recognized the Guiding Coalition and Counseling
department teams at Meridian Middle School. The Guiding Coalition consists of a group of teacher leaders
that meet bi-weekly at 7:15 AM to guide the middle school departments in their PLC work. Mr. Torgeson
expressed appreciation for the Counseling departments willingness to adopt several new programs and
shared the importance of kid’s having a caring voice and face they can go to. Mr. Torgeson shared that
both teams are instrumental in guiding students at Meridian Middle School.
REPORTS
PLC Presentations
School Data
Adrienne Somera, Director of Teaching and Learning presented an overview of our PLC assessment data,
the diverse types of data and variety of methods of data collection used. PLC teams will be sharing work
that is in progress and where they are today.
Irene Reither Elementary School
Fourth grade teachers Lisa Buckley, Stephanie Hamilton, and Christian Deranek presented the
importance of determining the main idea in text and the ability to summarize text. The team shared how
students receive the text, a sample assignment, and the common rubric used to grade student work.
Strategies used to assist student’s progress were also presented.
Meridian Middle School
Teachers Marcus Hollan, Kristin Garlatz, and Jacob Dennis presented the techniques that are being
utilized to teach students how to write an argumentative paragraph about a social studies related topic
where the student creates a claim and defends the claim with evidence. The team presented grade level
rubric and student growth data from October 2022 to December 14, 2022. The team also shared
techniques used to assist students that have not yet mastered the skill.
Meridian High School
Teachers Maria Nichols and Tammy Scott from the CTE department at Meridian High School presented
the CTE approach to teaching workplace expectations. Attendance, safety, and technology were focused
skills discussed. The team shared common formative assessments and the rubrics used to measure
progress. The team also shared initial and follow up data and discussed action steps taken to assist
student progress.
