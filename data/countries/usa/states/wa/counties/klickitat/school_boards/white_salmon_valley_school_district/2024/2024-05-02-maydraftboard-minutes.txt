The regular meeting of the Board of Education, White Salmon Valley School District, Klickitat and Skamania Counties, White
Salmon, WA was called to order at 7:01 p.m. Thursday, May 23, 2024, at the WSVSD Board Room, Park Center Building, located
at 170 NW Washington St., White Salmon, WA 98672, and via a ZOOM public meeting. Present: Board Chair Laurie Stanton,
Vice-chair Peter Harkema, Kris Ostness, Matthew Byrne, Pat Dempsey, Student Representatives, Superintendent Rich Polkinghorn,
and guests.
Board Chair Laurie Stanton led the Board in the flag salute.
Additions/Deletions to Agenda: None.
Matthew Byrne moved to adopt the agenda as presented. Seconded by Pat Dempsey. Carried.
Reports:
a) Superintendent Update: Superintendent Polkinghorn reviewed the budget status report, current fund balance, and
projected ending fund balance with the Board, noting the local revenue received in April. He provided updates on the
Capital Tech Levy for chrome book and laptop refresh cycles and upcoming purchases. Staffing updates for the 20242025 school year were discussed, including additional positions and plans for an online school option. Enrollment trends
for May were also reviewed. Superintendent Polkinghorn recognized the success of recent events including the Art
Walk, Health and Wellness Fair, and drama production, expressing appreciation for staff and community partners.
Superintendent Polkinghorn also recognized the CHS athletes headed to and those who have recently participated in
State events including Boys Soccer, Track and Field, and girls Tennis. Polkinghorn also recognized the CHS MEChA
club and the events that they have recently planned. Lastly Superintendent Polkinghorn gave an overview of upcoming
year end events.
b) Board Member Committee Report: Pat Dempsey shared highlights from the Wellness Fair and discussed various
community groups involved. Matthew Byrne provided a legislative update, including changes in enrollment funding and
new policies resulting from recent legislation. Kris Ostness reported on the Policy and Finance Committee as well as the
Wellness Committee.
c) Student Representatives Report: The Student Representative shared that the National Honor Society recently inducted
new members to the club. FBLA members achieved top placements in events with any qualifying for national
competition. Updates were provided on various student clubs, sports achievements, and upcoming end-of-year events.
Peter Harkema thanked the student representatives for their comprehensive reports.
Recognition and Good News:
Whitson Elementary: Todd McCauley recognized and introduced the K-8 PTO for their contributions, including
fundraising efforts and support for students and teachers. The Board applauded their dedication.
Public Comments: No public comment was received.
