OPENING ITEMS
Meeting Date and Location:
August 14, 2019 - 1306 Dupont St., Bellingham, Washington
Call to Order/Roll Call:
Ms. Diaz Hackler called the meeting to order at 6:00 p.m. Present: Directors Kelly
Bashaw, Camille Diaz Hackler (President), Douglas Benjamin, Jenn Mason,
Superintendent Greg Baker, and Sheri O’Day (recorder). Director Quenby Peterson was
unable to attend the meeting. Ms. Diaz Hackler acknowledged that this meeting is being
held on the ancestral homelands of Indigenous Peoples, in particular the Lummi Nation
and the Nooksack tribe.
Audience:
No public testimony was offered.
Public Hearing:
Ms. Diaz Hackler explained the purpose of the public hearing: to provide an opportunity
for anyone who would like to speak for or against the adoption of the 2019-20 budget to
comment before the board takes action on the budget later this evening. No audience
presented, and no public testimony was offered relating to the proposed budget adoption.
Superintendent’s Announcements and Report: Summary of Dr. Baker’s comments:
There are seven doctoral residents working with us this year. Two of them, Julie
VanWijk and Debora Haney, are here this evening to observe the meeting.
Today marks two weeks until the first day of school for students, and we are now gearing
up with staff professional development, orientations, etc. Principals, deans and teachers
on special assignment (TOSAs) spent time together today talking about and planning for
their professional learning for the year.
The all-staff kickoff event will be held at Sehome High School on Tuesday, August 27.
Our food services staff will be preparing breakfast and refreshments beginning at 7 a.m.
Tonight’s consent agenda contains the approval of the 2019-20 school year budget. Dr.
Baker thanked the finance and communications teams, as well as others, for their work in
preparing the budget documents.
On July 10, 2019, the board approved the sale of a parcel of unused, surplus real estate
located on East Sunset Drive. On tonight’s consent agenda are the proposed sales of two
additional parcels of surplus real estate, as well as the purchase of property on Barkley
Boulevard. Dr. Baker thanked Executive Director of Capital Projects and School
Facilities Ron Cowan for his efforts to sell the three properties and purchase the new site
for the district office.

Design work for the new Sunnyland Elementary building will begin this year. It appears
that not as much of the neighboring City of Bellingham park will be needed as was
originally anticipated.
Dr. Baker acknowledged Assistant Superintendent of Human Resources Bob Kuehl and
the human resources team for finalizing the Bellingham Association of School
Employees (BASE) and Teamsters labor contracts.
A brief special meeting is being scheduled for August 22, 2019 to approve this evening’s
meeting minutes and an administrative policy. Arrangements are being made for school
board directors to phone in for that meeting.
Dr. Baker acknowledged that Ron Cowan will be retiring at the end of August and
encouraged the board to wish him the best in his retirement.

POLICY GOVERNANCE REVIEW
Revision to Ends 1 and Ends 3 Policies
After review, a motion was made by Mr. Benjamin to approve the updated Ends 1 and
Ends 3 policies as presented. Unanimous affirmative vote.
Addition of Race and Equity Policy (EL-10)
The board discussed next steps in connection with this proposed new policy, including
the presentation of a monitoring report in October and further discussion at the February
retreat. The Communications and Community Relations team is currently working on a
communications plan regarding EL-10. A motion was made by Ms. Mason to adopt EL10, Equity and Race, as presented. Unanimous affirmative vote.
GP-7 Agenda Planning
There was discussion regarding item 2(c) of GP-7 (board governance training and
discussion). Potentially one of the board’s special dinner meetings or study sessions each
year could be focused on policy governance training. No changes or action is needed at
this time.
GP-7.1 Board Self-Assessment
As the result of discussion at the July 24, 2019 special meeting/retreat, a minor change
was made to GP-7.1. A motion was made by Mr. Benjamin to approve the change.
Unanimous affirmative vote.
GP-7.2 Annual Board Agenda
After discussion regarding the addition of monitoring dates for newly adopted policy EL10, Race and Equity, Ms. Mason made a motion to approve GP-7.2 as presented.
Unanimous affirmative vote.
