REGULAR AGENDA ITEMS
School Board Directors’ Reports
Dr. Steve Smith attended open houses at Geneva Elementary and Fairhaven Middle School. He
attended the League of Women Voters Talk where Dr. Baker was the guest speaker.
Dr. Ken Gass attended Kulshan Middle School’s Open House and was impressed with teachers’
use of online curriculum and textbook connections. Dr. Gass is also receiving updates from
Secretary of Education, Arne Duncan, on No Child Left Behind (NCLB).
Dr. Scott Stockburger attended Fairhaven Middle School’s Open House. He reported a large
turnout and noticed more teachers are putting their classroom materials on their websites. Dr.
Stockburger also sent a letter to each school’s PTA representative inviting them to attend the
Board linkage session at 7:00 p.m. on October 6, 2011. The Board will meet with parent
organization representatives and listen to their perspectives on various topics.
Director Kelly Bashaw attended a breakfast meeting with Dr. Baker and Ann Whitmyer. She also
attended open houses at Options High School and Whatcom Middle School. Ms. Bashaw
attended the League of Women Voters Talk.
President Ann Whitmyer attended a breakfast meeting with Dr. Baker and Kelly Bashaw. She
also attended Squalicum High School’s Back-to-School event where there was a lot of school
spirit and energy. She attended open houses at Sunnyland and Lowell Elementary Schools. A
Sunnyland teacher was enthusiastic about the summer pre-school program. The kids who
attended the program were better prepared to start kindergarten. The Lowell open house was a
more formal adult/parent event.
Whitmyer reported having a conversation with Monte Bridges regarding policy governance
before the workshop on Saturday, September 24.
Superintendent’s Announcements and Report
Dr. Baker was a panelist at the Superintendents’ Summit in Phoenix, Arizona on September 14.
One of the focuses of the summit was on physical activity, health and wellness for students and
staff. One discussion was around snacks for students who may go for several hours without food
from breakfast to lunch. Another discussion focused on struggling seniors in Clark County
Nevada, one of the largest school districts in the country. The superintendents discussed

2011–12 Enrollment Report
Ron Cowan, assistant superintendent of Business and Operations, reported on the enrollment
based on the OSPI September 12, 2011 official count day. The total headcount for the District
was 10,710, an increase of 38 students over last year’s headcount of 10,672. Enrollment at the
elementary schools increased by 65 students; middle school enrollment increased by 6 students;
and high school enrollment decreased by 33 students. State funding is based on the average of
the nine monthly enrollment counts (Actual Annual Average FTE–AAAFTE). Cowan reported
that the estimated 2011–2012 AAAFTE count will be approximately 74 less than the budgeted
AAAFTE. Cowan responded to questions from the Board. He reported on changes in funding
for Running Start and Alternative Learning Education.

POLICY GOVERNANCE REVIEW
Monitoring Report for EL-6, Staff Evaluation
Dr. Baker presented the Monitoring Report on EL-6, Staff Evaluation, which states in broad
terms that:
With respect to evaluation of employees, the Superintendent shall not cause or
allow an evaluation system that does not measure employee performance in terms
of achieving the Board’s Ends policies and complying with the Board’s Executive
Limitations policies.
