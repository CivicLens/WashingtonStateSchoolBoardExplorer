School Board Directors’ Reports
Director Ken Gass met with Dr. Baker on August 16 to discuss the superintendent evaluation
process.
Superintendent’s Announcements and Report
Dr. Greg Baker updated the Board on the theft at Cordata Elementary School that occurred
during the night of August 23, 2011. Laptop computers and two desk computers were stolen. The
District is working with its insurance company and will ensure that Cordata students and staff
have the equipment they need for a smooth start to the school year. The district is increasing
security at Cordata and other schools. Superintendent Baker was at Cordata Aug 24 and staff
were there focused on teaching and learning in preparation for the start of school. Cordata will
hold a ribbon-cutting and open house on September 8 at 5 p.m.

Whatcom Middle School will host a ribbon-cutting and open house on September 1, 2011 at
noon. Staff are moving back into the school in preparation for school.
Dr. Baker noted that the district will be receiving state assessment data. The state will release
this data to the public on August 30.
Many teachers are involved in professional development. Dr. Baker observed a literacy
conference at Western Washington University. Other teachers have participated in a middle
school English Language Learners (ELL) professional development. Additional professional
development is scheduled for next week for middle school Response to Intervention (RtI)
training and K-5 literacy.
Superintendent Baker invited the Board to participate in the All Staff Back-to-School Breakfast
on September 2 at Bellingham High School at 7 a.m.
The District is continuing talks with Bellingham Education Association (BEA) on the contract. A
number of districts throughout the state have settled contracts with teachers.
Dr. Baker noted one of the consent items was a Memorandum of Understanding with the
Bellingham Public School Foundation. He asked Foundation Executive Director Gennie
Clawson for comments. Ms. Clawson thanked the Board for its approval and expressed
excitement about the partnership with the District. The Foundation and the District share the
same vision and the Foundation will work hard to support the Board’s initiatives. Ms. Clawson
also noted a 5K Race for Education on October 8 at Civic Field will be hosted jointly by the
Foundation and Communities in Schools.
