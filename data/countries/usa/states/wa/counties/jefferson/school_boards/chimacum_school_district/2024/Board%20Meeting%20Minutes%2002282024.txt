superintendent/Management Reports
School Based Health Center Report
Dr. Melinda Bower updated the board on the SBHC in Jr./Sr. High School. Bower presented data on the
different services they provide at the center, when it was founded, and how it is funded. Bower also
highlighted data on types of services they provide and how many students they serve from each school
district. Bower explained the importance of the health center to the schools as well as the community
and thanked the district for allowing them to have the center at the school grounds.
Athletic Report
Athletics Director Shelby Box reported on Winter sports presented the number of students that signed
up for each winter sport. Box highlighted the students’ performance in basketball and wrestling.
Robotics and Outdoor Education Program Update
CES Principal Leigh Schwartz updated the board on Robotics, Outdoor Education and Camp Cispus.
He highlighted the students are excited about the Robotics program, showing up early in the morning
before school started to participate in the training sessions. The Program consisted of many hands-on
group activities and competitions.

Our sixth-grade students will be attending Camp Cispus this year. This is a student and teacher favorite
where students get to take part in group outdoor learning activities. They learn to share living spaces as
they all get to share bunk beds with each other and help in the kitchen cleaning up after meals.
SIP Presentations
CCP Principal Sarah Walker accompanied by Reading Specialist Miceala Riley presented School
Improvement Plans. Walker explained the goals for the Primary school to be Community, Collaboration
and Commitment to Foundational Skills. Walker highlighted TK data growth in language, physical and
social emotional categories showed early learning successes. She explained the next steps are to
increase academic rigor in math and literacy for growth.
Micheala Riley presented on the reading improvements the students have been making with small
groups and individual support. They have been recognizing what type of support the students need and
have been able to produce a plan that will maximize the support the student’s need.

CES Principal Leigh Schwartz accompanied by Interventionalists Natalie Fortier and Shari Glessing
presented School Improvement Plans. Fortier and Glessing explained the importance of the intervention
programs. They serve over one hundred students and have been seeing a significant improvement in the
students. They explained they use MAP data to decide the direction of instruction.
Principal Leigh Schwartz presented and compared attendance data, discipline data, math academic data,
and ELA academic data from 2023 and 2024. He explained their big picture is to have a deeper
understanding of the needs of students and provide support for the student success.
Student Director Report
Student Director Reid Martin presented Student director report. Martin highlighted winter sports and
reminded everyone spring sports begin on the 26th of February. He was excited to report on the
intersessions and went on to explain intersession offered an opportunity for all the high school students
to raise their grades. He expressed his excitement about scholarship notebooks and commented it
would be helpful to start earlier to give them enough time to gather all the information they need.
Martin explained he would like to see more information and focus on preparing seniors to graduate.
DES Agreement
This interlocal agreement with DES provides CSD with project management services and access to
Energy Services Companies (ESCO’s). ESCO would help CSD find energy grants, write applications, and
contract projects. ESCO is limited to energy saving projects. This is another team to help CSD improve
schools.
Director Kristina Mayer asked for a motion to approve the agreement.
Director Tami Robocker made a motion to approve the agreement.
Director Kathryn Lamka seconded.
Motion carried (3,0).
