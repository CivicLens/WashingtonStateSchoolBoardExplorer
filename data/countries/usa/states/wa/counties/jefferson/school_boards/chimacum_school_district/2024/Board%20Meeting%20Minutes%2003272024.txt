Superintendent/Management Reports
Quarterly Financial Report
Assistant Superintendent Art Clarke presented the fiscal update for 2023-24 and how it will impact
2024-25 school year. He pointed out the major changes such as enrollment decline which will impact
negatively on all revenues, discontinuation of ESSER funds, and state legislature funding enhancements.
Clarke highlighted projected enrollment for 2023-24 and the breakdown for the last 10 years as well as
apportionment of the funds.
Clarke presented the financial report for the end of second quarter 2023-24 school year. He compared
year-to-date actuals. He highlighted compared to the last year during the same period total revenues
are higher $199,577, total expenditures are higher $440,614, total fund balance is lower $270,158.
Clarke gave a detailed General Fund Summary including revenues, expenditures, and surplus. He also
presented the 2023-24 Capital Project fund review; he explained due to the grants we received we must
revise the Capital Projects budget and will bring it to the board for approval by late May.
Student Director Report
Student Directors, Reid Martin and Isaac Johnson presented their report, highlighting the running start
program, where six of Chimacum students will be receiving their Associated Degree from Peninsula
College before they graduate from High School.
They showed enthusiasm of the League of Women Voters essay competition and Winter sports. They
commented there are many track athletes ranking in the top ten in State competitions now.
Martin was excited to share about the ASB activities, where he mentioned they raised almost $26,000 at
the ASB auction. The ASB is in full steam now planning events and collaborating with the PT ASB for a
possible joint homecoming next year.
