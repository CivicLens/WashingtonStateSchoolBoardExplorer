1. Call to Order, Welcome, Pledge of Allegiance and Roll Call
Meeting called to order at 6:31 P.M. Board members in attendance were Steve Jilk, Brian Johnson, CJ Costanti,
Kelli Kettels, and David Vis. Student Directors Ruby VanderHaak and Riley Anderson were absent and excused.
2. Good News
 Jim Frey introduced new staff personnel: Michelle Nilsen, current LHS Assistant Principal as the new
Principal at Bernice Vossbeck Elementary; Rex Frasier, current LMS Dean of Students as the new
Assistant Principal at LHS; David Kish joining LHS as Assistant Principal; Rebecca Morgan joining the
District as Special Education Director. The Board congratulated and welcomed them and looks forward to
their work in the Lynden School District.
 The Board of Directors thanked the Information Technology team for their diligent work in the past
months and for the recent addition of more “hot spots” to help connect families to on-line learning.

None at this meeting.
6. Reports
 Superintendent
COVID-19/Distance Learning Updates
Each of the building principals updated the Board on methods, both on-line and pick-up and delivery,
utilized by their staff to connect with students to ensure continuous learning and shared the successes
and tribulations, and how good it has been to check on their students’ well-being. Principals shared
information on food service and childcare service taking place in their buildings. The Board shared their
appreciation to principals, teachers and other staff who are connecting with students and families.


Teaching and Learning
None at this meeting.



Finance and Operations
The Board reviewed information on the budget for March containing a summary of financial status,
enrollment, general fund comparative, and revenues and expenditures.

7. Board Workshop
 Bond and Facility Planning
The Board discussed whether to postpone or continue with the planning of a proposed facility bond for
high school improvements and a possible new elementary school to address student and staff needs. Due
to the uncertainty of the current circumstances, it was determined that postponing plans for a capital
facilities bond was the thing to do for now.
8. Action Items
 Resolution No. 05-20 - Emergency Waiver of High School Graduation Credits
The Board reviewed this resolution approving a waiver that assures that good faith efforts will be made
to ensure students meet all graduation requirements set by the state and local school boards, while also
acknowledging that certain circumstances beyond a students’ control may necessitate the waiver of
requirements.
David Vis motioned to approve the Resolution No. 05-20 seconded by Brian Johnson. Motion carried by voice
vote.


Resolution No. 06-20 – Emergency Waiver of School Days and Instructional Hours
The Board reviewed this resolution approving a waiver that outlines what is required for OSPI to waive
the 1,027 hours and 180 days of instruction hours/days in an academic year in the event of unforeseen
emergency events, including epidemics. Jim Frey shared the continuous learning plan that will be
provided to OSPI along with the waiver.
Brian Johnson motioned to approve the Resolution No. 06-20 seconded by David Vis. Motion carried by voice
vote.


Continuous Learning Plan
The Board reviewed the learning plan that will be provided to OSPI for approval of the Emergency Waiver
of School Days and Instructional Hours.
Brian Johnson motioned to approve the Continuous Learning Plan seconded by David Vis. Motion carried by voice
vote.
