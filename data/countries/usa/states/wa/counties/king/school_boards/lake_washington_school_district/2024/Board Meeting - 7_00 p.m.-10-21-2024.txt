Public Content
Land acknowledgement and commitment to native students and families:
The board of directors of Lake Washington School District acknowledges that our district is on the
Indigenous Land of the Coast Salish Peoples, who have reserved treaty rights on this land, including the
Duwamish (dxdwab) and the Snoqualmie Indian Tribe (sdukalbix). We thank these caretakers of this land
who have lived here since time immemorial. We commit to continue collaborating with our native
community, to teach the indigenous history of this land and these local tribes to all children of the district.
We also commit to provide the best possible education for all children of native descent who attend our
schools.

6. Authorization To Proceed With Initial Construction
Norman Rockwell Elementary School Rebuild And
Enlarge (Site 21) Resolution No. 2388

Meeting :

Oct 21, 2024 - Board Meeting - 7:00 p.m.

Type :

Action (Consent)

Recommended Action :

Adopt Resolution No. 2388 authorizing the Superintendent or
his designee to approve contract modifications with Lydig
Construction, Inc. to proceed with construction identified in
Amendment No. 1 for the Norman Rockwell Elementary
School Rebuild and Enlarge project in the amount of
$55,246,755 plus sales tax. Approval of this amendment
results in a final GMP or total construction cost of
$55,246,755 plus sales tax.

Public Content
SITUATION:

On June 5, 2023, the Board of Directors adopted Resolution No. 2354 which approved the use of a
Design-Build (DB) procedure for the Norman Rockwell Elementary School Rebuild and Enlarge project.
RCW 39.10.300 outlines the process to use design build procedures as approved by the District.
In-lieu of a single bid in the traditional design-bid build model, the Design-Builder and District, utilizing target value design have worked to
establish a final Guaranteed Maximum Price (GMP) or total construction cost.
Lydig Construction Inc., is the selected Design-Builder for the Norman Rockwell Elementary School Rebuild and Enlarge project and has
entered into a Design Build Contract with the District. Design-Build procurement methodology allows the district to contract a single entity to
be responsible for the design and construction.

Amendment No. 1 will be incorporated into the Design Build Contract and final GMP. Approval for this Amendment will facilitate maintaining
the schedule for completion of the project. Amendment No. 1 establishes a final GMP of $55,246,775 when combined with the design and
pre-construction services agreement establishes a current contract amount of $61,959,711.
RECOMMENDATION:
The Board of Directors adopt Resolution No. 2388 authorizing the superintendent or his designee to approve contract modifications with
Lydig Construction, Inc. to proceed with construction identified in Amendment No. 1 for the Norman Rockwell Elementary School Rebuild
and Enlarge project in the amount of $55,246,755 plus sales tax. Approval of this amendment results in a final GMP or total construction cost
of $55,246,755 plus sales tax.
