Lynden Middle School leadership students hosted the Middle Level Regional Leadership conference. In
attendance were 150 student leaders from 5 different middle schools in Whatcom and Skagit counties in
the new spacious middle school gym to be inspired and learn from one another about how to encourage
inclusiveness. Students collaborated about how to take steps to create a positive school culture in their
schools while leadership advisers met to share ideas. the theme this year was "Find Your Leadership
Beat".
Lynden High School held a successful Future Freshman Night with over 150 future freshman students
attending. They were able to meet the LHS staff, learn about courses offered, complete registration, take a
school tour, and sign up for clubs and athletics.
The middle school art students will hold the annual art show on May 16th. They are excited that they will be
able to showcase their work in the Art Hall at their new middle school!
We had some great outcomes from the winter sports teams. The boys wrestling team had 7 qualify for the
State Wrestling Tournament with 2 members placing 2nd and 8th. The girls wrestling team also had 1
member place in 7th at the State Wrestling Tournament. The girls basketball team came home with
a 4th place State Basketball Tournament win and the boys basketball team brought home the 1st place
gold ball from the tournament.
The Lynden High School Chess team placed 3rd in the Northwest League and 16th at the State Chess
Tournament. The players fought hard to win games and their dedication to the game was amazing and
recognized.
The Lynden Cheer team placed 3rd in the non-tumbling coed division at the State Cheer Championships!
Lynden and Lynden Christian are continuing their partnership around Early Writing instruction. LC is
hosting David Matteson training in May and Lynden Academy teachers will join them. Next year there are
plans to provide some refresher training to our K-2 teachers, and will invite Lynden Christian teachers.

Surplus Property – Main St. Middle School Ball Fields
The Board reviewed next steps and a timeline for the surplus sale of the old middle school (Main St.)
ball fields. At the July 12th, 2018 meeting the Board gave direction for Jim Frey to surplus the property,
