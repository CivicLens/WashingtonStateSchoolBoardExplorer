Call to Order: School Board President Douglas Benjamin welcomed everyone and
called the meeting to order at 6:00 p.m. Community linkage meetings give the school
board an opportunity to strengthen the connection with our students, and the school board
is excited to hear what families have to say about their experiences. He introduced
Superintendent Greg Baker who welcomed attendees and invited all to introduce
themselves.
Meeting Purpose – Explanation of Community Linkages: Dr. Baker thanked everyone
for taking the time to be here this evening. This linkage will give the school board
information about the Bellingham Family Partnership Program (BFPP). Dr. Baker
introduced Kate Baehr, principal of the BFPP. Ms. Baehr shared background information
about BFPP.
Dr. Baker asked the five table groups to converse and respond to the guiding questions
provided:

The feeling of community came through strongly with each topic discussed.
We’re all here holding each other – staff, students and families.
The building is a part of the BFPP community.
The flexibility of the program is valued and important.
There is a broad range of supports available.
The learning plan partner guidance is valued and appreciated.
Many families enroll at BFPP due to recommendations from other families.
Children and families who may feel a little lost are happy to find this program.
There is a need for a high school program.
BFPP has a strong theater program.
Each family’s story of how they ended up at BFPP is a little different.
Many families feel their kids were in a traditional school that wasn’t working for
them, for various reasons.
There is a partnership between the district’s traditional schools and BFPP, with
many staff in the traditional schools recommending BFPP to families.
The innovation of the classes is appreciated.
Having someone to onboard students and their families is helpful.
Some families want to control the journey of their student’s education.
There is a feeling of love, partnership and community at BFPP.
Many families come here feeling vulnerable.
Families hold each other up through difficult times.
There is shared ownership in kids’ growth.
Kids can find their passion here.
More opportunities would be welcomed.
For homeschool students, BFPP becomes less about the parents as their student’s
teacher and more about time with peers.
It’s a happy place.
Loving adults are resources for one another.
Many families need the flexible schedule provided at BFPP.
Possible improvements would include high school options, more capacity,
additional services and programs and access to specialists.

Dr. Baker thanked the attendees for participating in this evening’s community linkage.
