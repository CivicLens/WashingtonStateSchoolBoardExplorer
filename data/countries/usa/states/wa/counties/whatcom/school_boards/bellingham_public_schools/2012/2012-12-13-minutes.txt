2011–12 Annual Financial Report
Kathryn Weilage, Director of Business Services, reviewed the highlights of the 2011–12 Annual
Financial Report. Enrollment in the K–12 regular program decreased by 32 full-time equivalent
(FTE) students and enrollment in the Running Start Program remained the same, for a total
decrease of 32 FTE. Revenues totaled $101,041,015. Expenditures were $104,088,649. The
percent of expenditures spent on teaching and teaching support was 71.4%; 15.9% was spent on
support services; and 12.7% was spent on administration. Ms. Weilage reported $3,122,659 is
committed to maintain a minimum fund balance of 3%, which aligns with the Board’s policy to
keep the fund balance between three and five percent. Ms. Weilage responded to questions from
the Board.

POLICY GOVERNANCE REVIEW
Executive Limitation 7, Budget Planning, and Executive Limitation 8, Budget Execution
The Board discussed the proposed revisions to Executive Limitations EL-7 and Executive
Limitation EL-8. A draft prepared by Director Steve Smith was presented to the Board for
discussion. Several revisions were requested and a revised Executive Limitation 7 will be
submitted for approval at the January 10, 2013 School Board meeting.

POLICY GOVERNANCE MONITORING REPORT
Monitoring Report for Ends 3.0, Skills and Attributes
Deputy Superintendent Tom Venable and Research, Assessment Director Brian Rick, Program
Administrator for Title I Trina Hall and Program Administrator for English Language Learners
Amy Carder presented a monitoring report on Ends 3.0, Skills and Attributes. Mr. Venable and
Mr. Rick reported on the matrix that was designed to facilitate their ability to collect data, engage
in collaborative analysis, and examine the effectiveness of the instructional programs as they
relate to Ends 3.0. Mr. Rick reported on the data associated with each cell of the matrix. Ms. Hall
reported on the work on implementation of a standard-based reporting system at the elementary
and middle school level. Ms. Carder reported on instructional framework, which is a way to
define what good instruction looks like. Mr. Rick and Mr. Venable responded to questions from
the Board.
The Board thanked the team for the report. Director Smith suggested he would like to hear not
only information on what has happened in the past but what can be done in the future to support
a child’s learning. He also asked for information about the work at the high school level.

NEW BUSINESS
Superintendent Greg Baker asked Assistant Superintendent Ron Cowan to give an update on the
work of the Facilities Planning Task Force. Once the Task Force makes a recommendation to
Superintendent Baker, the information will be shared with the community again. A key focus is
to keep the community informed throughout the process and allow time to continue to receive
feedback. Dr. Baker indicated the Task Force has done a very thorough examination of the
District facilities and we should consider their recommendations very seriously.
