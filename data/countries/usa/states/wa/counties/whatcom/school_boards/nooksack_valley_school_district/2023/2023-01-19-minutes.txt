CALL TO ORDER
The regular meeting of the Nooksack Valley School Board of Directors was called to order by
Chairman, Steve Jones, at 7:00 p.m. on Thursday, January 19, 2023.
MINUTES APPROVED REGULAR MEETING
Mark Olson moved, and Cheryl Thornton seconded the motion to approve the minutes of the
December 15, 2022 regular meeting.
M.C. 5-0
COMMUNICATIONS
•
•
•

•
•

Superintendent Galley acknowledged the Governor’s proclamation to mark January as School
Board Recognition Month and read a statement of gratitude into the record (attached). Each
board member was presented with a certificate of recognition.
Jen Lautenbach also made a public statement of gratitude to the board for their service.
Steve Jones acknowledged the very helpful and professional service of Doreen Vermeulen and
Melissa Phillips to help him answer a question regarding vouchers; and mentioned the positive
story run by the Lynden Tribune about the NVSD and thanked Megan Vigre, Sarah Condreay,
and Matt Galley for their contributions that made the story possible.
John Moon, Habitat for Humanity, shared the low-income housing project that was just
approved in the city of Nooksack. This project will provide 30 housing units and will be
completed in 5-6 years.
Collin Buckley and Jeff Demorest presented work the high school has been doing to connect
school to post-secondary life for students. They shared their 4-year CTE plan, advisory
overhaul, and the “Majors” they are developing to create more relevance for students. They
also shared about their upcoming career fair and the 70+ employers who will be in attendance.

SUPERINTENDENT REPORT
Superintendent Matt Galley gave a brief report on the following:
•
•
•

Redistricting – All paperwork has been submitted to the county auditor and we are awaiting a
final confirmation letter.
Legislative outreach – Cheryl Thornton and Supt Galley will be attending the WASA
Legislative Conference Feb 26, 27 and are scheduled to meet with all three legislators of the 42 nd
District.
Levy Renewal – The current EP&O levy runs through 2024 but must be renewed in the 2024
calendar year. Preparations will begin in May to determine levy amount and begin community
outreach.
