Meeting called to order at 6:32 P.M. Board members in attendance were Brian Johnson, Steve Jilk, Kelli Kettels,
and David Vis. Board Director, CJ Costanti was absent and excused. Student Director Cynthia Torres was present
and Ruby VanderHaak was absent and excused.

Fisher Elementary PTA has sponsored a Chess Club after school. Students are greatly enjoying learning
the game and playing together!
Fisher teachers and para-educators had the great privilege of using staff development time with presenter
Karl Schleich. Karl enhanced professional skills with instruction by creating a thriving positive learning
environment for students, giving tools to prevent behaviors from escalating.
Lynden Academy won an award from Washington Achievement Council for 80% of their eligible students
signing up for the College Bound Scholarship program.
Lynden Academy senior, Alisa Hartley won the Fleet Reserve Association essay contest and is being
awarded with a scholarship!
Bernice Vossbeck Elementary's Peace Patrol program is up and running! The program engages 5th grade
students as mentors to kindergarten and 1st grade students at recess. Peace Patrol students are required
to fill out an application and attend three hours of training with a school counselor. Mentoring activities
include teaching games, helping students develop friendships and solving conflicts. Younger students
aspire to join Peace Patrol when the get to the 5th grade!
The new roof on the LHS 300 & 400 buildings are complete. The bulk of the project was done over
winter break with some tough weather days, but the contractor and many co-workers were on site to the
complete the project on schedule.
As of the first of the year, Lynden High School had 16 students apply for BTC fall enrollment and 22 enroll
at WCC. They are expecting many more and are looking forward to the FASFA night.
The high school First Generation Club held a Multi-Cultural night with over 100 community members in
attendance. They learned about different cultures by way of speeches, books, photos and educational
projects.

7. Board Workshop
• Glenning Street Property Update
Harlan Kredit, community member and spokesman for “Save Glenning Street” updated the Board on the
effort of securing donations and meeting with the City of Lynden to possibly maintain the property as open
park space. Mr. Kredit will send an update to the Lynden School District Board after meeting with City
officials. The Board discussed the need to be prepared with interaction with the City of Lynden for the sale
of the property, or to prepare for a surplus of sale of the property to public market. The Board will come to
a decision at the February 26th meeting.
•

School Improvement Plan (SIP) Discussion
The Board discussed the format of the SIP presentations provided by school principals and the types of
information they would like to review and engage in discussion with the principals at future meetings.

•

Legislative Update
Due to the cancellation of the Legislative Conference, there was not an update to provide. Jim Frey will
communicate with legislatures and provide updates to the Board.

Premera Healthcare requests Board approval to be a provider for the Lynden School District.

Third Addendum of Purchase Sale Agreement (PSA)
The PSA with the City of Lynden for the adjustment of a section of the property line, easement
agreements for access to the 516 Main Street school district property was reviewed by the Board.
The Board agreed that after further review with legal counsel and updates including “Exhibits” within
the contracts, the Board has agreed to approve.
Brian Johnson motioned to approve the Third Addendum seconded by Kelli Kettels. Motion carried by voice vote.

School Calendar 2019-2020
Two 2019-2020 calendar options were provided to the Board for review. The Board has requested that
the superintendent discuss with LEA to consider a change to utilize the mid-winter break as snow
make-up days. Superintendent will bring back at the next meeting.
