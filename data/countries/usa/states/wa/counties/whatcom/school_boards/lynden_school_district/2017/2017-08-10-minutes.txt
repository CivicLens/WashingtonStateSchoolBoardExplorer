4. Good News

Four middle school students applied and received summer scholarships to attend camps at WWU. The
scholarship is the Clayton D. Millage Scholarship, Clay Millage was a science teacher at LMS for 12
years. These current 8th graders received: Abigail Tiesinga - WWU Odyssey of Science and Arts Class
"Survival Science"; Cody Wallace - WWU Odyssey of Science and Arts Class: Robotics 1";
Melissa Brossow - WWU Odyssey of Science and Arts Class "Crafting with Circuits"; Harrison VanDeKop WWU Odyssey of Science and Arts Class "Rocket Science 101". Congratulations to these students!
The high school portable was successfully moved to the middle school. The move allows work to begin on
the new biotechnology facility at the high school with a completion date scheduled for December 2017.
Staff working during the summer months have been very productive in getting ready for the 2017-2018
school year.

8. Reports
• Superintendent
Annual Report
Jim Frey provided an annual report addressing the activities of the school district and recommendations
for improving student learning and district operations. The report also ensures awareness of compliance
with statutory requirements as specified in Policy #2106 (Program Compliance).
Teaching Assignments – Out of Endorsement
Jim Frey shared WAC 181-82-110 which identifies the process for approving teachers out of their
endorsed area. Lynden School district has 3 teachers to consider approving for the 2017-2018 school
year. The Board discussed the plans and experiences pertaining to each teacher.
Steve Jilk motioned to approve the Out of Endorsement Teaching Assignments seconded by Kelli Kettels. Motion
carried by voice vote.

Finance and Operations
Budget Update - June
Patti Fairbanks provided information on the June budget review containing a summary of financial
status, enrollment, general fund comparative, and revenues and expenditures.
Budget 2017-2018
Patti Fairbanks and the Board reviewed the 2017-2018 budget and discussed increase in student
enrollment, employee FTE, local taxes, general apportionment funding, curriculum and instruction
support, general fund, student body fund, debt services fund, capital projects fund, transportation fund,
and summaries by program. Patti addressed the priorities around the 2017-2018 budget including
teaching and learning, technology, facilities and maintenance, and salaries and benefits. The board
reviewed resources to continue to provide the best possible education for students.
