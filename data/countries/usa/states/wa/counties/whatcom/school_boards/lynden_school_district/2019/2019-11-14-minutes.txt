Isom Elementary, in partnership with Lynden Library and Humanities Washington, is offering a free sixweek literacy program for their students and families. Isom is celebrating the opportunity to help serve 24
families for the purpose of enhancing family literacy skill to build lifelong reading habits. Activities
included each week are a complimentary dinner, storytelling, reading, and a family discussion promoting
reading comprehension. The program is running through November 19th.
Local business, Cascade Dafo provided a buddy bench for the Fisher playground.
Lynden High School students, along with Mt. Baker Rotary, had a great day of community service together
by planting trees and laying sod at Bender Fields.
Many of our fall sports teams went to District Playoffs with some continuing to State. Congratulations to
girls soccer, girls swim, X-country, volleyball, and football. Go Lions!
We hosted a successful safety meeting with other community schools that focused on reunification
practices in case of an emergency.
Thanks to the District Office staff involved in coordinating the move to the SEBB medical program.
We have 181 AP exams ordered which mean 154 students are taking the exam versus 85 from last year.

6. Reports
Superintendent
 Levy Updates
Jim Frey provided an update on the recommended Education, Programs and Operations (EP&O) levy and
the Technology and Facilities levy. The Board will take formal action to approve Resolution No. 13-19 and
Resolution No. 14-19 for these levies to be placed on the February 11, 2020 Special Elections ballot.
Teaching and Learning
 Highly Capable Program
Megan Dickson, Highly Capable Program Coordinator, presented an overview of the program design, the
process of student identification, and the resources used to provide for a highly capable student. Megan
talked about the engagement of highly capable students and middle school students in the FIT program at
the middle school, and the professional development opportunities for staff and families.


Advancement Via Individual Determination (AVID)
High School Principal, Ian Freeman and Assistant High School Principal, Michelle Nilsen, presented the
components of the AVID program and how it helps to close the achievement gap by utilizing specific
strategies to support all students as they prepare for college, career, and life.

Board Reports
 WSSDA Conference
Board members discussed the Washington State School Director Association (WSSDA) Annual Conference
Agenda and Workshops and the areas of focus the Board would like to administer in the future. Board
members will be attending the conference November 21 – 24, 2019.


Listening Posts
Jim Frey provided a possible schedule of dates and list of topics for the Board to engage with community
members and stake holders about school related issues that impact young children and families in the
community.



Superintendent Evaluation
Jim Frey shared the timeline and criteria for the superintendent evaluation for the 2019-2020 school year.

Finance and Operations
None at this meeting.
