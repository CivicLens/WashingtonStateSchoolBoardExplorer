  6. PUBLIC COMMENTS- 6:40pm
  Information: 6.1 Audience Comments
  Andi Nofziger, EEA President, encouraged the board to allocate staffing appropriately and not increase staffing ratios. She emphasized that students feel a sense of belonging and are more likely to graduate when they are participating in electives and are in small classes.

Suzanne Wallace, ESD World Languages teacher, stressed that students without three years of world language are not as competitve when applying to colleges. She shared that her students are frustrated and angry about the reduction of these classes due to budget cuts.

Amy Frost, ESD teacher, shared how the proposed reduced educational plan will harm staff in buildings. She noted that teachers have a heavy workload and do not have time to assist with other initiatives.

Annika Beckstrom & Amita Rahbarrad, students at Edmonds Woodway High School spoke about climate change. They proposed a goal to make students aware of the problem and suggested the district create a class or curriculum unit about climate change.

Wyatt Ahrens, 5th grader at Terrace Park Elementary, spoke about how much he enjoys band and orchestra and that he would be devastated if music programs were cut due to budget reductions.

Jason Moore, community member, encouraged the district not to cut paraeducators. He questioned why the board was not cutting diversity and equity initiatives instead. He encouraged the board to focus on safety in schools.

Sharon Kulseth, ESD employee, spoke about the value of the work experience program. She shared that the proposed changes to this program were not discussed with the program coordinator and the paraeducators. She also shared that this group will be meeting with Dr. Miner in two weeks to discuss the proposed changes.

Doug Trost, ESD teacher and parent, shared that every year we end up reducing young teachers and then needing to hire them again in August. He also shared that he believes teachers are burnt out and that morale is suffering. Lastly, he shared the importance of our paraeducators.

Kaylee Marquiss & Kira Miller, Cedar Way Elementary school students, shared with the board how much they love band and orchestra and they don't want to lose these classes due to budget cuts.
  7. NEW BUSINESS- 7:40pm
  Action: 7.1 First Reading (no action), Resolution #24-09, 2024-2025 Reduced Educational Program
The board discussed the proposed reduced educational program. A second reading and vote will take place next week at the regular business meeting.

Director Kilgore asked clarifying questions regarding the fund balance.
Director Cramer asked questions about the cost spent on legal expenses and public records requests. He followed that up with questions about the fund balance and whether or not it was enough to cover emergency expenses.
Director Katims posed questions about the way we allocate staff at the secondary level. She also requested clarification that the reduced educational program is a maximum number of employees that would be reduced. This could change between now and the May 15th deadline to notify certificated staff.
Director Chase thanked everyone for coming out tonight to share their feedback.
  8. BOARD MEMBER COMMENTS
  Information: 8.1 Board Member Comments
  Director Cramer noted that he knows and understands that teacher and principal burnout is real. He shared his hopes that we will make the best of the funding we receive from the state and work on solving these problems together.

Director Smith shared that he understands how difficult these decisions are to make. He thanked the community for all of their feedback. He encouraged everyone to submit written comments and emails. He also shared that Wyatt Tonkin, Shoreline baseball coach recently passed away and wanted to share that he was very saddened by his passing and noted how much he impacted his community. He also congratulated Nalin Sood, MTHS basketball coach, on his retirement from coaching.

Director Chase shared that she is proud of our Equity and Achievement Alliance and that there is an exhibit in our building currently for Arab American Heritage month. She thanked this group and our district for their work on equity.

Director Kilgore shared that this is a tough night and that she understands the importance of all the programs that are potentially facing a budget reduction.

Director Katims thanked everyone for their comments. She reminded the community that our percentage of spending on central office is way below average compared to other large districts in the state and the amount we spend on teaching is very high. She also shared that she has spent a great deal of time this past year on advocating our legislators for additional and appropriate educational funding. They are currently planning additional events to tell our story to our legislators.

Dr. Miner thanked the board for their participation and questions on the budget and for advocating to the legislature tirelessly during this past year. She is hopeful that in the future we can use Balancing Act to do the reverse and identify areas where we could provide additional funding. She emphasized that the state is not currently fulfilling their duty to fund schools.
