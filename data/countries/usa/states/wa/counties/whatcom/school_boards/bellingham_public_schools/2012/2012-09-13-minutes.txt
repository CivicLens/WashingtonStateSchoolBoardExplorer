School Board Directors’ Reports
Director Camille Hackler attended the new teacher orientation breakfast and the back-to-school
kickoff at Bellingham High School. Ms. Hackler also attended the Silver Beach Elementary
School Meet and Greet and an English Language Learners meeting at Alderwood Elementary
School. She met with Superintendent Baker and Director Steve Smith for a breakfast meeting.
Ms. Hackler has been asked to serve as the Washington State School Directors Association
(WSSDA) representative to the Western Washington University Woodring College of
Education’s Professional Education Advisory Board for Teacher Education (PEAB).

will include the Teacher Principal Evaluation Process training. He said he has had many
conversations with parents, particularly regarding south side schools and math placement at
Fairhaven Middle School. He has encouraged them to submit their comments to the Listening
Post so all Board members will be equally informed.
Steve Smith attended the new teacher orientation breakfast and the Back-to-School Kickoff. He
also attended a breakfast meeting with Superintendent Baker and Director Camille Hackler. Dr.

Superintendent’s Announcements and Report
Superintendent Greg Baker showed the video “Class of 2024,” which included an introduction
by a kindergarten student.
Dr. Baker felt the 2012–13 school year was off to a great start with the typical issues normally
experienced during the first week of school. This is the second year of full-time kindergarten and
first grade teachers are impressed with the improved learning their new students are showing.
Pre-kindergarten started Sept. 7. This year, counselors have been assigned to cover all
elementary schools. The district has been experiencing some problems with the technology
infrastructure, which staff is working to solve.
Dr. Baker reviewed enrollment numbers and related staffing issues. He summarized how the
Executive Team reviews class sizes daily during the first week of school in order to make
adjustments. Total enrollment is 88 students more than projected.
The district has many students attending the Northwest Career and Technical Academy in Skagit
County and a few attending the construction and engineering classes at Meridian School District.
Information about the Facilities Task Force has been publicized. Individuals interested in serving
on the task force must submit an application by Sept. 18. Jim and Linden Darling will serve as
co-chairs of the task force.

POLICY GOVERNANCE MONITORING REPORT
Ends 1, Strategic Vision and Mission
Superintendent Greg Baker gave a monitoring report to the Board on Ends 1, Strategic Vision
and Mission. Dr. Baker indicated the district is “in compliance” and identified supporting
criteria. Steve Smith moved and Scott Stockburger seconded acceptance of the monitoring report
as in compliance. The motion carried unanimously.

POLICY GOVERNANCE REVIEW
The Board discussed revisions to Policy Governance policies Ends 2.1, Student Competence;
Ends 3, Skills and Attributes; and Governance Policy 1, Governance Commitment and Beliefs.
Executive Director of Communications and Community Relations Tanya Rowe reviewed the
revisions the Board discussed at the August 27, 2012 Study Session. Steve Smith moved and
Camille Hackler seconded the motion to accept the proposed revisions to the above Policy
Governance policies, as submitted. The motion carried unanimously.
