Public Content
Those wishing to address the Board must sign up on the sheet provided at the beginning of the meeting. Individuals may
address the Board on agenda items as well as non-agenda items. Speakers will be recognized by the Board president at the
time they are to speak.
Each speaker has a maximum of three minutes and may not transfer their time to another speaker. No more than 30 minutes is
allowed for a single subject except with the unanimous consent of the Board. The Board president may interrupt or terminate an
individuals statement when it is too lengthy, personally directed, irrelevant, or abusive. The Board as a whole has the final
decision on such matters.
Complaints concerning District employees or other individuals may not be addressed orally at the meeting. Such complaints
must be signed and presented to the Board in writing. Executive session may then be granted for a hearing. Questions or
concerns about instructional practices or materials should be directed to the superintendent for appropriate referral.

2.8 Bellingham Technical College Dual Enrollment
(#1617-067)

Meeting :

Oct 20, 2016 - Regular Board Meeting

Type :

Action (Consent)

Recommended Action :

Staff submitted the attached agreement with Bellingham
Technical College in connection with the Dual Enrollment
College in the High School program. As Superintendent, I
have reviewed and approved this agreement and am
submitting it as a consent item.

Public Content
Staff Contact: Steve Clarke, Assistant Superintendent, Department of Teaching and Learning
Explanation of Issue:
The agreement allows high school juniors and seniors the opportunity to concurrently earn college credit
and high school credit, while remaining on their high school campus. Bellingham School District faculty
teaching the courses will meet the same qualifications required of college faculty teaching the same
course. Tuition will be charged to the student ($125.00 for a five-credit class). Bellingham Technical
College (BTC) will pay Bellingham School District faculty teaching the class $45.00 per student in
recognition of the administrative duties outside their normal high school work day.
