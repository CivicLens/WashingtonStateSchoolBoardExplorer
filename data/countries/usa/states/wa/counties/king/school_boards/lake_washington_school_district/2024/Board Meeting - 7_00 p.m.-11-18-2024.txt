Type :

Recognition

Public Content
Land acknowledgement and commitment to native students and families:
The board of directors of Lake Washington School District acknowledges that our district is on the
Indigenous Land of the Coast Salish Peoples, who have reserved treaty rights on this land, including the
Duwamish (dxdwab) and the Snoqualmie Indian Tribe (sdukalbix). We thank these caretakers of this land
who have lived here since time immemorial. We commit to continue collaborating with our native
community, to teach the indigenous history of this land and these local tribes to all children of the district.
We also commit to provide the best possible education for all children of native descent who attend our
schools.

1. Transfer Of Territory Petition, Resolution
No. 2390

Meeting :

Nov 18, 2024 - Board Meeting - 7:00 p.m.

Type :

Action

Recommended Action :

Approve Resolution No. 2390 to approve the petition for
transfer territory from Riverview School District into the
Lake Washington School District.

Public Content
SITUATION:

August 6, 2024, the District received notification from Puget Sound Educational Service District (PSESD)
that a validated petition to transfer territory, consisting of 88 parcels of property from the Riverview
School District to the Lake Washington School District had been received.
Board President Leah Choi and Board Member Eric Laliberte were appointed to participate in
negotiations regarding the petition in accordance with guidelines for territory transfers. Board Member
Jodi Fletcher was appointed to represent the Riverview School District in the negotiations. Joining in the
negotiations were Superintendent Dr. Jon Holmen and Barbara Posthumus, Associate Superintendent, of
Lake Washington. Dr. Susan Leach, Superintendent and Meisha Roberson, Assistant Superintendent of
Business Operations, participated on behalf of Riverview.
A joint meeting was held on November 5, 2024 at the Lake Washington School District Resource Center
to negotiate the territory transfer petition with the representatives of Riverview School District. A report
from the Lake Washington negotiating committee is attached. The members of the negotiating team
considered the factors that guide the territory transfer process and recommend that the territory transfer
be approved.
On November 25, the Riverview School District Board of Directors are scheduled to take action on the
transfer of territory petition.
Once both boards take action, then the results will be communicated to the Puget Sound ESD no later
than December 5, 2024
RECOMMENDATION:
The Board approves Resolution No. 2390 to approve the petition for transfer territory from Riverview
School District into the Lake Washington School District.
