Call to Order

A regular meeting of the Board of Directors was held on Monday, April 29, 2024, in the
Administrative Service Center Board Room and was available for live streaming.
President Lynn Goebel called the meeting to order at 6:00 p.m. and led the flag salute.

Roll Call

Board members Lynn Goebel, Jessica Aws, Nicole Tesch, Sharon Jensen, and Josh
MacLean were present. Student board representatives Josiah Villano and Maggie
Litvachuk were present.

Adoption of Agenda

The agenda was adopted by unanimous consent.

Consent Agenda

Vice President Aws moved to approve the consent agenda. Director Jensen seconded
the motion. The motion passed unanimously.

Recognition of HandHand,
HomeConnection, &

Staff

Principal Shane Evans recognized and thanked students and staff of Hand-in-Hand Early
Learning Center, HomeConnection, and Oak Harbor Virtual Academy.

Recognition of OHHS
Students

Oak Harbor High School Principal Nate Salisbury and North Whidbey Fire & Rescue Chief
Chris Swiger recognized and thanked three Oak Harbor High School students for saving
a fellow student’s life.

Student
Representative
Report

Student Representative Josiah Villano announced Teacher Appreciation Week,
upcoming AP testing, and athletic events.

Visitors

Scott Minckley, parent, spoke about transportation at OVE. Eryn Harvey, parent,
provided a written comment about school safety which was read aloud.

Superintendent
Report

Superintendent Michelle Kuss-Cybula congratulated Oak Harbor High School for
receiving the US News & World Report Best High Schools award. She provided updates
on MTSS work in the district, inclusionary playground equipment at schools, a Crescent
Harbor Elementary School construction information night, budget reductions, the
student board representative application process, and board professional development
for the 2024-25 year. She highlighted the upcoming Instructional Review coming up on
June 6.

Hand-in-Hand Early
Learning Center /
HomeConnection /
Oak Harbor Virtual
Academy Report

Principal Shane Evans presented a report on the work of Hand-in-Hand Early Learning
Center, HomeConnection, and Oak Harbor Virtual Academy.

Legislative Update

Director Tesch spoke about the legislative priorities for the 2023-2024 school year and
explained the benefit of advocating for the district with Washington State School
Directors Association (WSSDA), Washington Association of School Administrators
(WASA), and Association of Washington School Principals (AWSP) – the “big three” – for
a unified message at the state level.

WIAA Update

Director Jensen reported that dozens of amendments were proposed in January, one of
which is related to high school transfers, which will go to committee and be voted on
next year. There will be a vote in May regarding the addition of two new high school
sports: badminton and girls flag football.

Board Comments

Vice President Aws announced that the Cape Club will be traveling next week to present
at the 2024 Spring Youth Forum and Competition. Director Jensen voiced her
appreciation for the opportunity to attend the recent student voice sessions. Director
Tesch praised the district’s communications work and thanked district leadership for
their support of the Anacortes district. She spoke about the Crescent Harbor
Elementary information night and a recent editorial about Memorial Stadium. President
Goebel also appreciated the opportunity to attend the student voice sessions. She
shared about athletic events, the Oak Harbor High School accreditation process, a
community choir program, student art show, and Arbor Day. In recognition of Teacher
Appreciation Week, she thanked the district’s teachers for their work.

Superintendent
Comments

Dr. Kuss-Cybula thanked Principal Evans for his work and the community for attending
the recognitions. She thanked Amber Porter for her presentation.

Upcoming Meetings

Regular meetings are scheduled for Monday, May 13, and Tuesday, May 28, 2024, at
6:00 p.m. in the Administrative Service Center Board Room.
