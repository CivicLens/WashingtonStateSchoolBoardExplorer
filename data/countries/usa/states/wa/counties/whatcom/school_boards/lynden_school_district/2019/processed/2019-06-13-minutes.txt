The high school Health Occupations Student Association Chapter cooperated with Bloodworks Northwest
to host a blood drive. Thanks to the 55 donors, as well as Hannah Jennings and Christiaan VanRooyen,
who organized the event. An estimated 141 patients will be helped.
The highly capable night was well attended with over 40 adults and students. The NWESD spoke about Hi
Cap laws and the change in legislation as well as the differences between a "bright" and a "highly capable"
child. It was a great night of making connections with families and community building.
MENTOR Washington has given Be the One (BTO) a Dedicated Partner Status for the work that has been
done to aligning the BTO program with national standards and best practices. Lisa Reynolds and Brian
Clemmer, BTO Coordinators, have done a lot of work to make this happen.
Spring sports had a great season including Brenna VanderYacht participating in Tennis Districts, Fastpitch
5th in State, Boys Soccer to Districts, Boys Golf, Jordan Medcalf 1st in State, Baseball to Regionals, Track,
Girls 4x400 Champions along with multiple 4th, 5th & 7th placings.

our Student Director, Cynthia Torres!

6. Reports
Superintendent
Teaching and Learning
• Mathematics Program Update
Elizabeth Hamming provided an update on the mathematics program and the evolving professional
development with the Teachers Development Group. The system-wide focus on math instruction will
include content knowledge, instructional strategies, evidence of student learning, curriculum materials, the
design of continuing professional learning, essential standards and vertical alignment, and targeted
support.
Finance and Operations
• Budget Update 2019-2020
Patti Fairbanks provided a projected 2019-2020 budget and explained the completion process to finalize a
budget for the 2019-2020 school year. Patti shared the use of a multi-year tool to help project enrollment,
revenues and expenditures. Currently the budget reflects a deficit between revenues and expenditures
which will require that adjustments be made in order to reach a balanced budget. Patti will provide an
update at the next meeting.

7. Board Workshop
• Early Learning
Tim Metz gave an overview on preschool programs in the district and the significant growth that continues
to take place. Opportunities for early learning are: Early Support for Infants and Toddlers; Lynden School
District Integrated Preschool; Early Childhood Education and Assistance Program; and Community
Playgroup. Tim shared how each of these programs have a positive impact on the early age for learning.
