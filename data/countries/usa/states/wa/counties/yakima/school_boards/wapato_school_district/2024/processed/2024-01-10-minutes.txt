5. Reports
Superintendent's Report: Kelly Garza, Superintendent, reported:
Bond Facts Information has been distributed to many staff, parents and patrons.  The District is scheduled to present this information on Jan. 16 at the Wapato City Council Meeting and on Jan 23 is a Community Forum at Camas Elementary to provide the community information about costs, and project facilities and timeline. The Board asked about signage throughout the community regarding the bond. Mr. Garza reported the Wapato Citizens Committee is working with local business to place Bond information in patron areas. The Board also asked about letters to the editor and waving signs on busy intersections.  The Board is concerned bond information is not being distributed to rural farm areas and farmers that are also voting constituents. Mr. Garza noted the Boards feedback and will bring this to the committeeâ€™s next meeting.
In the process of reviewing Construction Management Companies, the District is planning interviews next week and plans to bring forward a recommendation at the next regular school board meeting for board review and action.
The District received an email of notice of being recipients of a grant for an electrical bus. Mr. Garza was surprised of the news since the District did not apply for this type of grant.  Kevin Hill, Executive Director researched the grant and shared what he learned. RCW a transportation company applied for the grant to provide 1 electrical bus in the next 2 years.  The grant award is for $315,000.  Mr. Hill is concerned about maintenance, repairs, housing it, and distance driving and usage. Mr. Hill informed other Districts are also received this grant for electric buses. The overall concerns are the impact to the District and cost to sustain this type of vehicle. The Board engaged in discussion and Mr. Garza will continue to research this grant and its impacts to the District.
Javier Rodriguez shared an update of the pump sewage issue at Satus/Camas Elementary.  Currently the septic service company is coming daily to empty the overflow.  The District continues to await the new pump certification. Staff are monitoring the pump daily.  Mr. Rodriguez informed the pump estimated arrival will be determined after engineers certify the pump for install and usage.  Once the pump is cleared, the District will to schedule a thorough cleanup before electrician and engineers will install.
Financial Report: Sylvia Bazan, Director of Fiscal Services, presented the November 2023 Budget Status report.  She explained in detail the expenditures and revenues in each fund category: General, Capital Projects, Debt Service, ASB, Transportation Vehicle, and Private Purpose.
