LHS Chess Team took 2nd place in the league and 14th at State. The kids had a great year! Angel Lopez
deserves a shout out for winning all of his games this year - including the one he played against Mike
McKee. Players included Tommy Bridges (3rd place trophy), Samuel Bridges, Joe Giblin, Christian
Van Rooyn, Jony Garfia, Emma Doede, Angel Lopez-Chavarin, Jacob Top, Max Bernard.
Boys Swim Team took 13th place at State in the 200 Freestyle Relay, which included
Aaron Somma, Rafe Wolfisberg, Hans Tamminga, Marco Gutierrez.
LHS athlete, Bode Human attended Boys Wrestling State.
Girls Wrestling went to State with participants Hallie Holestine who placed 3rd in state (tied for our highest
finish ever) and Beatriz Briceno.
Mrs. Vis, Isom third grade teacher integrated a mini language arts and social studies unit. In this unit,
students learned listening, speaking, reading, writing, and art skills while learning how different countries
celebrate the holiday season. To make their learning more exciting students wrote and illustrated the
traditions their families share. They created a collaborative class book that was published. Students were
excited to share their published work with parents at a publishing party in February. Being published
authors has motivated students to write more and to build upon their reading and writing skills.
Twenty-one middle school leadership students attended a leadership conference at Nooksack Valley
Middle School hosted by the Association of Washington Student Leaders. Students came back with ideas
for how to promote a positive school climate and energized to promote school spirit for the end of the
school year. It was exciting to see 6th graders grow in their confidence to become leaders.
Lynden Academy's K-8 classrooms partnered with Gabby, a sophomore in Lynden High Schools National
Honor Society in a food drive. Gabby organized a food drive competition to support the Light
House Mission and rewarded the classrooms that brought the most items with a donut party! Mrs. Miller
and Mr. Binskin's classrooms tied with 47 items each. LA contributed a total of 173 food items

• Secondary School Improvement Plan (SIP)
Principals Ian Freeman, Michelle Nilsen (LHS), Molly Mitchell-Mumma, Cyndi Selcho (LMS) presented
their SIP report to the Board and provided information that addressed the work taking place to improve
learning in each classroom and build teacher capacity to ensure quality instruction and aligned learning.
The principals shared the appreciation for collaboration of instructional practices between staff at LHS
and LMS. Principals explained the concept of the AVID program that provides support for students. LMS
Dean of Students, Rex Frasier, shared about Social Emotional Learning practices. Student Directors, Riley

and Ruby spoke of the pros and cons of their experiences in the middle school and high school in both
academics and activities and the opportunities outside of the brick and mortar school building that has
been offered to provide experience for students to plan for their future.
