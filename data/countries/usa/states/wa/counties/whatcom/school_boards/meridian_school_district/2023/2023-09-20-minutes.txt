MERIDIAN SCHOOL DISTRICT #505
REGULAR SCHOOL BOARD MEETING AND BUDGET HEARING
MEETING HELD IN PERSON & VIA ZOOM
September 20, 2023
REGULAR MEETING
The regular meeting was called to order by Board Chair Sara Bernardy at 6:00 p.m.
The pledge of allegiance was recited.
Board Chair Sara Bernardy welcomed everyone that was in attendance.
Board members present at the meeting were Sara Bernardy, Craig Mitchell, Marty Gray, Steve Corwin,
and Jarrod Karuza.
Student Board Representatives present at the meeting were Kennedy Brzozowski and Morgan Adams.
A motion to adopt the agenda was made by Marty Gray and seconded by Craig Mitchell. The motion
passed 5-0.
CONSENT AGENDA
A. Approval of Meeting Minutes
B. Accounts Payable
C. Personnel Report
D. District Credit Card: Authorized Users
E. 2023-24 Teacher of the Visually Impaired (TVI) Interlocal Agreement
F. 2023-24 NWESD School Nurse Corps Contract
G. 2023-24 Ninth Grade Success Partnership Agreement
A motion to adopt the consent agenda was made by Jarrod Karuza and seconded by Steve Corwin. The
motion passed 5-0.
REPORTS
Staff Professional Development Plan
Director of Teaching and Learning, Adrienne Somera presented a report on the professional development
plan for the 2023-24 school year. Adrienne shared details about the professional development that staff
will engage in and how the learning is directly tied to our 2023-24 strategic plan commitment focus areas.
Summer Maintenance Update
Director of Maintenance and Operations, Jay Yeager reported to the Board on the maintenance and
facilities projects the crew completed over the summer months. Jay shared details about the work that
was completed at each facility.
PUBLIC COMMENT
There was no public comment.
OLD BUSINESS
Second Review and Approval: Bradley University Preceptor Agreement
Dr. James Everett presented Bradley University Preceptor Agreement to the Board for a second review
and approval. Student representatives favor approval. A motion to approve the Bradley University
Preceptor Agreement as presented was made by Steve Corwin and seconded by Marty Gray. The motion
passed 5-0.

FINANCE REPORT
Finance Report
Director of Finance, Daniel Yorton gave a financial update to the Board. Daniel reported to the Board on
our enrollment projections, budget status and budget projections.
SUPERINTENDENT’S REPORT
Dr. Everett started his report by recognizing the Board for submitting their Board of Distinction application
to WSSDA. Dr. Everett announced that OSPI has approved our application for a Core Skill Center in
Whatcom County and shared details about initial programming thoughts for the campus. Dr. Everett
highlighted our Band, Cheer and Color Guard students who were recently interviewed on KAFE radio.
Details about recent collaborative meetings Dr. Everett has been engaged in, as well as upcoming training
and meeting opportunities were shared with the Board.
INFORMATION FROM STUDENT BOARD REPRESENTATIVES
Student Representatives Morgan and Kennedy shared a brief introduction about how they would like to
engage student voices.
