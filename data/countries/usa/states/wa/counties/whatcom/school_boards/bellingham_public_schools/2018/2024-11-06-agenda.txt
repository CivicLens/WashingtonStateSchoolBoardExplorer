Public Content
Those wishing to address the board are asked to complete a sign-insheetat the beginning of the meeting. Individuals may
address the board on agenda items as well as non-agenda items. The board president will recognize speakers at the time they
are to speak.
Each speaker has a maximum of three minutes and may not transfer their time to another speaker. No more than 30 minutes is
allowed for a single subject except with the unanimous consent of the board. The board president may interrupt or terminate an
individuals statement when it is too lengthy, personally directed, irrelevant, or abusive. The board as a whole has the final
decision on such matters.
Complaints concerning district employees or other individuals may not be addressed orally at the meeting. Such complaints must

be signed and presented to the board in writing. Executive session may then be granted for a hearing. Questions or concerns
about instructional practices or materials should be directed to the superintendent for appropriate referral.

2018-19 budget and fully support its adoption and the
approval of Resolution 15-18.

Public Content
Staff Contact: Simone Sangster, Assistant Superintendent of Finance and Operations
Explanation of Issue:
Attached is Resolution 15-18 which, if approved by the board, will formally adopt the 2018-19 Bellingham
School District budget.
The final proposed 2018-19 budget is attached. This includes the district's budget book and the state
required budget document, Form F-195. The F-195 has been updated slightly. We have distributed
copies of thesedocuments to board members.
General Fund expenditures for 2018-19 are projected at $170,658,085 an increase of $17,338,076 (11.3
percent) compared to 2017-18 budged expenditures. This increase is the result of a combination of
factors including mandatory obligations associated with Collective Bargaining Agreements, salary
increases, education/experience step increases for applicable certificated and classified staff;stateset
adjustments to salaries and benefits; investments in 2018-19 programs and services through additional
certificated and classifiedstaffing for schools; and districtwide support.
After carefully overseeing the 2018-19 budget process and a thorough review of the budget assumptions
and resulting calculations, I am submitting the 2018-19 budget for adoption in accordance with Resolution
15-18.
