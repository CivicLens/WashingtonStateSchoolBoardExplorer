The regular meeting of the Board of Education, White Salmon Valley School District, Klickitat and Skamania Counties, White
Salmon, WA was called to order at 7:00 p.m. Thursday, June 27, 2024, at the WSVSD Board Room, Park Center Building, located
at 170 NW Washington St., White Salmon, WA 98672, and via a ZOOM public meeting. Present: Board Chair Laurie Stanton
(online), Vice-chair Peter Harkema, Kris Ostness, Matthew Byrne (online), Pat Dempsey, Superintendent Rich Polkinghorn, and
guests.
Vice-chair Chair Peter Harkema led the Board in the flag salute.
Additions/Deletions to Agenda: None.
Kris Ostness moved to adopt the agenda as presented. Seconded by Pat Dempsey. Carried.
Public Hearing 2024-2025 Final Budget:
a) Open Public Hearing: At 7:05 p.m. Peter Harkema opened the public hearing.
b) Presentation of the 2024-2025 Budget: Superintendent Rich Polkinghorn and Jim Brittain presented the 2024-2025
budget for input and feedback from the Board and public.
c) Board Member Discussion: The Board thanked Jim and Rich for their work on the budget.
d) Public Comment on the 2024-2025 Budget: At 7:48 p.m. Peter Harkema opened the hearing for public comment on the
2024-2025 Budget. No comment was made.
e) Close Public Hearing: At 7:49 p.m. the public hearing was closed.

Reports:
a) Superintendent Update: Superintendent Polkinghorn provided an update, discussing the recent certificated bargaining
session and process of building the 2024-2025 budget to align with board policy and the strategic goals. Superintendent
Polkinghorn expressed gratitude to the staff for their efforts throughout the year in order to make it a success for our
students.
b) Board Member Committee Report: Pat Dempsey acknowledged Brenda McGowen's retirement. Kris Ostness reported
on updates made by the wellness committee and Laurie Stanton mentioned positive outcomes from budget committee
meetings.
c) Student Representatives Report: No report was given.
Recognition and Good News:
Math and Literacy Night Events: Brenda Hope shared highlights from Family Math Night, noting positive feedback
from attendees and recognized both community and student volunteers. Megan Sheffels discussed the Literacy Carnival,
highlighting its success and the community support it received. Superintendent Polkinghorn and the Board expressed
appreciation for the events and all those who worked to put them on.
Public Comments: No public comment was received.

appreciation for the continued effort of the committee.
