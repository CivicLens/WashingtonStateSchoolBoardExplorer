Public Content
Those wishing to address the Board must sign up on the sheet provided at the beginning of the meeting. Individuals may
address the Board on agenda items as well as non-agenda items. Speakers will be recognized by the Board president at the
time they are to speak.
Each speaker has a maximum of three minutes and may not transfer their time to another speaker. No more than 30 minutes is
allowed for a single subject except with the unanimous consent of the Board. The Board president may interrupt or terminate an
individuals statement when it is too lengthy, personally directed, irrelevant, or abusive. The Board as a whole has the final
decision on such matters.
Complaints concerning District employees or other individuals may not be addressed orally at the meeting. Such complaints
must be signed and presented to the Board in writing. Executive session may then be granted for a hearing. Questions or
concerns about instructional practices or materials should be directed to the superintendent for appropriate referral.

2.8 North Cascades Institute Program Master
Agreement (#1617-095)

Meeting :

Jan 12, 2017 - Regular Board Meeting

Type :

Action (Consent)

Recommended Action :

Recommended Action from Deputy Superintendent Mike
Copland: As outlined below, I have reviewed and, as Deputy
Superintendent, am recommending board approval of the
attached contract with North Cascades Institute in connection
with the two-day Snow School program. (The
superintendent's service as a board member of the North
Cascades Institute represents a remote interest per RCW
42.23.040 and precludes him from any involvement in this
contract, including recommending it to the board for approval.)

Public Content
Staff Contact: Mike Copland, Deputy Superintendent
Explanation of Issue:
This agreement covers the services of North Cascades Institute to the Bellingham School District for the
purpose of Bellingham students participating in the-two day"Snow School" program. Day 1 (Classroom
Session) consists of a classroom session whereby a Snow School instructor visits students in their

classroom to provide an introduction to snow science and help prepare them for a field session. On Day 2
(Field Session), the students visit the Mt. Baker Ski Area and work together in small teams to carry out a
field investigation surrounding the current snow and weather conditions.
Notification of remote interest: Per RCW 42.23.040, the superintendent's service as a board member of
the North Cascades Institute (a non-profit corporation) is considered a remote interest. The
superintendent is not recommending this contract for approval by the board. Assuming approval of this
agenda item, the contract will be signed by the board president.
