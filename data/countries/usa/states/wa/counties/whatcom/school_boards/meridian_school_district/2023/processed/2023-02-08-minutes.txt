REPORTS
PLC Presentations
The STEAM PLC team from MP3 gave a PLC report on Visual Arts: Creating; Anchor Standard 2.3. Details
were shared about how students combined art, language arts and technology together to create stop
motion videos. The team shared formative assessments used to measure student knowledge of the skills
being measured, strategies used to assist with further progress and student self-reflection comments.

Kindergarten teachers from Irene Reither presented PLC information on kindergarten grade-level
phonics and word analysis skills in decoding words. The kindergarten team shared several different
strategies being utilized to teach the skill, common formative assessments that are used to measure
progress, and spoke of the many techniques that are being used to proactively increase the skill
knowledge and further grow the skill. Initial and post assessment scores were shared from classes.
Math teachers from Meridian Middle school gave a PLC math presentation on understanding
proportional relationships using common mathematical models, such as: diagrams, tables, graphs,
double number lines, and equations. The team spoke about the standard for each grade level, pretest
and post-tests used to identify if students know the desired skill, and the steps that are taken if students
do or do not know the skill. Student growth was shared for each grade level.
Curriculum Report
Director of Teaching and Learning Adrienne Somera presented a curriculum update to the Board with a
focus on how our curriculum practices align with the newly adopted strategic plan. Dr. Everett expressed
appreciation to Adriene for the continual work she does to keep our school district aligned.
MP3 School Report
MP3 Principal Michelle Siadal presented a MP3 school report to the Board. Michelle shared details about
the MP3 campus and extension program that are offered for grades K-8 kids. Michelle spoke about the
instruction models, curriculum offerings, assessments administered, and the finances of MP3.

Dr. Everett spoke on Policy 1250 and shared the intent of the policy to prioritize student voice. Dr.
Everett asked the Board to seek input from student representatives, prior to vote.
Dr. Everett recognized our school counseling staff, gave an update on the JUUL Lawsuit, shared details
about upcoming Board training with Lori Oviatt.
Dr. Everett spoke about the opposition to House Bill 1550 which is designed to eliminate transitional
kindergarten as written.
Dr. Everett shared details about House Bill 1605, and Senate Bill 5668. Both bills seek to provide support
through sustained FTE for small school districts who send students to skill center, so long as they
account for at least 10% of their junior and senior students. We have submitted a request to a hearing
for both bills.
An update on our student athletics and food service was also shared with the Board.
Dr. Everett shared updates about the Whatcom County Skills Center meetings and communication being
had with key legislators and workforce development stakeholders. Information was also shared about
the exploration of an Early Childhood Education center as a potential supplement to the Whatcom Skills
Center.
Several upcoming training workshops and opportunities were shared by Dr. Everett.
