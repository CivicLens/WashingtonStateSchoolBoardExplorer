OPENING ITEMS
Meeting Date and Location:
August 10, 2022 – Options High School, 2015 Franklin St., Bellingham, WA and remote
via Zoom.
Call to Order/Land Acknowledgement
Ms. Bashaw called the meeting to order at 6:01 p.m. She acknowledged that this meeting,
while attended from various locations, is being held on the ancestral homelands of the
Coast Salish people.
Roll Call
Present: Directors Kelly Bashaw (President), Douglas Benjamin, Camille Diaz Hackler,
Jenn Mason, Katie Rose, Superintendent Greg Baker and Sheri O’Day (recorder).
Public Hearing on Adoption of 2021-22 Budget
Ms. Bashaw explained the purpose of the public hearing, which is to provide an
opportunity for anyone who would like to speak for or against the adoption of the 202223 budget to comment before the board takes action on the budget later this evening. No
audience presented, and no public testimony was offered or shared in written form
relating to the proposed budget adoption.
Audience:
Ms. Bashaw noted that public participation is limited to written submissions only, and all
written comments received will be shared with the school board directors. Each board
member has seen all messages received.
Superintendent’s Announcements and Report: Summary of Dr. Baker’s comments:
The consent agenda contains the proposed budget for the 2022-23 school year. Prior to
this evening, individual board members received answers to their questions regarding the
proposed budget. Dr. Baker thanked Dr. Simone Sangster, assistant superintendent of
finance, and her team for preparing the thorough budget document. He shared that the
executive team also contributed to the priorities and expenditures. He shared the
Materials, Supplies and Operational Costs (MSOCS) and noted that federal COVID-19
funds will need to be spent by the end of next year. Dr. Baker responded to questions
from the board regarding the budget. Mr. Benjamin noted that the budget document is an
amazing educational tool.
Dr. Baker shared that construction has begun at the new district office site and the
construction at Sunnyland Elementary School is proceeding, with the expectation that the
new building will be ready for students on the first day of school.

INFORMATIONAL ITEMS
School Board Directors’ Reports. Highlights of comments:
Mr. Benjamin shared that an ad hoc committee consisting of himself, Ms. Bashaw and
Executive Director of Communications and Community Relations Jackie Brawley met
earlier today to discuss applying for WSSDA’s Board of Distinction award. He noted that
the submission window closes on Sept. 10 and the application will be discussed further at
the board’s Aug. 24 retreat. He spent last weekend in Olympia with the WSSDA board,
which is undertaking an overhaul of its strategic plan. He will forward information about
WSSDA’s e-clippings, which is a great tool containing links to news articles related to
education. He has read and recommends the book “Indians of the Pacific Northwest” by
Vine Deloria.
Ms. Rose expressed appreciation for the work that went into the preparation of the 202223 budget book.
