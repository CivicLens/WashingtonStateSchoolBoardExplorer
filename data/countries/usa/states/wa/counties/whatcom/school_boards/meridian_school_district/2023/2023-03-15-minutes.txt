MERIDIAN SCHOOL DISTRICT #505
REGULAR SCHOOL BOARD MEETING
MERIDIAN SCHOOL DISTRICT
MEETING HELD IN PERSON & VIA ZOOM
March 15, 2023
REGULAR MEETING
The regular meeting was called to order by Board Chair Sara Bernardy at 7:00 p.m.
Board members present at the meeting were Sara Bernardy, Marty Gray, Craig Mitchell, Jarrod Karuza
and Steve Corwin.
Student Board Representative present at the meeting was Mirage DeNeal.
Student Board Representative Kurtis Kettman was excused from attendance.
The pledge of allegiance was recited.
A motion to adopt the agenda was made by Marty Gray and seconded by Craig Mitchell. The motion
passed 5‐0.
CONSENT AGENDA
A. Approval of Meeting Minutes
B. Personnel Report
C. Payroll
D. Accounts Payable
E. Donation Listing
F. Student Travel
A motion to adopt the consent agenda was made by Steve Corwin and seconded by Jarrod Karuza. The
motion passed 5‐0.
RECOGNITION
MHS Principal Derek Forbes recognized and shared the many ways the multi‐cultural club has cared for
the MHS community. Five students from the multi‐cultural club were also present.
REPORTS
PLC Presentations
Nate Beede and Catherine Mullett presented on behalf of the Social and emotional learning (SEL) team
at IRE, information about social awareness indicators. Information was shared about the data points
used to track growth and strategies used to grow and coach the skills.
Science teachers from MMS gave a PLC science presentation on systems and STEM models to include
developing and using models. The team spoke about the modeling standard for each grade level. The
tools used to measure if students know the desired skill were shared, as well as the steps that are taken
if students do or do not know the skill.

Teachers from the social studies department at MHS gave a PLC presentation on civics; students
understanding key ideas and principals of the Declaration of Independence and constructing arguments
with precise and knowledgeable claims with evidence from reliable sources and acknowledging the
counterclaims. The team spoke about the standard for each grade level. The tools used to measure if
students know the desired skill were shared, as well as the steps that are taken if students do or do not
know the skill.
MHS School Report
MHS Principal Derek Forbes and Assistant Principal Shaun Doffing presented a MHS school report to the
Board. Updates were shared about the work being done to increase the graduation rate, the grade on
track rate, post‐secondary education opportunities, attendance, and close opportunity gaps. Shaun also
spoke about the freshman success team.
CTE Program Report
Derek Forbes presented an update on the CTE program at MHS. Information was shared about new classes
being offered this year, certificates being offered for some courses, and partnerships between the
community and student groups.
PUBLIC COMMENT
There was no public comment.
NEW BUSINESS
First Reading: Juul Lawsuit Settlement
Dr. James Everett presented resolution 04‐23, Resolution to Approve Settlement in Juul Litigation. This
was a first reading of resolution 04‐23. At the Board meeting on March 29th, the Board will be asked to
approve this resolution.
Curriculum Materials Proposal First Review
Director of Teaching and Learning Adrienne Somera presented DESMOS Math and OpenSciEd curriculum
materials to the Board for first review.
Surplus of District Property
Dr. Everett presented a surplus request submitted by maintenance and operations director, Jay Yeager.
The surplus request is asking the Board to approve one vehicle for surplus. A motion to surplus the
vehicle as presented was made by Craig Mitchell and seconded by Steve Corwin. The motion passed 5‐0.
OLD BUSINESS
Confirmation of MHS Graduation Date
Dr. Everett presented a revised school year calendar with a revision to the MHS graduation date to the
Board for review and approval. A motion to approve the MHS graduation date as presented was made by
Jarrod Karuza and seconded by Marty Gray. The motion passed 5‐0.
Second Reading: Agreement to Participate ‐ Resolution 02‐23
Dr. James Everett presented a second reading of resolution 02‐23, Agreement to Participate in the
Branch/Core Campus of the Northwest Career & Technical Academy in Bellingham, WA. A motion to
adopt Resolution 02‐23 as presented was made by Steve Corwin and seconded by Craig Mitchell. The
motion passed 5‐0.

Second Reading: Policy and Procedure 6970
Kurt Harvill presented a second reading of Policy and Procedure 6970; Naming Schools, Facilities, and
Teams, and Use of School Mascots, Images, or Logos. A motion to adopt Policy and Procedure 6970 as
presented was made by Marty Gray and seconded by Steve Corwin. Student advisor abstained. The
motion passed 5‐0.
Second Reading: Policy and Procedure 3231
Kurt Harvill presented a second reading of Policy and Procedure 3231; Student Records. A motion to
adopt Policy and Procedure 3231 as presented was made by Craig Mitchell and seconded by Jarrod
Karuza. The motion passed 5‐0.
SUPERINTENDENT’S REPORT
Dr. Everett presented to the Board legislative bill updates, the latest information about the Whatcom
County Skills Center and Early Learning Childhood Development Center. Dr. Everett shared details about
the Washington State Risk Management Pool conference he recently attended and shared information
about upcoming training workshops and opportunities.

The Executive Session will begin at 6:00 p.m. followed by a Regular Board meeting at 7:00 p.m.
INFORMATION FROM STUDENT BOARD MEMBERS
No information was presented.
